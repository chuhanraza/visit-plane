## Overview

The United Kingdom is a top study-abroad destination for Pakistani students — world-ranked universities, one- and two-year master's degrees, and a post-study **Graduate Route** that allows graduates to stay and work. To study a course longer than six months, Pakistani students need the **UK Student visa** (the route formerly known as Tier 4).

This guide explains the 2026 process for Pakistani applicants — eligibility, the all-important CAS, the financial requirement, fees including the Immigration Health Surcharge, processing times, and work rights. On VisitPlane, we verify every route against official sources.

> **Key takeaway:** Pakistani students need a **UK Student visa**. You must hold a **CAS** from a licensed sponsor, prove **funds for fees + living costs**, and pay the **£558 application fee (from 8 April 2026)** plus the **Immigration Health Surcharge of £776 per year**.

## Do Pakistani Students Need a UK Student Visa?

Yes. To study a degree or any course longer than six months at a UK institution, Pakistani nationals must obtain a Student visa before travelling. Short courses (up to six months) may use a Visitor visa instead, but degree study requires the Student route.

## Eligibility: What You Need First

- An **unconditional offer** from a UK education provider that is a **licensed student sponsor**, confirmed by a **CAS** (Confirmation of Acceptance for Studies).
- **English language ability** at the required level (often IELTS/UKVI-approved tests).
- **Enough money** to cover tuition and living costs (the financial requirement).
- Genuine intention to study (the "genuine student" assessment).

## The Financial Requirement

You must show you can pay your **course fees** plus **living costs**:

- **Living costs:** roughly **£1,483 per month** (up to 9 months) if studying in London, or **£1,136 per month** outside London.
- Plus your **first year's tuition fee** (or the full fee for shorter courses).
- Funds must usually be held for **28 consecutive days** before you apply, with the statement dated within 31 days of the application.

## Documents You Need

- A valid **passport**.
- Your **CAS** reference from the university.
- **Proof of funds** meeting the requirement (bank statements, loan letters, or official sponsorship).
- **Academic documents** (degrees, transcripts) listed on your CAS.
- **English test results** if required.
- **TB test certificate** (mandatory for Pakistani applicants).
- **Parental consent** and birth certificate if under 18.

## How to Apply: Step by Step

1. **Accept your offer** and obtain your **CAS** from the university.
2. **Prepare funds** and hold them for the required period.
3. **Complete the online application** on the UK government website.
4. **Pay the visa fee and the IHS.**
5. **Book and attend a biometrics appointment** at the visa centre in Pakistan.
6. **Submit documents** and complete the TB test.
7. **Wait for the decision**, usually within a few weeks.

## Costs and Fees (2026)

- **Student visa application fee:** **£558** (from 8 April 2026).
- **Immigration Health Surcharge (IHS):** **£776 per year** of your course, paid upfront for the full period.
- **TB test, English test, and biometrics**: additional.

For a one-year master's, the visa fee plus IHS alone is roughly **£1,334**, before tuition and living costs.

## Processing Time

Student visa decisions are usually made within about **3 weeks** of your biometrics appointment, though it can be faster or slower with demand. A **priority service** is sometimes available for an extra fee. Apply **as early as your CAS allows** — typically up to **6 months before** your course start date.

## Work Rights and the Graduate Route

- **During study:** you can usually work up to **20 hours per week** in term time (full-time in holidays), subject to your conditions.
- **After study:** the **Graduate Route** lets eligible graduates stay to work for **2 years** (3 for PhDs), a major draw for Pakistani students.

## Why Applications Get Refused (and How to Avoid It)

- **Financial requirement not met** — wrong amount, wrong dates, or funds not held long enough.
- **CAS issues** — mismatched documents or an expired CAS.
- **Missing TB certificate.**
- **Doubts about being a genuine student** — be ready to explain your course and plans.

The fees are non-refundable, so get the financial evidence exactly right.

## How Much Will Studying Cost Overall?

Rough 2026 estimates for a Pakistani student (one-year master's):

- **Visa + IHS:** ~£1,334.
- **Tuition:** typically **£14,000–£30,000** depending on the course and university.
- **Living costs:** **£12,000–£16,000+** per year (higher in London).
- **Flights and setup:** budget separately.

Many students fund this through family support, scholarships, or education loans — document the source clearly in your application.

## How to Strengthen Your UK Student Application

Whether your route is straightforward or strict, the same principles separate smooth approvals from frustrating delays. At VisitPlane, we've mapped these patterns across dozens of visa routes, and the advice below applies directly to Pakistani students heading to the UK.

The heart of a student application is the **financial requirement**, and it is where most refusals happen. Get the exact figure right (tuition shortfall plus the correct monthly living amount for your location and course length), hold the funds for the full **28-day** period, and make sure the closing balance never dips below the required total during that window. If parents or a sponsor are funding you, include the right relationship and consent documents rather than leaving an unexplained transfer.

Next, ensure **consistency between your CAS and your documents**. The qualifications your CAS lists must match the certificates you submit, and your personal details must be identical across every paper. Mismatches are a common, avoidable cause of delay and refusal.

Be ready for the **genuine student** assessment: understand your course, why you chose it and the university, and how it fits your career plans. A clear, honest explanation goes a long way. Finally, complete every step — the **TB test** is mandatory for Pakistani applicants, and missing it stalls the whole application.

## Key Dates and Deadlines

Timing is everything with a student visa, because the academic calendar is fixed and the visa must be ready before term starts. Work backwards from your **course start date**:

- **6–9 months before:** apply to universities and accept an offer.
- **About 3 months before:** receive your **CAS** (you can apply for the visa up to **6 months** before the course starts).
- **At least 28 days before applying:** ensure your funds are in place and held continuously.
- **As soon as the CAS is issued:** book biometrics, complete the **TB test**, and submit — don't wait.
- **Around 3 weeks before term:** typical decision window; use **priority service** if you're cutting it close.

You can enter the UK shortly before your course begins (your visa start date is set accordingly). Missing the funds-maturation window is the most common timing mistake, so plan your bank balance well ahead rather than at the last minute.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Funds held for too short a period** or below the required amount.
- **Skipping the TB test.**
- **CAS and certificate mismatches.**
- **Applying before funds have matured** the required days.
- **Booking flights** before the visa is granted.

## Funding and Scholarships

Most Pakistani students fund UK study through a combination of family support, education loans, and scholarships. Worth exploring: the **Chevening Scholarships** (fully funded master's), **Commonwealth Scholarships**, and individual **university scholarships** that many institutions offer to strong international applicants. Whatever the source, the visa application needs the money to be **documented and traceable** — a scholarship award letter, a bank loan sanction, or seasoned bank balances all work, but unexplained lump sums do not.

## Bringing Dependants

Rules on dependants have tightened: most taught master's students can **no longer bring family members** as dependants, though students on longer research programmes (such as PhDs) may still qualify. If you're considering bringing a spouse or children, confirm your specific course's eligibility before you plan, as the policy is strict and changes periodically.

## After You Arrive

Once in the UK, collect your **BRP or confirm your eVisa** status, register with the university, and open a UK bank account. Keep within your **term-time work limit** (usually 20 hours/week) — breaching it is treated seriously and can end your visa. Plan ahead for the **Graduate Route** if you intend to stay and work after graduating; applying before your Student visa expires is essential.
