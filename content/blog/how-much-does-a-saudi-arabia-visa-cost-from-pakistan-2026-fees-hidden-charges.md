## Which Saudi Visa Do You Actually Need?

Cost depends entirely on the visa category. The main routes for Pakistani travellers:

| Visa type | Purpose | Cost note |
|---|---|---|
| Tourist e-visa | Holidays, sightseeing, family visits, events | ~SAR 535 incl. insurance (where eligible) |
| Umrah visa | Umrah pilgrimage | Often free or low official fee; package/agent costs vary |
| Hajj visa | Hajj pilgrimage | Quota-based, arranged through approved operators |
| Business visa | Meetings, conferences | Requires Saudi invitation; fee + service charges |
| Work visa | Employment | Sponsored by employer; separate process |

This guide focuses on the **tourist e-visa**, the most common paid route. Umrah and Hajj are handled through approved operators and priced as packages, not a simple visa fee. Confirm your category first — paying for the wrong one wastes money.

## The Headline Visa Fee (2026)

The core Saudi tourist e-visa fee is around **SAR 535**, and crucially this figure already **includes the compulsory medical insurance** Saudi Arabia requires of tourist-visa holders. On top of the government fee you may pay a platform or service charge depending on how you apply. So treat SAR 535 as the *base*, not the final total.

## The Hidden Charges Most People Miss

The fee that surprises applicants isn't the visa charge — it's everything around it:

- **Service-centre fees** are charged *per application*, so a family pays several times over.
- **Premium or priority services** are optional but heavily marketed.
- **Photos, printing, and bank-statement charges** are easy to forget.
- **Document translation** if any of your paperwork isn't accepted in English.
- **Agent fees**, if you use one, can exceed the visa cost itself.

None of these are scams — but knowing about them lets you decide what you actually need and skip the rest.

## Step-by-Step: Applying for the Tourist e-Visa

For most eligible Pakistani travellers the tourist e-visa is applied for entirely online:

1. **Check eligibility** for the e-visa on the official Visit Saudi portal (visa.visitsaudi.com). Eligibility is set by Saudi authorities and changes — confirm before paying.
2. **Create an account** and start a new tourist-visa application.
3. **Enter your details** exactly as they appear in your passport.
4. **Upload your photo** to the required spec and a passport scan.
5. **Pay the fee** (around SAR 535, insurance included) with an international card.
6. **Receive the e-visa** by email, typically within a few days for complete applications.

If you're not eligible for the e-visa, you'll apply through a Saudi consulate or an approved visa centre instead — which adds a service charge.

## Processing Time

- **Tourist e-visa:** often issued within a few days for complete, eligible applications; some are approved much faster.
- **Consulate/agent routes:** longer, depending on the centre and season.
- **Peak periods** (Umrah season, Ramadan, major events): allow extra time.

Apply at least **two to three weeks before travel** so a document re-request doesn't derail your plans.

## Converting to PKR

Saudi fees are set in SAR, so what you pay in **PKR** depends on the exchange rate on the day and any card or conversion fee your bank charges. Rates move, so budget with a small buffer above the headline conversion and check whether paying by card adds a foreign-transaction fee. Never rely on a fixed PKR figure you saw months ago — confirm the current rate close to when you pay.

## Tourist e-Visa vs Umrah Visa — Don't Pay Twice

A frequent and expensive confusion for Pakistani travellers is mixing up the **tourist e-visa** and the **Umrah visa**. They are different products with different costs and different permissions:

- The **tourist e-visa** lets you visit for leisure, family, and events — and, under current rules, holders can also perform Umrah outside peak Hajj periods (confirm the live policy). It carries the ~SAR 535 fee.
- A dedicated **Umrah visa** is arranged through approved operators, often as part of a package that bundles flights, transport, and accommodation. Its "cost" is really the package price, not a simple visa fee.

If your trip is primarily a pilgrimage, an Umrah package may be better value and more convenient than buying flights and a tourist visa separately. If it's a general visit, the tourist e-visa is usually the cheaper, more flexible route. Confirm which fits your purpose **before** you pay — buying the wrong one is the single most costly mistake here.

## What the Fee Does and Doesn't Cover

The ~SAR 535 e-visa fee covers the **visa issuance and the mandatory basic insurance** — nothing more. It does **not** cover your flights, accommodation, in-country transport, or any optional premium service. Treat the visa as one line in a larger trip budget, and map the full picture (flights, stay, transport, food, connectivity, and a contingency buffer) so the headline fee never misleads you about the real cost of the trip.

## Do You Get a Refund If You're Refused?

The visa fee is generally a **processing fee, not a deposit** — it pays for the assessment, so it is **not refunded if you're refused**. Service charges are likewise non-refundable once the work is done. That's why getting the application right the first time matters financially: a refusal means paying the whole set of fees again to reapply.

## Budgeting for a Family or Group

Most fees are charged **per person** — the visa fee, any service charge, photos, and insurance all multiply with each applicant. A trip that looks affordable for one can be a substantial outlay for several, so build your budget per head and then total it. Children may attract reduced fees on some routes — check the official portal.

## How to Avoid Overpaying

- **Apply yourself** through the official Visit Saudi portal where you're eligible, instead of a markup-charging agent.
- **Skip optional premium services** you don't need.
- **Prepare your own documents and photos correctly** the first time to avoid redos.
- **Pay in a way that minimises conversion fees.**
- **Confirm your visa category first** so you don't pay for the wrong route.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Budgeting only for the headline fee and forgetting service, photo, and card costs.
- Buying premium services you don't actually need.
- Applying for the wrong visa type (tourist vs Umrah vs business).
- Ignoring exchange-rate and card fees when paying in PKR.
- Redoing photos or documents because they didn't meet the spec.
