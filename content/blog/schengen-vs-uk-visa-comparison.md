## Schengen vs UK Visa — A Direct Comparison for South Asian Applicants

For Pakistani and Indian travelers, two visa categories dominate European travel planning: the Schengen visa and the UK Standard Visitor Visa. Both are demanding, both require substantial documentation, and both carry meaningful rejection risks for South Asian passport holders. But they are very different applications with different rules, different costs, and different approval dynamics.

This guide breaks down every dimension so you can make a strategic decision — and build the strongest possible application.

---

## What Each Visa Covers

**Schengen Visa (Type C)**
The Schengen visa grants access to 27 European countries under a single application. You apply through the embassy (or its VFS centre) of the country where you'll spend the most nights. Once approved, you can travel freely across the entire Schengen Zone for up to 90 days in any 180-day period.

**UK Standard Visitor Visa**
The UK is not in the Schengen Zone. A UK visa is a separate application processed by UK Visas & Immigration (UKVI). A Standard Visitor Visa is typically issued for 6 months (sometimes 2 or 10 years multi-entry) and allows stays of up to 180 days per visit.

---

## Eligibility Differences

| Factor | Schengen | UK |
|---|---|---|
| Application route | VFS Global / Embassy | UKVI online portal |
| Biometrics required | Yes | Yes |
| Interview | Rare (some embassies) | No standard interview |
| Passport validity required | 3 months beyond return | 6 months beyond return (recommended) |
| Prior travel history weight | Moderate | High |
| Previous refusal disclosure | Mandatory | Mandatory |

For Pakistani applicants in particular, the UK places heavy emphasis on **prior international travel history**. An applicant who has already visited Schengen countries or the USA/Canada is viewed more favourably by UKVI. The Schengen system is more document-driven and less influenced by travel history — though it still matters.

## Document Requirements Comparison

### Schengen — Core Documents
- Valid passport (3+ months beyond return date)
- Completed Schengen application form
- 2 passport photographs (35x45mm, white background)
- Travel insurance (minimum €30,000 coverage, valid for all Schengen states)
- Flight itinerary (confirmed or dummy booking)
- Hotel bookings for full duration
- 3–6 months bank statements (stamped and signed)
- Employment letter / salary slips (6 months)
- Income tax returns (last 2 years)
- Cover letter / travel purpose statement
- VFS appointment confirmation

### UK — Core Documents
- Valid passport
- Online application form (VAF1A equivalent)
- 6 months bank statements
- Employer letter (or business registration documents)
- Evidence of ties to home country (property, family, employment)
- Accommodation proof (invitation letter from host or hotel)
- Detailed travel itinerary
- Evidence of sufficient funds
- TB test certificate (required for Pakistani nationals from specific regions)

**Key difference:** The UK application is largely paperless — you upload documents digitally through the UKVI portal. Schengen requires physical documents submitted in person at VFS.

## Financial Requirements

| Destination | Minimum Funds Guideline | Presentation |
|---|---|---|
| Schengen | €45–€100 per day | 3–6 months statements |
| UK | £85/day (approx.) | 6 months statements |

The UK's financial assessment is more holistic — immigration officers look at your overall financial profile, consistency of income, and genuine ability to fund the trip without working. A lump sum deposit a week before application is a red flag for both, but especially for UK applications.

---

## Processing Times

| Visa | Standard Processing | Peak Season |
|---|---|---|
| Schengen | 15 calendar days | Up to 45 days (apply 6 weeks early) |
| UK | 15 working days | 3–5 weeks (priority: 5 working days) |

The UK offers a **priority service** (£250 extra) for a 5-working-day turnaround, and a Super Priority option (£1,000) for next-day processing. Schengen has no equivalent paid fast-track; your only option is to apply as early as possible (up to 6 months before travel).

## Costs Comparison

| Item | Schengen | UK |
|---|---|---|
| Visa fee | €90 (~PKR 28,000) | £115 (~PKR 40,000) |
| VFS service fee | ~PKR 8,000–12,000 | ~PKR 8,000–12,000 |
| Travel insurance (mandatory) | ~PKR 5,000–15,000 | Not mandatory (but recommended) |
| Priority service | Not available | £250 |
| Total estimated cost | ~PKR 40,000–50,000 | ~PKR 55,000–70,000 |

**The UK is significantly more expensive.** The base visa fee alone is 27% higher, and mandatory travel insurance for Schengen adds to that total.

## Approval Rates for Pakistani Applicants

Official statistics are compiled by the EU and UKVI annually.

- **Schengen:** Pakistan's refusal rate has historically ranged from 25–40% across Schengen embassies. Germany and Netherlands tend to be stricter; Spain and Portugal have been somewhat more approachable.
- **UK:** UKVI does not publish country-specific approval rates, but immigration consultants estimate Pakistani applicant refusal rates at 20–35%, with strong employment and financial profiles being decisive.

**Practical implication:** Neither visa is "easy" for Pakistani applicants. The UK may have a marginally lower refusal rate for applicants with strong financial and employment profiles, but the documentation burden is comparable.

---

## Biometrics and In-Person Requirements

Both visas require biometric enrollment (fingerprints and photograph).

- **Schengen:** You must attend a VFS appointment in person. Biometrics are valid for 59 months if enrolled for any Schengen application; you may not need to re-enroll within that window.
- **UK:** Biometrics are given at a UKVI application centre. Like Schengen, once enrolled, they remain on file for future applications.

---

## Which Should You Apply For First?

If you have no prior international travel history, **Schengen first** is generally better strategy. A Schengen stamp in your passport significantly strengthens a subsequent UK application. UK immigration officers view European visa history as evidence of low overstay risk.

If you have prior Schengen stamps and are building towards a UK visa, your application is considerably stronger.

For those aiming at **both**, apply for Schengen first, travel, return on time, then apply for the UK.

---

## Key Tips

1. **Never apply to both simultaneously** unless your travel plans genuinely require it — an active Schengen application when applying for a UK visa can complicate your financial picture.
2. **Disclose all prior refusals** — both systems share data and concealment triggers automatic refusal.
3. **Bank statements should show consistency**, not just a balance. Embassies look for salary credits, regular expenses, and an authentic financial picture.
4. **Your cover letter matters** — especially for Schengen. [Learn how to write a strong visa cover letter](/blog/how-to-write-visa-cover-letter).
5. **Apply for the country you'll spend the most time in** (Schengen) or where your main host/host organisation is (UK).

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is the Schengen visa harder to get than the UK visa for Pakistanis?
They are comparably difficult. Schengen refusal rates for Pakistanis are slightly higher on average (25–40% vs an estimated 20–35% for UK), but this varies significantly by individual profile. Strong financial documentation and employment ties are decisive for both.

### Can I use the same bank statements for both applications?
Yes, but check the date requirements. Schengen typically wants the last 3–6 months. The UK requests the last 6 months. If applying sequentially, ensure your statements cover the required period for each.

### Do I need travel insurance for a UK visa?
Travel insurance is not a legal requirement for the UK Standard Visitor Visa, unlike Schengen where €30,000 coverage is mandatory. However, carrying insurance is strongly recommended for any international trip.

### Will a UK visa rejection affect my Schengen application?
A UK refusal must be disclosed on any subsequent Schengen application where the form asks about prior refusals. It does not automatically disqualify you, but it requires explanation and your new application must be demonstrably stronger.

### How early should I apply for each visa?
Apply for Schengen up to 6 months before travel (minimum 15–21 days, but 6 weeks is recommended during peak season). UK applications can be submitted up to 3 months before travel. In both cases, earlier is safer.
