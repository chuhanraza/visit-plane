## Overview

Azerbaijan has surged in popularity with Indian travellers — Baku's striking modern skyline beside a UNESCO old town, the Caspian seafront, and easy day trips to the mountains and mud volcanoes. It's affordable, visually spectacular, and just a short flight away. Entry is simple: Indian passport holders apply for the **ASAN e-visa** online and receive it by email, with **no embassy visit** needed.

This guide explains the 2026 process for Indian citizens — the fee, documents, processing speed (with a same-day urgent option), validity, and approval tips. On VisitPlane, we verify every route against official sources.

> **Key takeaway:** Indians need an Azerbaijan **ASAN e-visa** (no visa-on-arrival). Apply at the **official portal (evisa.gov.az)**; standard processing is **3 working days**, with an **urgent** option in hours for an extra fee.

## Do Indian Citizens Need a Visa for Azerbaijan?

Yes. Azerbaijan does **not** grant visa-on-arrival to Indian passport holders. The standard route is the **ASAN e-visa** — a single-entry electronic visa for short tourist stays.

## How the ASAN e-Visa Works

- Applied for **online** at evisa.gov.az.
- **Single entry**, usually a **30-day stay** within a 90-day validity.
- **Standard** processing **3 working days**; **urgent** options in **3–24 hours** for higher fees.
- Delivered as a PDF by email — print and carry it.

> **Key takeaway:** Apply only via the **official evisa.gov.az** portal; third-party sites add large mark-ups for the same e-visa.

## Documents You Need

The ASAN e-visa needs very little:

- A **passport valid for at least 6 months** from arrival.
- A **scan of the passport bio-data page**.
- A valid **email** and a card for the fee.
- Your **travel dates**.

Keep a return ticket and hotel booking handy for immigration on arrival.

## How to Apply: Step by Step

1. **Visit evisa.gov.az** and start an application.
2. **Select standard or urgent** processing.
3. **Enter details** and upload your passport page.
4. **Pay online**.
5. **Receive the e-visa by email** and print it.
6. **Carry it** with your passport for entry.

## Costs and Fees (2026)

- **Standard e-visa:** around **₹2,450** all-in (government fee + service).
- **Urgent processing:** higher — roughly **₹5,000+** depending on speed (12–24h or 4–6h options).

Confirm the current amount on the official portal before paying.

## Processing Time

**Standard:** about **3–5 working days**. **Urgent:** as fast as **3 hours**. Apply a few days ahead for standard processing.

## Visa Validity and Stay

The ASAN e-visa is typically **single-entry**, allowing a **30-day stay** within a **90-day validity**. You cannot work on it. Track your days and don't overstay.

## Why Applications Get Refused (and How to Avoid It)

The approval rate is high; the few issues come from:

- **Typos** in name or passport number.
- **Passport validity** under 6 months.
- **Low-quality** passport scans.

The fee is non-refundable, so review every field before submitting.

## Best Time to Visit and Practical Tips

Baku and Azerbaijan shine in **April–June** and **September–October** — mild and ideal for sightseeing and the mountains. Practical notes for Indian travellers:

- **Currency:** the Azerbaijani Manat (AZN); cards work in Baku, cash is handy outside the capital.
- **Connectivity:** tourist SIMs are cheap; Bolt and Uber operate in Baku.
- **Getting around:** Baku's old city is walkable; popular day trips include Gobustan, Gabala, Sheki, and the Absheron Peninsula.
- **Food:** plenty of halal and vegetarian-friendly options; Azerbaijani cuisine is hearty and affordable.
- **Etiquette:** dress respectfully at religious sites; locals are warm and hospitable to Indian tourists.

## What You Can and Can't Do on This Visa

The ASAN e-visa is a **single-entry tourist visa**, typically a 30-day stay within a 90-day validity. You can travel the country and take day trips, but you **cannot** work, and you cannot re-enter on the same visa once you leave. Track your days and avoid overstaying, which carries fines.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Typos** in name or passport number on the non-refundable form.
- **Passport validity** under 6 months.
- **Expecting visa-on-arrival** — Indians must apply online in advance.
- **Poor-quality passport scan.**
- **Booking non-refundable flights** before the e-visa is approved.

## What to See in Azerbaijan

Most trips start in **Baku**, where the futuristic Flame Towers and Heydar Aliyev Center sit beside the UNESCO-listed walled Old City (Icherisheher) with its Maiden Tower and Palace of the Shirvanshahs. Stroll the Caspian-front Boulevard, ride up to the Highland Park for sweeping views, and explore the museums and cafe culture downtown.

Beyond the capital, easy day trips open up a different side of the country. **Gobustan** offers ancient rock petroglyphs and bubbling mud volcanoes within a couple of hours of Baku. To the north and west, **Gabala**, **Sheki** (famous for its Khan's Palace and sweets), and the **Absheron Peninsula** (fire temples and the eternal flames of Yanar Dag) make memorable additions. For a India-friendly trip, halal food is widely available, the people are warm and hospitable, and distances are short enough that a 5–6 day itinerary comfortably covers Baku plus two or three excursions.

## How to Strengthen Your Azerbaijan Application

Whether your route is straightforward or strict, the same principles separate smooth approvals from frustrating delays — and they are worth understanding before you apply. At VisitPlane, we've mapped these patterns across dozens of visa routes, and the advice below applies directly to Indian travellers heading to Azerbaijan.

Start with your **finances**, because they are the single most scrutinised part of almost any application. Officers and systems are not simply checking that you have money today; they are checking that your funds are *genuine and stable*. A balance that has sat comfortably in your account for three to six months tells a far more convincing story than a large sum that appeared a week before you applied. If a relative is supporting your trip, document that relationship clearly rather than leaving an unexplained deposit. Keep your statements clean, recent, and consistent with the trip you are describing.

Next, think about **consistency across your documents**. Your flight dates, hotel bookings, cover letter, and bank statements should all tell one coherent story. If your itinerary says ten days but your hotel booking covers four, or your stated purpose is tourism while your documents suggest something else, that mismatch invites questions and slows everything down. Before you submit, lay your documents side by side and read them as an officer would — every date and detail should line up.

For routes that assess intent, **ties to your home country** matter enormously. Evidence of stable employment, a running business, property, or dependents reassures the decision-maker that you intend to return after your visit to Azerbaijan. This is less about any single document and more about the overall picture your application paints.

Finally, be **honest and complete**. Declaring a previous refusal — for any country — is almost always better than hiding it, because non-disclosure is treated far more seriously than an old rejection. Submit every requested document, double-check every field, and apply with enough time to absorb processing and any follow-up requests.
