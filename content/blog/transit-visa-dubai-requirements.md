## Dubai Transit — What You Need to Know Before Your Layover

Dubai International Airport (DXB) is one of the world's busiest transit hubs. Millions of travellers pass through every year, connecting between South Asia, Southeast Asia, Europe, the Americas, and Africa. For Pakistani and Indian citizens, the question is always the same: do I need a transit visa for Dubai?

The answer depends on three factors: your **nationality**, how long your **layover** is, and whether you plan to **leave the airport terminal**. Get those three right and you'll know in seconds whether you can simply walk to your gate, claim a free transit visa, or need to apply in advance.

> **Key takeaway:** If you stay airside (inside the international departure zone), most travellers — including Pakistanis — need **no visa** at all. To leave the airport, Pakistani citizens can often claim a **free 96-hour Emirates/Etihad transit visa**, while Indian citizens can use **visa on arrival**. Always confirm your exact case for your airline and route with the [VisitPlane Visa Wizard](/wizard) before you fly.

## Two Types of Transit

### Airside Transit (Staying in the International Zone)

If your layover is short and you stay within the international departure lounge — never passing through UAE immigration — you generally do **not** need a transit visa. You remain in the "airside" area and board your next flight without formally entering the UAE.

### Landside Transit (Exiting the Airport)

If you want to leave the airport terminal — visit Dubai city, stay in a hotel, or explore during a long layover — you must pass through UAE immigration. This requires either a UAE visa or a transit visa.

## Do Pakistani Citizens Need a Transit Visa for Dubai?

### Airside (no airport exit): Generally NO

Most nationalities, including Pakistan, can transit airside through Dubai International Airport without a visa, provided they stay in the international departure area and don't pass through immigration.

**Exception:** travel to or from certain destinations can trigger additional checks regardless of transit status — confirm your specific routing with your airline.

### Landside (exiting the airport): the free 96-hour transit visa

Pakistani citizens have a valuable benefit: **Emirates and Etihad offer a free 96-hour transit visa** for Pakistani passport holders travelling on qualifying flights with a layover in Dubai (DXB) or Abu Dhabi (AUH).

**Emirates Free Transit Visa:**
- Available to Pakistani citizens with a DXB layover of **8+ hours**
- Valid for **96 hours (4 days)**
- **Free** of charge when booked on Emirates
- Lets you exit the airport and explore Dubai
- Apply through your Emirates booking reference or at the check-in counter

**Etihad Transit Visa (Abu Dhabi):**
- Similar programme for AUH layovers
- Available to Pakistani passport holders
- 96 hours free transit
- Apply via Etihad when booking

This benefit is significant — a long Dubai layover can become a free mini-holiday, with no separate paid visa application needed. Exact eligibility (layover length, fare type) is set by the airline and can change, so confirm when you book.

## Do Indian Citizens Need a Transit Visa for Dubai?

- **Airside:** No visa needed to stay in the international zone.
- **Landside:** Indian citizens generally receive **visa on arrival** access to the UAE (subject to conditions, and especially when holding qualifying US/UK/EU visas or residence). The visa-on-arrival stamp is issued at the immigration counter, making Dubai transits particularly easy for Indian passport holders.

## How to Apply for a UAE Transit Visa

If you're not eligible for the free Emirates/Etihad transit, or need longer than 96 hours, you can apply for a paid UAE transit visa.

### Option 1: Through Emirates Airline
1. Log in to the Emirates website with your booking reference.
2. Go to **Manage My Booking**.
3. Select the Dubai stopover/transit visa option.
4. Complete the form and pay the fee.
5. Receive your e-visa by email.

### Option 2: Through a UAE Tourism Sponsor
UAE-registered hotels, tour operators, and travel agents can sponsor transit or tourist visas. Many Dubai hotels offer visa facilitation as part of a stopover package.

### Option 3: Through the UAE ICP Portal
Apply directly at **icp.gov.ae** for a short-stay transit visa. Processing usually takes 24–72 hours.

## Dubai Airport Guide for Transit Passengers

Dubai International Airport has three terminals:
- **Terminal 1:** flydubai and many international carriers
- **Terminal 2:** low-cost and charter airlines
- **Terminal 3:** Emirates (the largest terminal)

Terminals 1 and 3 are connected airside and landside. Terminal 2 is separate — if transiting between terminals, factor in extra time.

**Airside facilities at Terminal 3:**
- Extensive duty-free shopping
- Restaurants and cafés open 24/7
- Prayer rooms on every floor
- Sleep pods and premium lounges
- Shower facilities

For long layovers, Terminal 3's airside area is comfortable enough that many travellers skip the landside visa just to shop and rest.

## Should You Exit the Airport? A Quick Decision Guide

Whether a landside transit visa is worth it depends on your layover length and energy:

- **Under 4 hours:** stay airside. By the time you clear immigration both ways, there's little time left — Terminal 3's lounges and dining are a better use of it.
- **4–8 hours:** borderline. A quick taxi to Dubai Mall and the Burj Khalifa fountains is doable, but watch the clock and re-entry security queues.
- **8–24 hours:** the sweet spot for a city visit. The free 96-hour transit visa (if eligible) turns a dead layover into a real outing — see the creek, a souk, or the beach.
- **24+ hours:** treat it as a mini-break. Book a hotel, rest properly, and plan one or two highlights without rushing.

## What to Do on a Dubai Layover

If you do head into the city, even a few hours go a long way:

- **Downtown Dubai:** the Burj Khalifa, Dubai Mall, and the evening fountain show — all close together.
- **Old Dubai:** Deira and Bur Dubai souks, the Creek, and a cheap **abra** (water taxi) ride for a few dirhams.
- **The beach:** free public stretches along Jumeirah, with the Burj Al Arab as a backdrop.
- **A quick meal:** Dubai's food scene spans every budget and cuisine, much of it open late.

Keep your **onward boarding pass and passport** on you at all times, and leave a generous buffer to return, clear security, and reach your gate.

## Transit Hotel Tips

If you're exiting the airport for an overnight layover, Dubai has hotels at every price point within 20–30 minutes of DXB:

- **Budget (PKR 8,000–15,000/night):** Al Qusais, Deira
- **Mid-range (PKR 20,000–40,000/night):** Bur Dubai, near DIFC
- **Upmarket (PKR 50,000+/night):** Downtown Dubai, Dubai Marina

A taxi or ride-hail from DXB to Deira or Bur Dubai costs roughly AED 20–30; to Downtown Dubai, around AED 50–70. Prices vary — treat these as a guide.

## Special Case: Holders of US, UK, or EU Visas

If you hold a **valid US visa, US green card, or a UK/EU residence permit**, your transit options often widen — some travellers in this position qualify for a UAE visa on arrival even where they otherwise wouldn't. Eligibility (visa type and remaining validity) is set by UAE immigration and changes, so confirm your exact case with your airline or the [Visa Wizard](/wizard) rather than assuming. It's a genuinely useful shortcut for frequent travellers, but only when the conditions are met.

## Common Transit Mistakes to Avoid

- **Assuming you need a visa for a short airside connection** — you usually don't.
- **Missing the 8-hour minimum** for the free Emirates transit visa.
- **Forgetting your onward-destination visa**, which immigration may ask to see.
- **Cutting terminal transfers too fine**, especially to/from Terminal 2.
- **Overstaying 96 hours** — beyond that you need a separate visit visa.
