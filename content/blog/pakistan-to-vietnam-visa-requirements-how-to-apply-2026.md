## Overview

Travelling from Pakistan to Vietnam means securing the right visa before you go. This 2026 guide walks Pakistani travellers through who needs a visa, what the Vietnam **e-visa** requires, what it costs, how long it takes, and how to apply — so you arrive at the application already prepared.

> **Key takeaway:** Pakistani travellers need a Vietnam e-visa. Apply through the official channel, show genuine **funds** and strong **ties** to Pakistan, keep every detail consistent, and apply early — clean, well-documented applications are what win approvals.

## Who Needs a Visa

Vietnam offers Pakistani travellers a straightforward **e-visa** online, valid up to **90 days** (single or multiple entry).

Confirm your eligibility route first, because applying through the wrong channel is a common cause of delay and refusal.

## Fees

About **USD 25** single and **USD 50** multiple entry.

Fees change, so treat these as a guide and confirm the current amount on the official portal before you pay. Keep every payment receipt — they form part of a tidy application file.

## Documents You'll Need

You'll typically need a **passport** valid well beyond your trip with blank pages, a recent **photo** to specification, proof of **funds** for the trip, your **purpose** (itinerary, bookings, or invitation), evidence of **ties** to Pakistan, and **travel medical insurance** where required. Organise these into a clear set so a reviewer can find anything quickly.

## Step-by-Step: How to Apply

First, **confirm your route** and gather documents. Second, **complete the application** through the correct official channel. Third, **pay the fee** and keep the receipt. Fourth, **give biometrics or attend any appointment** where required, in Islamabad. Fifth, **track your application** and, once approved, check every detail before you travel. Don't book non-refundable travel until your visa is confirmed.

## Proof of Funds and Finances

You must show you can **afford the trip** without working illegally. Provide bank statements covering the **past three to six months** — not just a recent balance — and document the source of any large deposit. If a sponsor or relative supports your trip, include their invitation, status, and financial documents. Funds that appear suddenly or sit unexplained in a third party's account are routinely questioned.

## Ties to Pakistan: The Quiet Decider

For most visit routes, the officer's central question is whether you'll **return home**. Answer it before it's asked: show stable **employment** or a **business**, **family** responsibilities, **property**, and a clear reason your life continues in Pakistan after the trip. A short, honest cover letter tying these together — who you are, why you're travelling, when you'll return, and what brings you back — is one of the most valuable pages in the file.

## Processing Time

Usually issued within about three working days. Whatever the route, apply **well ahead** of your travel dates and avoid committing to non-refundable bookings until you have the visa in hand.

## Validity and Length of Stay

Know what the visa grants before you apply. The e-visa sets a specific permitted stay and validity, so confirm the dates and entry type and never overstay, as fines and bans can follow. The visa is **permission to travel, not a guarantee of entry** — a border officer makes the final call, so keep your supporting documents accessible when you travel.

## Tourism, Visiting Family, or Business

The visa may be the same, but the **supporting story** differs with your purpose. For **tourism**, show a realistic itinerary and accommodation. For **visiting family or friends**, include an **invitation letter** from your host stating your relationship, the purpose and length of your visit, and where you'll stay — with their status and, if relevant, their funds. For **business**, include letters from your Pakistan employer and the inviting organisation. Matching documents to your real purpose is what makes the application read as genuine.

## A Realistic Cost Snapshot

Budget beyond the visa fee: the **application fee**, any **service or biometrics charges**, a compliant **photo**, **insurance** where required, document courier or translation, and — once approved — your travel. Keep receipts for everything official. Remember the money you must *show* to prove you can afford the trip is separate from, and larger than, the fees you *pay* to apply.

## A Strong Travel History Helps

A clean record of previous travel is a quiet advantage. If you've held visas for the US, UK, Schengen, or other major destinations and returned home on time, include copies of those visas and stamps — they show you've complied before, which speaks directly to whether you'll return to Pakistan. First-time travellers should compensate with especially clear ties and a well-documented purpose.

## After You Apply: What to Expect

Once you've submitted and given any biometrics, the decision arrives through the official channel for your route. If you're approved, verify the **name, validity, and entry type** on the visa as soon as you receive it, and only then convert refundable bookings into paid ones. If you're refused, find the **specific reason**, fix that weakness (most often funds or ties), and reapply with a stronger file rather than resubmitting the same one. A refusal addressed properly often becomes an approval.

## Booking and Insurance

Keep your travel **refundable or flexible** until the visa is confirmed, then convert it once approved. Travel insurance is wise — and required on Schengen and some other routes — so factor it into your budget early. Keep digital and printed copies of your confirmations with your visa, since a border officer may ask to see onward travel and accommodation. Handling these logistics in advance lets you focus on the parts of the application that actually decide it: your funds and your ties to Pakistan.

## First-Time Traveller? Start Early

If this is your first international trip, give yourself extra runway. Build your funds history months ahead so your statements look settled rather than rushed, gather your ties evidence carefully, and read the official requirements twice so nothing surprises you. First-time applicants aren't disadvantaged, but they have no travel record to lean on, so a complete, consistent, well-presented file does the reassuring instead. Apply early and double-check every name and date against your passport.

## A Quick Note on Onward Travel

Carry proof of **onward or return travel** and your **accommodation** details, as immigration officers can ask for them on arrival. Keep a printed and digital copy together with your visa, along with enough accessible funds for your stay. These small habits rarely cause trouble when prepared in advance — and occasionally save a great deal of it at the border.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **A sudden large deposit** just before applying, with no source documentation.
- **Weak or vague ties** to Pakistan, so the officer doubts you'll return.
- **Applying through the wrong channel** or under the wrong visa type.
- **Booking non-refundable travel** before approval.
- **Inconsistencies** between your form, your funds, and your stated plans.
- **Applying too late** for the current processing time.

## Keep Your Documents Organised

A tidy file does quiet work in your favour. Arrange your passport, photo, funds evidence, itinerary or invitation, and ties documents in a clear order, and label anything that isn't obvious. An officer who can find each piece in seconds reads you as a genuine, prepared applicant — while a jumbled bundle invites doubt even when the underlying facts are fine. Keep both a printed set and digital copies, and bring the originals of anything important to any appointment.
