## The List

### 1. Thailand
Bangkok's temples and street food, Chiang Mai's mountains, and the southern islands — one of the region's most complete, best-value trips, and an easy first stop from Vietnam.

### 2. Malaysia
Kuala Lumpur's skyline, Penang's food scene, and Borneo's rainforests and islands — a modern, easy-to-navigate neighbour. [Check the Vietnam → Malaysia visa](/visa/Vietnam/Malaysia) for your passport before booking.

### 3. Philippines
Over 7,000 islands' worth of beaches, diving, and distinct regional cultures — a short flight from Ho Chi Minh City or Hanoi.

### 4. Cambodia
Angkor Wat alone justifies the trip, paired with very low day-to-day costs and an easy overland or short-flight connection from Vietnam.

### 5. Indonesia
Bali especially offers affordable stays, low-cost food, and stunning landscapes, and the wider archipelago rewards a longer visit.

### 6. Singapore
A compact, ultra-modern city-state that works well as a short break or a stopover on a longer Southeast Asia trip.

### 7. Turkey
Istanbul's bazaars, Cappadocia's hot-air balloons, and the Aegean coast — a longer-haul trip that punches well above its visa difficulty.

### 8. Morocco
Marrakech's medinas, the Atlas Mountains, and the Sahara's dunes — atmospheric and affordable, with a genuinely different feel from Southeast Asia.

### 9. Qatar
Doha's museums, souqs, and waterfront skyline, with the Gulf's usual mix of modern architecture and desert excursions nearby.

### 10. Georgia
Tbilisi's old town, dramatic Caucasus scenery, and an emerging wine region — one of the least-crowded, best-value longer-haul options here.

### 11. Azerbaijan
Baku's striking modern skyline paired with mountains and ancient sites, at modest prices and with short flight connections via the Gulf.

### 12. Jordan
Petra, Wadi Rum's desert, and the Dead Sea packed into a compact, welcoming country — a strong single-country history trip.

## How to Choose the Right One for You

Start with what you actually want from the trip. If it's a **quick, easy Southeast Asia break**, the six ASEAN neighbours need the least planning. If it's **history and culture on a longer trip**, Turkey, Morocco, and Jordan each deliver a genuinely different world. If it's a **short, striking Caucasus trip**, Georgia and Azerbaijan pair naturally together. Then weigh three practical filters: the **flight cost and time** from your city, the **visa route** for your Vietnamese passport, and the **season** you can travel.

## A Money-Saving Booking Checklist

Whichever destination you pick, these habits keep costs down: search flights in **incognito mode** and set **fare alerts**; be flexible with dates and fly **midweek**; travel in the **shoulder or low season**; book accommodation a little **outside the centre** with good transport links; and prefer places with a kitchen or included breakfast. On the ground, eat where locals eat, use public transport, and buy sensible multi-day passes.

## When Is the Best Time to Go?

Timing shapes both price and experience. The **ASEAN neighbours** are generally best avoiding the peak monsoon window in each destination — check the specific country, since Southeast Asia's wet seasons don't all line up. Turkey and Georgia suit the warmer months for sightseeing and hiking; Morocco is most comfortable in spring and autumn, avoiding summer's Sahara-edge heat; and Jordan is best in spring or autumn to avoid summer desert heat. Confirm the specific climate window for your chosen country before booking.

## Practical Tips for Vietnamese Travellers

Keep a **clean, multi-month bank history** so that any visa or e-visa application is straightforward, and hold copies of previous visas and stamps to show a good travel record. Build a **realistic daily budget** for each destination — Qatar and Singapore cost noticeably more day-to-day than Cambodia or the Philippines. Consider **shoulder-season travel** and **midweek flights**, and look at combining nearby countries — Georgia and Azerbaijan, or Thailand and Cambodia — to spread a long-haul airfare across more than one stop. Register any required arrival details or e-visa approval before you fly, so nothing holds you up at the airport.
