## Overview

Saudi Arabia has transformed from a country you mainly visited for Hajj and Umrah into a full tourism destination — AlUla's desert wonders, the Red Sea coast, Riyadh's skyline, and, of course, the holy cities of Makkah and Madinah. For Pakistani travellers, the key route is the **Saudi tourist eVisa**, which now also allows you to perform **Umrah** (Hajj excluded).

This guide explains exactly how Pakistani citizens apply in 2026 — where to apply, the documents and financial proof required, the fee, processing times, and how Umrah ties into the Nusuk platform. On VisitPlane, we verify every route against official sources.

> **Key takeaway:** Pakistani citizens apply for the Saudi **tourist eVisa** (currently single-entry, 30-day) through **Tasheer** visa centres. It permits tourism **and Umrah** (not Hajj), with Umrah appointments booked via the official **Nusuk** platform.

## Do Pakistani Citizens Need a Visa for Saudi Arabia?

Yes. Pakistani passport holders need a visa for every type of visit — tourism, Umrah, business, or family. The most common route is the **tourist eVisa**. Since February 2025, Pakistani applicants are generally issued **single-entry, 30-day** tourist visas (rather than the one-year multiple-entry some other nationalities receive).

A useful shortcut: if you already hold a valid **US, UK, or Schengen visa** (used at least once) or residence in those regions, you may qualify for a faster, simpler eVisa.

## Where Pakistani Applicants Apply

Pakistani tourist visa applications are handled through **Tasheer** visa facilitation centres, located in **Islamabad, Lahore, Karachi, Peshawar, Quetta, and Multan**. Most applicants complete **biometric enrolment** at a Tasheer centre as part of the process.

> **Key takeaway:** Don't rely on unofficial "instant Saudi visa" websites. Use Tasheer (the authorised facilitation centre for Pakistan) or the official Saudi eVisa channel.

## Documents You Need

For the Saudi tourist eVisa, Pakistani applicants should prepare:

- A **passport valid for at least 6 months** with blank pages.
- A recent **passport-size photograph** to specification.
- **Bank statements** showing minimum monthly credits of around **USD 750** (~PKR 210,000), consistent over **3–6 months**.
- **Confirmed return flight** booking.
- **Accommodation** details for your stay.
- **Biometric enrolment** at a Tasheer centre.
- Health insurance is typically bundled with the visa fee.

> **Key takeaway:** The **financial requirement is strict** for Pakistani applicants — show genuine, stable income/savings of roughly USD 750/month over several months. A sudden deposit will not satisfy it.

## Performing Umrah on a Tourist Visa

A major benefit: the Saudi **tourist visa now allows Umrah** (Hajj is separate and requires a dedicated Hajj permit). To visit the holy sites in Makkah and Madinah, you must book your **Umrah and Ziyarah appointments through the official Nusuk platform** before travelling. Plan this step in advance, as slots are managed digitally.

## How to Apply: Step by Step

1. **Start your application** through a Tasheer centre (online booking, then in-person biometrics) or the official Saudi eVisa portal if eligible.
2. **Complete the form** and upload your passport, photo, and financial documents.
3. **Attend biometric enrolment** at your chosen Tasheer centre.
4. **Pay the visa fee** (see below).
5. **Wait for processing** (typically 7–14 working days).
6. **Receive your eVisa** by email — print it and carry it.
7. **Book Umrah/Ziyarah via Nusuk** if you intend to visit the holy sites.

## Costs and Fees (2026)

- **Tourist eVisa fee:** from around **SAR 300** (~PKR 23,000), which usually includes mandatory insurance.
- **Tasheer service charges** apply in addition.
- Budget separately for flights, accommodation, and Umrah logistics.

Fees can change and vary with the riyal exchange rate, so confirm the current amount when you apply.

## Processing Time

Saudi tourist eVisa processing for Pakistani applicants typically takes **7–14 working days** after biometrics. Apply **at least 3–4 weeks** before travel, and earlier around peak Umrah seasons (Ramadan and school holidays) when demand surges.

## Visa Validity and Stay

Pakistani applicants currently receive a **single-entry visa valid for a 30-day stay**. You cannot work on a tourist visa. Overstaying carries heavy fines and possible bans, so track your days carefully and depart on time.

## Why Applications Get Refused (and How to Avoid It)

For Pakistani applicants, the most common issues are:

- **Insufficient financial proof** — balances below the ~USD 750/month guideline, or sudden deposits.
- **Incomplete biometrics or documents** at the Tasheer stage.
- **Inconsistent travel plans** — flights and accommodation that don't match.
- **Passport validity** under 6 months.

Get the financial evidence and biometrics right and the process is usually smooth.

## How Much Will the Whole Trip Cost?

Rough 2026 estimates per person for a Pakistani traveller (tourism or Umrah):

- **Visa:** from ~SAR 300 (~PKR 23,000) including insurance, plus Tasheer charges.
- **Return flights (Pakistan–Jeddah/Riyadh):** roughly PKR 90,000–180,000 depending on season; Umrah season is pricier.
- **Accommodation:** PKR 8,000–25,000 per night, higher near the Haramain in Makkah/Madinah.
- **Daily spend (food, local transport, Ziyarah):** PKR 8,000–15,000 per person.
- **Umrah logistics:** transport between cities, guide services, and Nusuk-booked appointments.

A typical 10-day Umrah-plus-tourism trip can land around **PKR 350,000–700,000 per person**, driven mostly by flights and accommodation near the holy sites.

Apply 3–4 weeks ahead (more in Umrah season), since processing runs 7–14 working days and biometrics must be completed in person. Lock your passport validity and bookings first, then complete the visa and Nusuk steps.

- Passport valid 6+ months
- Bank statements (~USD 750/month over 3–6 months)
- Biometric enrolment booked at a Tasheer centre
- Return flight and accommodation confirmed
- Umrah/Ziyarah appointments booked via Nusuk (if visiting holy sites)
- Printed eVisa carried with passport

Tick every box and you remove the main causes of delay. Saudi Arabia is more open to Pakistani travellers than ever — preparation is what makes the trip smooth.

## Best Time to Visit and Practical Tips

The most comfortable months in Saudi Arabia are **November to March**, when the heat eases for AlUla, Riyadh, and the Red Sea coast; Makkah and Madinah are visited year-round but are busiest in Ramadan. Practical notes for Pakistani travellers:

- **Currency:** the Saudi Riyal (SAR); cards are widely accepted, and ATMs are everywhere.
- **Dress and conduct:** dress modestly in public; this is essential near the holy sites. Alcohol is strictly prohibited nationwide, and penalties for drugs and prohibited items are severe.
- **Connectivity:** local SIMs (STC, Mobily, Zain) are easy to buy; ride-hailing (Careem, Uber) works well in cities.
- **Umrah logistics:** book Nusuk appointments early, especially in Ramadan; intercity high-speed rail (Haramain) links Makkah, Jeddah, and Madinah.
- **Prayer times:** many shops pause briefly at prayer times — plan around them.

## What You Can and Can't Do on This Visa

The tourist eVisa is for **tourism, family visits, and Umrah** — not Hajj (which needs a dedicated permit) and not paid work. You can travel freely between cities, visit the holy sites with a Nusuk booking, and enjoy tourism attractions. You **cannot** take up employment or overstay. Overstaying carries heavy fines and possible bans, so depart within your 30-day window or arrange any permitted extension before it lapses.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Weak financial proof** — not meeting the ~USD 750/month guideline over 3–6 months.
- **Skipping Nusuk** — arriving to perform Umrah without booked appointments.
- **Passport validity** under 6 months at travel.
- **Assuming Hajj is included** — it is not; Hajj needs a separate permit.
- **Using unofficial visa sites** instead of Tasheer or the official portal.

## How to Strengthen Your Saudi Arabia Application

Whether your route is straightforward or strict, the same principles separate smooth approvals from frustrating delays — and they are worth understanding before you apply. At VisitPlane, we've mapped these patterns across dozens of visa routes, and the advice below applies directly to Pakistani travellers heading to Saudi Arabia.

Start with your **finances**, because they are the single most scrutinised part of almost any application. Officers and systems are not simply checking that you have money today; they are checking that your funds are *genuine and stable*. A balance that has sat comfortably in your account for three to six months tells a far more convincing story than a large sum that appeared a week before you applied. If a relative is supporting your trip, document that relationship clearly rather than leaving an unexplained deposit. Keep your statements clean, recent, and consistent with the trip you are describing.

Next, think about **consistency across your documents**. Your flight dates, hotel bookings, cover letter, and bank statements should all tell one coherent story. If your itinerary says ten days but your hotel booking covers four, or your stated purpose is tourism while your documents suggest something else, that mismatch invites questions and slows everything down. Before you submit, lay your documents side by side and read them as an officer would — every date and detail should line up.

For routes that assess intent, **ties to your home country** matter enormously. Evidence of stable employment, a running business, property, or dependents reassures the decision-maker that you intend to return after your visit to Saudi Arabia. This is less about any single document and more about the overall picture your application paints.

Finally, be **honest and complete**. Declaring a previous refusal — for any country — is almost always better than hiding it, because non-disclosure is treated far more seriously than an old rejection. Submit every requested document, double-check every field, and apply with enough time to absorb processing and any follow-up requests.
