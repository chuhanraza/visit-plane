## Overview

Canada and Australia are the two destinations Indian students most often weigh against each other — both are English-speaking, both have strong universities, generous post-study work, and clear immigration pathways. The differences are in the detail: cost, climate, the size of the upfront financial requirement, and how each country's settlement route works.

This guide compares Canada and Australia head-to-head for Indian students in 2026 so you can match the choice to your goals and budget. On VisitPlane, we verify every route against official sources.

> **Key takeaway:** Both offer excellent education, work rights, and PR pathways. Choose **Canada** for lower tuition and a long-established Express Entry route; choose **Australia** for strong graduate-job prospects, a warmer lifestyle, and a points-based skilled-migration system. The upfront visa cost is notably higher for Australia.

## Tuition and Course Length

**Canada** generally offers **lower tuition** than Australia, with two-year master's and four-year bachelor's programmes common — a longer stay that builds Canadian experience.

**Australia** tuition is typically **higher**, with master's degrees usually 1.5–2 years. The trade-off is strong employability in many fields and a lifestyle many Indian students rate highly.

If keeping total tuition down matters most, Canada usually wins; if graduate outcomes and lifestyle weigh heavily, Australia competes strongly despite the higher price.

## Visa Cost and Requirements

The **Canada study permit** fee is modest — **CAD 150 plus CAD 85 biometrics** — but you must evidence **proof of funds** (living-cost benchmark around **CAD 20,635/year** plus tuition, often via a GIC) and obtain a **Provincial Attestation Letter (PAL)**.

The **Australia Student visa (subclass 500)** carries a much higher **application charge of around AUD 2,000**, plus **OSHC health cover** and evidence of living costs (benchmark around **AUD 29,710/year**). You must also satisfy the **Genuine Student** requirement.

So Australia's upfront visa cost is significantly higher; Canada's fee is low but the proof-of-funds bar is firm.

## Work Rights While Studying

- **Canada:** recently up to around **24 hours per week** off-campus during term, full-time in breaks.
- **Australia:** up to **48 hours per fortnight** during term, unlimited in scheduled breaks.

These work out broadly similar in practice, giving students in both countries meaningful part-time earning potential.

## Post-Study Work and PR Pathway

- **Canada's PGWP** can run up to **3 years**, feeding into **Express Entry** and **Provincial Nominee Programs** — a clear, published route many Indian graduates use to settle.
- **Australia's Temporary Graduate visa (subclass 485)** lets eligible graduates stay and work, feeding into a **points-tested skilled migration** system. Australia's PR is competitive and occupation-driven.

Both are genuine settlement routes; Canada's Express Entry is often seen as more transparent, while Australia's system rewards in-demand occupations and strong points scores.

## Which Is Right for You?

Pick **Canada** if you want **lower tuition**, a longer study runway, and the well-mapped Express Entry pathway, and you don't mind cold winters.

Pick **Australia** if you value **graduate employability, climate, and lifestyle**, your field aligns with in-demand occupations, and you can absorb the higher upfront visa and tuition cost.

## How to Decide: A Simple Framework

Weigh three factors. **Budget:** Canada is generally cheaper upfront and on tuition; Australia costs more but may offer strong returns in certain fields. **PR strategy:** if you want the clearest published route, Canada's Express Entry is attractive; if your occupation is in demand in Australia, its points system can move quickly. **Lifestyle:** climate, city vibe, and proximity to family or community can tip the balance — be honest about what you'll enjoy living in for two-plus years.

## Costs Beyond Tuition

Both have high living costs in major cities (Toronto/Vancouver; Sydney/Melbourne). Budget for accommodation, transport, OSHC (Australia) or provincial health arrangements (Canada), the GIC (Canada), flights, and setup. Compare your **specific city and programme**, not just the country averages, since costs vary widely within each.

## How to Strengthen Whichever You Choose

Whichever you pick, the visa rewards the same fundamentals. At VisitPlane, we've mapped these patterns across dozens of routes, and the advice below applies directly to Indian students.

The decisive factor is **financial evidence**. For Canada, evidence tuition plus the living-cost benchmark convincingly — a GIC is the cleanest route — and secure your PAL. For Australia, show genuine, stable funds and a credible **Genuine Student** statement. In both, money seasoned over time beats a sudden deposit; a documented education loan is widely accepted.

Next, present a **coherent study plan**: why this course, this country, and how it fits your career. Make sure your **academic records and English scores** match the offer, arrange **OSHC** (Australia) for the full period, and apply early to respect intake deadlines.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Choosing on lifestyle alone** without checking PR-pathway fit for your occupation.
- **Underestimating Australia's upfront visa cost** (around AUD 2,000).
- **Underestimating Canada's proof-of-funds** requirement.
- **Missing the PAL** (Canada) or **OSHC** (Australia).
- **A weak Genuine Student statement** for Australia.

## Head-to-Head Scorecard

Here's how the two stack up dimension by dimension for Indian students:

- **Tuition:** Canada generally lower — Canada wins.
- **Upfront visa cost:** Canada wins decisively (CAD 235 vs ~AUD 2,000).
- **Proof-of-funds benchmark:** both firm; Australia's is higher in absolute terms.
- **Work while studying:** broadly equal (Canada ~24 hrs/week; Australia 48 hrs/fortnight).
- **Post-study work:** Canada's PGWP (up to 3 years) is generous; Australia's 485 is solid but varies.
- **PR pathway:** Canada's Express Entry is more transparent; Australia's points system rewards in-demand occupations.
- **Climate & lifestyle:** Australia wins for most students who dislike harsh winters.
- **Graduate employability:** strong in both; field-dependent.

Canada leads on cost and PR transparency; Australia leads on lifestyle and (for some occupations) migration speed.

## Verdict by Scenario

**You want the lowest cost and clearest PR route:** Canada — lower tuition, low visa fee, and Express Entry.

**Your occupation is in demand in Australia and you value lifestyle:** Australia's points-tested migration can move quickly, and the climate and graduate job market are big draws.

**You're cost-constrained upfront:** Canada's CAD 235 visa fee versus Australia's ~AUD 2,000 charge is a meaningful difference before you even arrive.

**You prioritise weather and outdoor life:** Australia is the obvious pick for students who'd struggle with Canadian winters.

Match the choice to whether cost-and-PR-clarity (Canada) or lifestyle-and-occupation-fit (Australia) matters more.

## Cost Snapshot (2026)

Rough first-year figures for an Indian student:

- **Canada:** permit CAD 150 + biometrics CAD 85, tuition roughly CAD 15,000–40,000/year, living-cost benchmark ~CAD 20,635/year, plus a GIC.
- **Australia:** visa ~AUD 2,000, OSHC ~AUD 600–1,000/year, tuition roughly AUD 20,000–45,000/year, living-cost benchmark ~AUD 29,710/year.

The clearest difference is the **upfront visa charge** — CAD 235 for Canada versus around AUD 2,000 for Australia — before tuition even enters the picture. Australia's higher tuition and living benchmark make it the pricier option overall, though strong graduate outcomes can offset that. Compare your specific programme and city.

## The Bottom Line

Canada and Australia are both outstanding choices for Indian students, and you won't go wrong with either — but they reward different priorities. Choose Canada if you want to keep costs down, value a long-established and transparent Express Entry route to permanent residence, and don't mind cold winters. Choose Australia if lifestyle, climate, and graduate employability top your list, your field aligns with in-demand occupations, and you can absorb the much higher upfront visa charge and tuition.

The single biggest practical difference is the money you part with before you even arrive: Canada's CAD 235 visa-and-biometrics fee versus Australia's roughly AUD 2,000 application charge. Beyond that, both demand serious proof of funds and a credible study plan. Whichever you pick, season your finances, secure your PAL (Canada) or write a strong Genuine Student statement and arrange OSHC (Australia), and apply well ahead of your intake. Decide on *cost-and-PR-clarity versus lifestyle-and-occupation-fit*, and you'll have made the right call for your goals.

## Long-Term Outlook: Careers and Staying On

Both countries are built to retain international graduates, but through different machinery. Canada's PGWP plus Express Entry rewards Canadian study and work experience with points toward permanent residence — a transparent, well-trodden path many Indian graduates follow. Australia's Temporary Graduate visa feeds a points-tested skilled-migration system that prizes in-demand occupations; if your field appears on the relevant skills lists, the route can move quickly, but if it doesn't, it can be slower and more competitive.

So your *field* matters as much as the country. A nursing, engineering, or IT graduate may find Australia's occupation-driven system fast; a broader profile may prefer Canada's points-based clarity. Research the current skilled-occupation lists and Express Entry draws before committing, since both systems are periodically retuned. Plan from the start: choose a programme and city that strengthen your post-study options, build relevant work experience during and after study, and keep your documentation immaculate. Whichever you pick, the graduates who settle successfully are the ones who treated study as step one of a deliberate, well-researched migration plan rather than an afterthought.
