## Overview

Dubai and the UAE are top destinations for Nigerian travellers — tourism, shopping, business, and visiting family. Nigerian passport holders need a **UAE visit (tourist) visa**, but there's no embassy queue: the process is **online**, arranged through an **airline, hotel, or authorised agent** that sponsors the application.

This guide explains the 2026 process for Nigerian citizens — the 30 vs 60-day options, documents, fees, security considerations, and entry tips. On VisitPlane, we verify every route against official sources.

> **Key takeaway:** Nigerian citizens need a **UAE visit visa** (no visa on arrival), applied for **online** via an airline (Emirates, flydubai, Air Arabia), a hotel, or an authorised agent — in **30-day** or **60-day** options.

## Do Nigerian Citizens Need a Visa for the UAE?

Yes. Nigerian passport holders must obtain a **visit visa before travel**. The usual categories are the **30-day** and **60-day** tourist visas (single or multiple entry), issued electronically. Note that Nigerian applications may undergo **additional security screening**, so apply with a comfortable buffer.

## Who Sponsors the Application

A UAE tourist visa needs a **sponsor** to submit it:

- **Airlines** — Emirates, flydubai, Etihad, Air Arabia (often bundled with flights).
- **Hotels** — many arrange visas for booked guests.
- **Authorised travel agents / tour operators.**
- A **UAE resident** relative or friend (family/visit visa) in some cases.

> **Key takeaway:** Use a **reputable airline or established agent**. Nigeria sees many visa scams — avoid unverified sellers promising "guaranteed" or unusually cheap visas.

## Documents You Need

For the UAE visit visa, Nigerian applicants typically need:

- A **passport valid for at least 6 months** with blank pages.
- A clear **passport bio-page scan** and a **passport-size photo** (white background).
- **Confirmed return flights**.
- **Accommodation** (hotel booking or host's details).
- **Bank statements** (often last 3–6 months) showing sufficient funds.
- Sometimes proof of employment/business and additional documents for screening.

> **Key takeaway:** Robust **financial evidence** and clear travel plans are especially important for Nigerian applicants, given the extra scrutiny.

## How to Apply: Step by Step

1. **Choose your sponsor** — airline, hotel, or authorised agent.
2. **Select the visa type** — 30-day or 60-day, single or multiple entry.
3. **Submit documents** — passport, photo, flights, accommodation, bank statements.
4. **Pay the fee** (plus service charge).
5. **Wait for processing and any security check** (commonly 3–7 working days).
6. **Receive the e-visa by email** — print it and carry it.

## Costs and Fees (2026)

- **30-day visit visa:** commonly around **AED 350** plus service charges.
- **60-day visit visa:** commonly around **AED 650** plus service charges.
- Express and multiple-entry options cost more.

All-in costs in naira vary widely with exchange rates and agent fees, so confirm the total with your sponsor before paying.

## Processing Time

UAE visit visas for Nigerian applicants are usually processed in **3–7 working days**, but the possible **security check** can extend this. Apply **at least 2 weeks** before travel to be safe.

## Visa Validity and Stay

The 30-day and 60-day visas permit stays of **30 or 60 days** respectively. Check whether the validity counts from issue or entry. You cannot work on a visit visa, and overstaying incurs **daily fines** — depart on time or extend via your sponsor where allowed.

## Why Applications Get Refused (and How to Avoid It)

Common issues for Nigerian applicants:

- **Additional security screening** delays or refusals — apply early and provide complete documents.
- **Weak financials** or missing bank statements.
- **Passport validity** under 6 months or poor scans.
- **Unverified agents** mishandling applications.

## Best Time to Visit and Practical Tips

The UAE is best enjoyed **November to March**, when temperatures are comfortable for the Burj Khalifa, desert safaris, and beaches; summers are intensely hot but indoor attractions are air-conditioned. Practical notes for Nigerian travellers:

- **Currency:** the UAE Dirham (AED); cards are accepted nearly everywhere.
- **Connectivity:** tourist SIMs (du, e&) on arrival; Careem and Uber are reliable.
- **Getting around:** Dubai Metro and taxis make the city easy to navigate.
- **Etiquette:** dress modestly in public, respect Ramadan customs, and note alcohol is limited to licensed venues; penalties for prohibited items are severe.
- **Documentation:** keep your e-visa, return ticket, and hotel booking handy on arrival.

## What You Can and Can't Do on This Visa

The visit visa is for **tourism and family visits** — not employment. You can sightsee, shop, and visit relatives for the visa's 30 or 60 days. You **cannot** work on it. Overstaying incurs **daily fines**, so depart on time or arrange a permitted extension through your sponsor. Carry proof of onward travel and funds for entry checks.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Unverified agents** promising "guaranteed" visas — avoid scams.
- **Underestimating the security check** — apply at least two weeks ahead.
- **Weak bank statements** or missing financial proof.
- **Passport validity** under 6 months or poor scans.
- **Overstaying** and accumulating daily fines.

## How to Strengthen Your the UAE Application

Whether your route is straightforward or strict, the same principles separate smooth approvals from frustrating delays — and they are worth understanding before you apply. At VisitPlane, we've mapped these patterns across dozens of visa routes, and the advice below applies directly to Nigerian travellers heading to the UAE.

Start with your **finances**, because they are the single most scrutinised part of almost any application. Officers and systems are not simply checking that you have money today; they are checking that your funds are *genuine and stable*. A balance that has sat comfortably in your account for three to six months tells a far more convincing story than a large sum that appeared a week before you applied. If a relative is supporting your trip, document that relationship clearly rather than leaving an unexplained deposit. Keep your statements clean, recent, and consistent with the trip you are describing.

Next, think about **consistency across your documents**. Your flight dates, hotel bookings, cover letter, and bank statements should all tell one coherent story. If your itinerary says ten days but your hotel booking covers four, or your stated purpose is tourism while your documents suggest something else, that mismatch invites questions and slows everything down. Before you submit, lay your documents side by side and read them as an officer would — every date and detail should line up.

For routes that assess intent, **ties to your home country** matter enormously. Evidence of stable employment, a running business, property, or dependents reassures the decision-maker that you intend to return after your visit to the UAE. This is less about any single document and more about the overall picture your application paints.

Finally, be **honest and complete**. Declaring a previous refusal — for any country — is almost always better than hiding it, because non-disclosure is treated far more seriously than an old rejection. Submit every requested document, double-check every field, and apply with enough time to absorb processing and any follow-up requests.
