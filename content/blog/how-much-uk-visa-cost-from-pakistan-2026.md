## Overview

The United Kingdom is one of the most popular destinations for Pakistani travellers — for tourism, visiting family, and business. But the true cost of a UK visa surprises many first-time applicants, because the headline visa fee is only one part of the total. Between the visa charge, the VFS Global service fee, biometrics, the mandatory TB test, and insurance, the real number is higher than the figure you see advertised.

This guide breaks down exactly what a Pakistani applicant pays for a **UK Standard Visitor visa** in 2026, including the fee rise that took effect in April 2026, so you can budget accurately. On VisitPlane, we verify every route against official sources.

> **Key takeaway:** A 6-month **UK Standard Visitor visa** costs **£135** (around **PKR 50,000**) in 2026. Add VFS service charges, the **mandatory TB test**, biometrics, and insurance, and most Pakistani applicants spend **PKR 60,000–75,000** all-in before flights and hotels.

## The Headline Visa Fee (2026)

UK visit visa fees rose across all categories in April 2026. For the **Standard Visitor visa**, the most common type for tourism and family visits:

- **6-month visit visa:** **£135** (~PKR 50,000)
- **2-year long-term visit visa:** around **£512**
- **5-year long-term visit visa:** around **£931**
- **10-year long-term visit visa:** around **£1,165**

Fees are paid at the official rate, so the rupee figure shifts slightly with the exchange rate.

## The Full Cost Breakdown

The visa fee alone isn't the whole story. A realistic Pakistani applicant's budget includes:

- **Visa fee:** ~PKR 50,000 for the 6-month visa.
- **VFS Global service charge:** a few thousand rupees depending on the centre and options.
- **TB test:** mandatory for Pakistani applicants, at an approved clinic (IOM) — budget a few thousand rupees.
- **Biometrics:** included in the appointment; premium/priority slots cost extra.
- **Travel insurance:** PKR 4,000–10,000 for a short trip (recommended).

Add these up and the **all-in cost is typically PKR 60,000–75,000** per adult for a standard 6-month visit visa.

## The Mandatory TB Test

Unlike applicants from many countries, **Pakistani applicants must complete a tuberculosis (TB) test** at an approved IOM clinic before applying for a UK visa of more than six months — and it's standard practice to have a valid certificate. Factor in the cost and the appointment time, as a missing certificate delays the application.

## Optional Extras That Add to the Cost

- **Priority service:** around **£500** for a faster (usually 5 working day) decision.
- **Super Priority service:** around **£1,000** for a next-working-day decision.
- **Premium lounge / prime time appointments** at VFS: additional.

These are optional — the standard service works for most travellers who apply early.

## How to Pay

You pay the visa fee online when you complete the application on the official UK government website, then pay VFS service charges at or before your appointment, by card. The **visa fee is non-refundable** even if your application is refused, so prepare your documents carefully before paying.

## What the Whole Trip Costs

Beyond the visa, budget for the trip:

- **Return flights (Pakistan–UK):** roughly PKR 200,000–380,000 depending on season.
- **Accommodation:** PKR 25,000–55,000 per night in London (less outside the capital), or stay with family.
- **Daily spend (food, transport, attractions):** PKR 12,000–28,000 per person.
- **Transport:** budget for the Oyster card and any intercity rail.

A typical 10-day UK trip can land around **PKR 6–12 lakh per person**, with flights and London hotels the biggest variables.

## Ways to Keep the Cost Down

- **Apply for the right duration** — don't pay for a long-term visa you don't need.
- **Skip priority** unless you're short on time.
- **Stay with family** or outside central London.
- **Travel off-peak** to cut flight and hotel costs.
- **Book refundable** flights/hotels until the visa is granted.

## How the UK Compares

For Pakistani travellers, the UK visit visa is pricier upfront than a Schengen visa (€90, about PKR 28,000) but a single visa covers the whole UK, and long-term multi-year visas can be cost-effective for those who visit family in Britain regularly.

## Documents That Justify the Spend

To avoid wasting a non-refundable fee, build a strong application: a passport valid for your trip, proof of funds (bank statements over 3–6 months), evidence of ties to Pakistan (job, business, property, family), a clear itinerary, accommodation details, and your TB certificate. ## What's Refundable — and What Isn't

The **UK visa fee is non-refundable** once your application is submitted, regardless of outcome. VFS service charges and the TB test cost are also non-refundable. The smart order of operations is to secure **refundable flights and hotels** first, submit a strong application, and only convert to non-refundable bookings once the visa is granted — protecting the much larger trip budget.

## How to Strengthen Your UK Application

Whether your route is straightforward or strict, the same principles separate smooth approvals from frustrating delays — and they protect the money you spend. At VisitPlane, we've mapped these patterns across dozens of visa routes, and the advice below applies directly to Pakistani travellers heading to the UK.

Start with your **finances**, the single most scrutinised part of almost any application. Officers check that your funds are *genuine and stable*, not just present. A balance that has sat comfortably for three to six months tells a far more convincing story than a large sum that appeared a week before you applied. If a relative in the UK is supporting your trip, document that relationship clearly rather than leaving an unexplained deposit.

Next, ensure **consistency across your documents**. Flight plans, accommodation, cover letter, and statements should tell one coherent story. Mismatches invite questions and delay. Because the visit visa assesses intent, **ties to Pakistan** matter enormously — stable employment, a business, property, or dependents reassure the officer you'll return.

Finally, be **honest and complete**, complete your **TB test** early, and declare any previous refusal truthfully — non-disclosure is treated far more seriously than an old rejection. ## Hidden Costs People Forget

Beyond the headline fee, several smaller costs catch Pakistani applicants out. The **TB test** at an approved IOM clinic is mandatory and has its own fee and appointment. **Document translation and attestation** — if any of your papers aren't in English — adds up. **Travel to the VFS centre** in Islamabad, Karachi, or Lahore, sometimes with an overnight stay, is a real cost for applicants outside those cities. Optional **premium lounge or courier return** services add convenience charges. And once approved, **forex spreads** on converting rupees for your trip quietly eat into your budget. Tallying these alongside the £135 fee gives a far more realistic picture than the visa charge alone — and avoids unwelcome surprises right before travel.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Underestimating the total** by looking only at the headline visa fee.
- **Forgetting the TB test** cost and appointment.
- **Paying for priority** you don't need.
- **Booking non-refundable flights** before approval.
- **Buying the wrong visa duration** for your travel pattern.

## Sample Budgets

To make the numbers concrete, here are two realistic all-in budgets for a Pakistani traveller (visa costs plus the trip):

- **Family visit (10 nights, staying with relatives):** visa ~PKR 70,000 + flights ~PKR 250,000 + daily spend and local travel ~PKR 150,000 = roughly **PKR 4.5–5 lakh per person**.
- **Two-week tourist trip (London + Scotland, hotels):** visa ~PKR 70,000 + flights ~PKR 300,000 + hotels ~PKR 5 lakh + rail and daily spend ~PKR 2 lakh = roughly **PKR 10–11 lakh per person**.

Families benefit from shared accommodation, but each traveller pays their own visa fee and TB test (with reduced fees for children on some visa types).

## Currency and Payment Tips

Pay the visa fee through the official channel only — never hand cash to an "agent." For the trip itself, a mix of a **travel/forex card**, a little cash, and a card with low foreign-transaction fees works best. Contactless payment is near-universal in the UK, so you'll rarely need large amounts of cash. Watch the **GBP/PKR rate** when you pay, since the rupee cost of the £135 fee moves with it.
