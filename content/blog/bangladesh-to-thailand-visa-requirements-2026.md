## Overview

Thailand is one of the most popular international getaways for Bangladeshi travellers — Bangkok's temples and shopping, the beaches of Phuket and Krabi, and the cooler hills of Chiang Mai. The big change to know in 2026: Thailand now runs a **mandatory e-Visa system**, so Bangladeshi citizens apply **online** through the official Thai e-Visa portal rather than submitting passports at the embassy.

This guide explains the 2026 e-Visa process for Bangladeshi passport holders — documents, the fee, processing time, validity, and how to maximise approval. On VisitPlane, we verify every route against official sources.

> **Key takeaway:** Bangladeshi citizens need a Thailand tourist visa, applied for **online** at the official Thai e-Visa portal (**thaievisa.go.th**). There is no visa on arrival for a standard tourist visit.

## Do Bangladeshi Citizens Need a Visa for Thailand?

Yes. Bangladeshi passport holders require a **tourist visa (TR)**. Since the e-Visa system became mandatory, you apply online and receive an electronic visa by email — no physical sticker in most cases.

## How the Thai e-Visa Works

- Applications go through the **official Thai e-Visa website (thaievisa.go.th)**.
- You apply from **within Bangladesh** and may be asked to **upload proof of your current location**.
- The approved e-Visa is **emailed to you** — print it and carry it.
- Avoid copycat sites; use the official portal.

> **Key takeaway:** Use only the **official** Thai e-Visa portal. Lookalike websites charge inflated "service" fees for the same government process.

## Documents You Need

For the Thailand tourist e-Visa, Bangladeshi applicants should prepare:

- A passport valid for **at least 6 months** with **2 blank pages**.
- A recent **passport-size photograph**.
- **Confirmed round-trip flights**.
- **Accommodation** bookings.
- **Bank statement** showing sufficient funds.
- Proof of employment or business, and a cover letter where relevant.

> **Key takeaway:** Strong, consistent **financials** and confirmed onward travel are central to a Thai tourist visa approval — show genuine funds, not a sudden deposit.

## How to Apply: Step by Step

1. **Create an account** on the official Thai e-Visa portal.
2. **Complete the application** with personal, passport, and travel details.
3. **Upload documents** — passport, photo, flights, accommodation, bank statement.
4. **Pay the visa fee** online.
5. **Wait for processing** (around 14 working days).
6. **Receive the e-Visa by email**, print it, and travel.

## Costs and Fees (2026)

- **Tourist e-Visa fee:** around **THB 2,000** (~BDT 6,500–7,000 depending on exchange rate).
- Use the official portal to avoid inflated agent charges.

## Processing Time

The Thai e-Visa typically takes about **14 working days** after a complete submission. Apply **at least 3–4 weeks** before travel, and earlier during peak holiday periods.

## Visa Validity and Stay

A single-entry tourist visa generally allows a stay of up to **60 days**, usually **extendable** by 30 days at a Thai immigration office for a fee. The e-Visa shows the validity (the window to enter) and permitted stay. You cannot work on a tourist visa, and overstaying carries daily fines.

## Why Applications Get Refused (and How to Avoid It)

For Bangladeshi applicants, the most common issues are:

- **Weak or unstable finances** — low balances or a sudden deposit.
- **Mismatched travel plans** — flights/hotels that don't align with dates.
- **Weak ties to Bangladesh** — no proof of employment, business, or family commitments.
- **Incomplete uploads** or using **unofficial sites**.

Fix the weak area (usually finances or ties) and reapply through the official portal if refused.

## How Much Will the Whole Trip Cost?

Rough 2026 estimates per person for a Bangladeshi traveller:

- **Visa:** ~THB 2,000 (~BDT 6,500).
- **Return flights (Bangladesh–Thailand):** roughly BDT 45,000–80,000 depending on season.
- **Accommodation:** BDT 3,000–9,000 per night; hostels/guesthouses cost less.
- **Daily spend (food, transport, activities):** BDT 3,500–7,000 per person.
- **Islands and tours:** modest add-ons.

A typical 7-day Thailand trip can land around **BDT 120,000–220,000 per person**, mostly flights.

## Best Time to Visit and Practical Tips

Thailand's **cool, dry season (November–February)** is the most pleasant across Bangkok, the islands, and the north. March–May is hot, and June–October brings monsoon showers. Practical notes for Bangladeshi travellers:

- **Currency:** the Thai Baht (THB); ATMs are everywhere, and cards work in cities — carry cash for markets and street food.
- **Connectivity:** cheap tourist SIMs at the airport; Grab for rides and food, plus Bangkok's BTS Skytrain and MRT.
- **Getting around:** budget domestic flights link Bangkok with Phuket, Krabi, and Chiang Mai.
- **Etiquette:** dress modestly at temples (cover shoulders and knees), and treat images of the monarchy and Buddhism with great respect.
- **Halal food:** widely available in Bangkok and tourist areas.

## What You Can and Can't Do on This Visa

The tourist visa is for **tourism and short visits** — not work. You can travel the country for up to **60 days**, usually extendable by 30 days at an immigration office. You **cannot** take up employment. Overstaying carries daily fines and possible bans, so track your permitted days from the entry stamp and extend in good time if needed.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Using unofficial sites** instead of the official Thai e-Visa portal.
- **Weak or sudden funds** rather than stable savings.
- **Mismatched flights and hotels** versus stated dates.
- **Applying too late** for the ~14-day processing window.
- **Forgetting location proof** if requested during the application.

## How to Strengthen Your Thailand Application

Whether your route is straightforward or strict, the same principles separate smooth approvals from frustrating delays — and they are worth understanding before you apply. At VisitPlane, we've mapped these patterns across dozens of visa routes, and the advice below applies directly to Bangladeshi travellers heading to Thailand.

Start with your **finances**, because they are the single most scrutinised part of almost any application. Officers and systems are not simply checking that you have money today; they are checking that your funds are *genuine and stable*. A balance that has sat comfortably in your account for three to six months tells a far more convincing story than a large sum that appeared a week before you applied. If a relative is supporting your trip, document that relationship clearly rather than leaving an unexplained deposit. Keep your statements clean, recent, and consistent with the trip you are describing.

Next, think about **consistency across your documents**. Your flight dates, hotel bookings, cover letter, and bank statements should all tell one coherent story. If your itinerary says ten days but your hotel booking covers four, or your stated purpose is tourism while your documents suggest something else, that mismatch invites questions and slows everything down. Before you submit, lay your documents side by side and read them as an officer would — every date and detail should line up.

For routes that assess intent, **ties to your home country** matter enormously. Evidence of stable employment, a running business, property, or dependents reassures the decision-maker that you intend to return after your visit to Thailand. This is less about any single document and more about the overall picture your application paints.

Finally, be **honest and complete**. Declaring a previous refusal — for any country — is almost always better than hiding it, because non-disclosure is treated far more seriously than an old rejection. Submit every requested document, double-check every field, and apply with enough time to absorb processing and any follow-up requests.
