## UK Family Visit Visa — Complete Guide for Pakistani Applicants

Thousands of Pakistani families are separated by borders, with relatives settled in the UK as citizens, permanent residents, or visa holders. Visiting them requires a UK Standard Visitor Visa — there is no separate "family visit visa" category. The same visa covers tourism, family visits, business trips, and medical visits.

But the approach, documentation, and emphasis of a family visit application differ meaningfully from a pure tourism application. This guide is written specifically for Pakistani citizens visiting family in the UK.

---

## Is There a "Family Visit Visa" for the UK?

Not as a distinct visa category. The **Standard Visitor Visa** covers all short-term visits to the UK, including family visits. When you apply, you select "visiting family or friends" as your purpose.

What changes is the **documentation** you submit and the **narrative** of your application. Your supporting case will lean on your host family's status, their invitation, and their ability and willingness to support your visit.

---

## Who Qualifies?

Any Pakistani national with a family member legally resident in the UK can apply, including visits to:

- Citizens (naturalised or British-born)
- Indefinite Leave to Remain (ILR) holders
- Visa holders (spouse visas, student visas, work visas)

The family definition for visit purposes is broad — parents, children, siblings, grandparents, cousins, in-laws, and extended family all count. The relationship must be genuine and ideally provable.

**Visa vs No Visa:** There is no visa-exempt category for Pakistani passport holders visiting the UK. All Pakistani nationals require a Standard Visitor Visa regardless of family connections.

## Documents Required

### Your Documents (Applicant)

1. **Valid Pakistani passport** — at least 6 months of remaining validity recommended beyond your return date
2. **Previous passports** — include all passports showing prior travel history and visas
3. **Completed online application form** — submitted via the UKVI portal
4. **Biometric enrollment** — at a UKVI application centre (VFS Pakistan)
5. **Passport photographs** — current, meeting UK specifications
6. **Bank statements** — last 6 months, bank-stamped and signed, showing consistent income and savings
7. **Employment documents** — employer letter confirming your job, salary, position, and approved leave dates; 6 months of payslips
8. **Tax returns / income evidence** — last 2 years (IRIS e-tax filings, FBR documents)
9. **Property ownership documents** — if applicable; demonstrates ties to Pakistan
10. **Evidence of ties to Pakistan** — family registration document, children's birth certificates (if family remains in Pakistan), property, business registration
11. **Travel history evidence** — copies of any prior visas (Schengen, UAE, USA, etc.)
12. **TB certificate** — required for applicants from certain regions; check whether your city is listed on the UKVI website

### Documents from Your UK Host

13. **Formal invitation letter** — signed, addressed to the British High Commission, with full details (see below)
14. **Proof of their legal status in the UK** — passport (if British citizen) or Biometric Residence Permit (if ILR/visa holder)
15. **Proof of address** — utility bill, tenancy agreement, or Council Tax bill from the last 3 months
16. **Proof of financial ability to support your visit** — last 3 months' bank statements, employment letter, or payslips

## How to Write an Invitation Letter

The invitation letter from your UK host is one of the most scrutinised documents in a family visit application. A weak invitation letter — vague, unverifiable, or missing key details — is a common rejection trigger.

**What the letter must include:**

1. Full name of the inviting person
2. Their UK address and contact details
3. Their relationship to you (mother, brother, friend, etc.)
4. Your full name and passport number
5. Dates of your proposed visit
6. Where you will stay (their address, or hotel if applicable)
7. A statement that they will support you financially (if they are contributing to your costs), or a statement that you are self-funding
8. Their signature and the date

**Format:** A formal letter on plain paper is fine. It does not need to be notarised for a visit visa — but notarisation adds credibility, especially for large extended family groups.

**Sample Opening:**
> *I, [Full Name], a British citizen residing at [Full Address], am writing to formally invite my [relationship], [Your Name] (Pakistani Passport No. [XXXXXX]), to visit me in the United Kingdom from [start date] to [end date]. During this period, [he/she/they] will reside at my address...*

## Processing Times

**Standard Processing:** Approximately 3 weeks (15 working days)
**Priority Service:** 5 working days
**Super Priority:** Next working day (very limited availability)

Apply at minimum 5 weeks before travel. For summer travel or visits around Pakistani and UK public holidays, apply 8–10 weeks early.

## Common Rejection Reasons for Family Visit Applications

1. **Insufficient evidence of ties to Pakistan** — the officer is not convinced you'll return. Counter with employment, property, family, and financial documentation.
2. **Host's invitation letter is vague** — incomplete dates, unclear relationship, no supporting documents from the host.
3. **Financial inconsistency** — your stated income doesn't match your bank statements.
4. **Previous overstay or refusal not disclosed** — mandatory disclosure; non-disclosure is automatic refusal.
5. **Weak employment evidence** — self-employed applicants without formal documentation struggle the most.
6. **Application submitted too close to travel date** — suggests urgency rather than genuine planned visit.
7. **Previous UK refusal** — prior refusal must be disclosed and the underlying issue clearly addressed in the new application.
8. **Host cannot demonstrate right to have guests** — tenants sometimes need landlord permission to host; include tenancy agreement that doesn't prohibit guests.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can my whole family apply together for UK family visit visas?
Yes. Family members can submit separate applications simultaneously and link them by noting the family relationship in each application. Each person pays the individual fee and submits their own documents.

### Does my UK family member need to sponsor my visa financially?
Not necessarily. If you are self-funding the trip (your own flights, accommodation costs, spending money), make this clear in your application and provide financial evidence. If your UK family is covering costs, their financial documentation must be included.

### How long can I stay in the UK on a family visit visa?
The Standard Visitor Visa allows stays of up to 6 months per visit. However, UKVI may limit your approved stay to a shorter period if they feel it is more appropriate. Multi-entry visas (2 or 10 years) are sometimes granted to applicants with strong histories.

### What if my UK family member has temporary status (student visa, work visa)?
They can still invite you. Include their Biometric Residence Permit and evidence of their own legal status, employment or student enrollment, and financial stability. A visitor sponsored by a visa holder requires stronger documentation.

### Is a TB test required for all Pakistani applicants?
TB testing is required for applicants from countries on the UK's screening list. Pakistan is on this list. You must be tested at an approved clinic; results are valid for 6 months. Check the UKVI website for the current list of approved clinics in Pakistan.
