## Overview

Vietnam is one of Asia's best-value and most beautiful destinations for Pakistani travellers — the limestone karsts of Ha Long Bay, the lantern-lit old town of Hoi An, bustling Hanoi and Ho Chi Minh City, terraced rice fields, and incredible street food. Best of all, the visa is simple: Pakistani passport holders apply for a **Vietnam e-Visa** entirely online and receive it by email.

This guide explains the 2026 process for Pakistani citizens — the application, the fee, documents, processing, validity, and approval tips. On VisitPlane, we verify every route against official sources.

> **Key takeaway:** Pakistanis can get a **Vietnam e-Visa** online at the official portal. It allows stays of **up to 90 days** (single or multiple entry), processing usually takes about **3 working days**, and there's **no embassy visit** required.

## Do Pakistani Citizens Need a Visa for Vietnam?

Yes. Pakistani passport holders need a visa for Vietnam, and the easiest route is the **e-Visa** — a fully online electronic visa introduced for all eligible nationalities. There's no need to visit an embassy.

## How the e-Visa Works

- Applied for **online** at the official Vietnam e-Visa portal.
- Allows stays of **up to 90 days**, available as **single or multiple entry**.
- Processed in about **3 working days** and delivered as a **PDF by email**.
- You print it and present it with your passport on arrival.

> **Key takeaway:** Apply only through the **official government e-Visa portal**; many lookalike sites charge large mark-ups for the same visa.

## Documents You Need

The Vietnam e-Visa needs very little:

- A **passport** valid at least **6 months** from arrival, with blank pages.
- A **scan of the passport bio-data page** and a **passport-style photo** (white background).
- Your **travel dates** and **intended entry/exit points**.
- A valid **email** and a card for the fee.

## How to Apply: Step by Step

1. **Visit the official Vietnam e-Visa portal** and start an application.
2. **Upload your passport page and photo**, and enter your travel details.
3. **Choose single or multiple entry** and your validity period.
4. **Pay the fee** online.
5. **Wait for processing** (usually about 3 working days).
6. **Receive the e-Visa by email**, print it, and carry it for entry.

## Processing Time

The e-Visa typically takes about **3 working days**, sometimes faster. Apply **at least a week or two ahead** of travel to allow for any delays. Avoid leaving it to the last minute.

## Visa Validity and Stay

The Vietnam e-Visa allows stays of **up to 90 days**, in single- or multiple-entry form depending on what you select and pay for. You cannot work on it. Track your days and don't overstay, which carries fines.

## Why Applications Get Refused (and How to Avoid It)

Approval rates are high; the few issues come from:

- **Typos** in name or passport number.
- **A photo** that doesn't meet specifications (white background, clear face).
- **Passport validity** under 6 months.
- **Using an unofficial site** that mishandles the application.

The fee is non-refundable, so review every field before submitting.

## Best Time to Visit and Practical Tips

Vietnam spans climates, but broadly **November–April** is drier in much of the country. Practical notes for Pakistani travellers:

- **Currency:** the Vietnamese Dong (VND); cards work in cities, cash for markets and small towns.
- **Connectivity:** tourist SIMs are cheap; Grab is the main ride-hailing and food app.
- **Getting around:** domestic flights and trains link north and south; motorbike taxis are everywhere.
- **Food:** incredible, affordable street food; halal options exist in cities — ask ahead.
- **Etiquette:** dress modestly at temples; bargaining is normal in markets.

## What You Can and Can't Do on This Visa

The Vietnam e-Visa is a **tourist visa** for tourism and short visits. You **can** sightsee, cruise, and take tours; you **cannot** work, and you must respect the permitted stay. Overstaying carries fines and can affect future entry, so leave on time.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Using unofficial e-Visa websites** that overcharge.
- **A photo** that fails the white-background/clarity rules.
- **Typos** in passport details on the non-refundable form.
- **Choosing the wrong entry/exit points** for your itinerary.
- **Applying too late** for processing.

## Single vs Multiple Entry: Which to Choose

A small decision in the application has a real effect on both cost and flexibility: **single** versus **multiple** entry. If your trip is a straightforward visit to Vietnam and out again, **single entry** is cheaper and perfectly sufficient. But if your itinerary loops through neighbouring countries — say a side trip to **Cambodia, Laos, or Thailand** before returning to Vietnam — you'll need **multiple entry**, which costs more but saves you from buying a second visa.

Think this through before you apply, because the e-Visa is tied to what you select and the fee is non-refundable. Also pick your **entry and exit checkpoints** to match your flights (for example, Hanoi in, Ho Chi Minh City out for a north-to-south journey). Getting these two choices right up front avoids the most common headaches Pakistani travellers face on this otherwise simple route, and means your printed e-Visa lines up cleanly with how you actually plan to travel.

## What to See in Vietnam

In the north, **Hanoi**'s Old Quarter, lakes, and street food set the scene, with the world-famous **Ha Long Bay** (or quieter Lan Ha Bay) a short trip away for an overnight cruise among emerald karsts. Central Vietnam offers **Hoi An**'s lantern-lit, UNESCO-listed old town, the imperial city of **Hue**, and the beaches of Da Nang. In the south, **Ho Chi Minh City** (Saigon) buzzes with history and energy, with the Cu Chi Tunnels and the Mekong Delta nearby. Add the cool highlands of **Sapa** with its terraced rice fields for a complete picture. Great value and easy domestic connections make a 7–10 day trip from north to south very doable.
