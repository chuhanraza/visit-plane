## Overview

Turkey is a hugely popular destination for Bangladeshi travellers — Istanbul's mosques and bazaars straddling two continents, the fairy chimneys and balloons of Cappadocia, the turquoise coast at Antalya, and the travertines of Pamukkale. But there's an important catch on the visa: **most Bangladeshi passport holders are NOT eligible for the Turkey e-visa** and must apply for a **sticker (embassy) visa** instead.

This guide explains the 2026 process for Bangladeshi citizens — who qualifies for the e-visa, how the sticker visa works, fees, documents, processing, and approval tips. On VisitPlane, we verify every route against official sources.

> **Key takeaway:** Bangladeshis can only use the **Turkey e-visa** if they hold a **valid visa or residence permit from a Schengen country, the USA, the UK, or Ireland** (with conditions). Without that, you must apply for a **sticker visa** through the Turkish Embassy / authorised visa centre, which takes about **15–20 working days**.

## Do Bangladeshi Citizens Need a Visa for Turkey?

Yes — and the type depends on what other visas you hold:

- **E-visa (conditional):** if you hold a **valid Schengen, US, UK, or Ireland visa/residence permit**, you may apply for the Turkey **e-visa** (single entry, 30-day stay), subject to the conditions on the official portal.
- **Everyone else:** you must apply for a **sticker visa** at the Turkish Embassy in Dhaka or its authorised visa application centre.

There is no visa-on-arrival for Bangladeshi citizens.

## How the E-Visa Works (If You Qualify)

- Applied for **online** at the official **evisa.gov.tr** portal.
- Requires a **valid supporting visa/residence** from Schengen/US/UK/Ireland.
- **Single entry**, allowing a **30-day stay**.
- Passport must be valid at least **60 days beyond** your intended stay.

> **Key takeaway:** Apply for the e-visa **only on evisa.gov.tr** — third-party sites charge large mark-ups. If you don't hold a qualifying supporting visa, the sticker visa is your route.

## How the Sticker Visa Works (Most Applicants)

- Applied for at the **Turkish Embassy in Dhaka** or its authorised visa application centre.
- Requires a full document set (below) and **biometrics**.
- **Processing about 15–20 working days.**

## Documents You Need (Sticker Visa)

- A **passport** valid **60+ days beyond** your stay, with blank pages.
- The **completed application form** and **biometric photos**.
- **Round-trip flight reservation** and **confirmed hotel bookings**.
- **Proof of funds** — bank statements (3–6 months), salary slips or business documents.
- **Travel insurance** for the trip.
- A **cover letter** and proof of **ties to Bangladesh**; an invitation letter if visiting family.

## How to Apply: Step by Step

1. **Check eligibility** — do you hold a valid Schengen/US/UK/Ireland visa? If yes, you may use the e-visa.
2. **E-visa route:** apply at evisa.gov.tr, pay online, and print the PDF.
3. **Sticker route:** book an appointment at the Turkish Embassy/visa centre, complete the form, and gather documents.
4. **Attend the appointment** to submit documents and give **biometrics**.
5. **Pay the visa fee** plus any service charge.
6. **Track and collect** your passport once decided.

## Costs and Fees (2026)

- **Sticker visa fees** vary by validity and nationality; budget for the visa fee plus service/biometric charges at the centre.
- The **e-visa** (for eligible holders) is cheaper but requires the qualifying supporting visa.

Confirm current amounts on official channels before paying — fees are non-refundable.

## Processing Time

The **sticker visa** takes about **15–20 working days** from application. The **e-visa** (if eligible) is usually issued within minutes to a few hours. Apply **at least 3–4 weeks ahead** for the sticker route to allow for biometrics and any extra checks.

## Visa Validity and Stay

The **e-visa** is single-entry with a **30-day stay**. **Sticker visas** vary by the validity granted and may be single or multiple entry. You cannot work on a tourist visa. Track your days and don't overstay.

## Why Applications Get Refused (and How to Avoid It)

- **Using a third-party "e-visa" site** when you're not actually eligible — apply for the sticker visa instead.
- **Insufficient or unstable funds.**
- **Weak ties to Bangladesh** or a thin invitation letter.
- **Passport validity** under 60 days beyond the stay.

The fee is non-refundable, so confirm your route and documents first.

## How Much Will the Whole Trip Cost?

Rough 2026 estimates per person for a Bangladeshi traveller:

- **Visa:** the visa fee plus service/biometric charges (sticker route).
- **Return flights (Bangladesh–Turkey):** roughly BDT 70,000–140,000 depending on season.
- **Accommodation:** BDT 6,000–18,000 per night in Istanbul.
- **Daily spend:** BDT 5,000–12,000 per person.
- **Tours (Cappadocia balloon, Bosphorus cruise):** add-ons worth budgeting for.

A typical 6–7 day Turkey trip can land around **BDT 2–4 lakh per person**, with flights the main variable.

## Best Time to Visit and Practical Tips

Turkey is best in **April–June** and **September–November** — mild and ideal for sightseeing and balloon rides. Practical notes for Bangladeshi travellers:

- **Currency:** the Turkish Lira (TRY); cards work in cities, cash helps in bazaars.
- **Connectivity:** tourist SIMs are cheap; domestic flights link Istanbul, Cappadocia, and Antalya.
- **Getting around:** Istanbul has trams, metro, and ferries; internal flights save time over long drives.
- **Food:** halal food is the norm; vegetarian options are easy — Bangladeshi palates feel at home.
- **Etiquette:** dress modestly at mosques (head covering for women); locals are warm and hospitable.

## What You Can and Can't Do on This Visa

A Turkey tourist visa (e-visa or sticker) is for **tourism and short visits**. You **can** sightsee and take day trips; you **cannot** work or stay beyond your permitted period. The e-visa is single-entry (30 days); sticker visas follow the validity granted. Track your days and avoid overstaying, which carries fines.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Assuming you can get an e-visa** without a qualifying Schengen/US/UK/Ireland visa.
- **Booking non-refundable flights** before approval.
- **Underfunded or unexplained** bank statements.
- **Using unofficial visa websites** that overcharge.
- **Passport validity** under 60 days beyond the stay.

## What to See in Turkey

Most trips begin in **Istanbul**, where the **Blue Mosque**, **Hagia Sophia**, Topkapı Palace, and the Grand and Spice Bazaars sit on a peninsula between Europe and Asia, with a Bosphorus cruise tying it all together. **Cappadocia** is the bucket-list highlight — surreal fairy-chimney landscapes best seen from a **sunrise hot-air balloon**, plus underground cities and cave hotels. On the coast, **Antalya** and the turquoise Mediterranean offer beaches and ancient ruins, while **Pamukkale**'s white travertine terraces and the Roman city of **Ephesus** reward history lovers. With halal food everywhere and short domestic flights, a week-long trip covering Istanbul plus Cappadocia is very comfortable.

## How to Strengthen Your Turkey Application

Whether your route is straightforward or strict, the same principles separate smooth approvals from frustrating delays — and they are worth understanding before you apply. At VisitPlane, we've mapped these patterns across dozens of visa routes, and the advice below applies directly to Bangladeshi travellers heading to Turkey.

The first step is **confirming the right route**. Many applicants waste money on third-party "e-visa" sites without realising they aren't eligible. If you don't hold a valid Schengen, US, UK, or Ireland visa, plan for the sticker visa from the start and gather the full document set.

Then focus on your **finances**, the single most scrutinised part of almost any application. Officers check that your funds are *genuine and stable*, not just present. Next, ensure **consistency** across flights, hotels, cover letter, and statements. Because tourist visas assess intent, **ties to Bangladesh** matter enormously — stable employment, a business, property, or dependents reassure the officer you'll return. Be honest and complete, and declare any prior refusals truthfully.
