## Overview

Nepal passport-holders need a **Temporary Resident Visa (TRV)** — a visitor visa — to travel to Canada for tourism, visiting family, or business. The process is **online through IRCC**, with **biometrics** given in person at the VFS Global Canada Visa Application Centre in Kathmandu. Done in the right order, it's straightforward; the refusals usually come from weak funds or unclear ties, not from the paperwork itself.

## Who Needs a Visa

All Nepali citizens need a visitor visa before travelling to Canada — there is no visa-on-arrival or eTA route for Nepal passport-holders travelling for tourism (the eTA applies only to visa-exempt nationalities, which Nepal is not). You apply for the TRV in advance, every time, unless you already hold a valid multiple-entry visa.

## Requirements at a Glance

To apply you'll need a **passport** valid for your intended stay (with blank pages), a completed online application, a recent **digital photo** to specification, proof of **funds** to cover your trip, a **purpose-of-travel** explanation (tourism plan, invitation, or business letter), evidence of **ties to Nepal** (employment, business, family, property), and your **travel history**. If you're visiting family, an **invitation letter** from your host in Canada strengthens the file.

## Step-by-Step: How to Apply

First, **create an IRCC secure account** and complete the visitor-visa application online. Second, **upload your documents** — passport, photo, funds, purpose, and ties. Third, **pay the fees**: the visitor-visa fee is **CAD 100**, plus the **CAD 85 biometrics** fee. Fourth, you'll receive a **Biometric Instruction Letter (BIL)** and book an appointment at the VFS Global Canada Visa Application Centre in Kathmandu to give fingerprints and a photo. Fifth, **wait for processing** and, if approved, send your passport for visa stamping as instructed. Track everything through your IRCC account.

## Proof of Funds and Finances

Canada doesn't publish a fixed visitor-visa amount, but you must show you can **cover the trip and your stay** and that the money is genuinely yours. Provide bank statements for the **past four to six months**, not just a recent balance, and document the source of any large deposit. If someone in Canada sponsors your visit, include their **invitation letter**, status in Canada, and supporting financial documents. The goal is to remove any doubt that you can afford the trip without working illegally.

## Ties to Nepal: The Quiet Decider

The most common reason visitor visas are refused is that the officer isn't convinced you'll **return home**. Counter that head-on: show stable **employment** or a **business**, **family** responsibilities, **property**, and a specific reason your life continues in Nepal after the trip. A short, honest cover letter tying these together — who you are, why you're visiting, when you'll return, and what brings you back — is one of the highest-value pages in the file.

## Processing Time

Visitor-visa processing varies by season and demand, so check the **current estimate** in your IRCC account or on the IRCC website rather than relying on a fixed number. Apply **well ahead** of your travel dates — at least several weeks, and more during peak periods — and don't book non-refundable travel until your visa is approved and you've checked the details.

## A Realistic Cost Snapshot

Budget for the **CAD 100** visa fee, **CAD 85** biometrics, the cost of your photo and any document courier, and — once approved — your travel. Keep receipts for fees paid; they're part of a tidy file. Remember these are the *application* costs; the funds you must *show* for the trip itself are separate and larger.

## Tourism, Visiting Family, or Business: What Changes

The visitor visa is the same TRV whichever the purpose, but the **supporting story** differs. For **tourism**, show a rough itinerary, accommodation, and funds for the trip. For **visiting family or friends**, the centrepiece is an **invitation letter** from your host in Canada stating who they are, your relationship, the purpose and length of your visit, and where you'll stay — backed by their proof of status (citizen, PR, or valid permit) and, if they're helping financially, their funds. For **business**, include a letter from your Nepal employer and the inviting Canadian company explaining the meetings, conference, or training. Matching your documents to your actual purpose is what makes the application read as genuine.

## Your Document Checklist in Detail

Build the file in four bundles. **Identity:** passport with blank pages and a spec-compliant photo. **Money:** four-to-six months of bank statements, proof of income, and source documents for any large deposit. **Purpose:** itinerary, bookings, invitation letter, or business correspondence as relevant. **Ties:** employment or business proof, property papers, family documents, and a short cover letter that connects them. Label and order the bundles so a reviewer can move through them quickly — a tidy file signals a genuine, organised applicant, while a jumbled one invites doubt even when the underlying facts are fine.

## Multiple-Entry Visas and Validity

Canada commonly issues **multiple-entry visitor visas** valid for up to the passport's expiry (and up to ten years where the passport allows), letting you visit repeatedly for stays of usually up to six months each. You don't choose single versus multiple entry — the officer decides based on your profile — so you don't need to request it specially. What you *can* control is presenting a strong enough application that a longer-validity, multiple-entry visa is the natural outcome. Each time you enter, a border officer confirms your purpose and the length of stay, so keep your supporting documents accessible when you travel.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Showing a sudden large deposit** with no source documentation.
- **Weak or vague ties** to Nepal, so the officer doubts you'll return.
- **Booking non-refundable travel** before approval.
- **An incomplete purpose** — no plan, invitation, or business letter.
- **Inconsistencies** between your form, your funds, and your stated plans.
- **Applying too late** for the current processing time.

## After You Apply: What to Expect

Once you've given biometrics, the decision arrives through your IRCC account. If you're approved, you'll be asked to submit your passport for the visa to be affixed — check the **name, validity, and entry type** as soon as you receive it, and only then convert any refundable bookings into paid ones. If you're refused, you can request your **GCMS notes** to see the officer's reasoning, fix the specific weakness — most often funds or ties — and reapply with a stronger file rather than resubmitting the same one. There's no mandatory waiting period, but reapply only when your application genuinely answers the refusal.

## If You Hold a US Visa

Holding a valid US visa doesn't exempt Nepal travellers from the Canadian visitor visa, but a clean history of travel to the US, the UK, or Schengen — with timely returns — strengthens your file considerably. It demonstrates you've been trusted with a visa before and complied with its terms, which speaks directly to the officer's main question about whether you'll return home. Include copies of prior visas and entry/exit stamps in your travel-history bundle.

## The Bottom Line

For Nepali travellers, a Canada visitor visa is very achievable: apply online, pay CAD 100 plus CAD 85 biometrics, give biometrics in Kathmandu, and — most importantly — show genuine funds with a clear history and strong ties to Nepal. The paperwork is routine; the judgement calls are about money and intent. Get those two right, document them cleanly, and apply early, and you turn a daunting-looking process into a predictable approval.
