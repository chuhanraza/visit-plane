## Overview

The Netherlands is a compact, easy-to-love destination for Pakistani travellers — Amsterdam's canals and museums, the tulip fields of Keukenhof, windmills, cycling-friendly cities, and a famously international, English-speaking culture. To visit, Pakistani passport holders need a **Schengen short-stay visa (Type C)** issued by the Netherlands for tourism, family visits, or business.

This guide explains the 2026 process for Pakistani citizens — the new fee, documents, where you apply, how long it takes, and how to maximise approval. On VisitPlane, we verify every route against official sources.

> **Key takeaway:** Pakistanis need a **Schengen visa** for the Netherlands. Apply through the official partner (**VFS Global**) up to **6 months** ahead; the fee rose to **€90** on 11 June 2026, and the Netherlands is among the **fastest** Schengen processors, often deciding in **5–10 days**.

## Do Pakistani Citizens Need a Visa for the Netherlands?

Yes. Pakistani passport holders must obtain a Schengen visa before travelling; there is no visa-on-arrival. For tourism or family visits, you apply for a **short-stay Type C visa** allowing up to **90 days within any 180-day period** across the Schengen area.

## How the Schengen Visa Works

- It is a **short-stay visa** valid for the whole **29-country Schengen area**.
- You apply at the consulate of your **main destination** — if the Netherlands is where you'll spend the most days, you apply to the Netherlands.
- Stays are capped at **90 days in any rolling 180-day window**.
- Entries can be single or multiple, at the consulate's discretion.

> **Key takeaway:** Apply to the **Netherlands** only if it's your main destination (most nights) or first entry point — applying to the wrong country is a common refusal reason.

## Documents You Need

- A **passport** valid at least **3 months beyond** departure, with two blank pages, issued within 10 years.
- The **completed Schengen form** and a recent **biometric photo**.
- **Travel medical insurance** covering at least **€30,000**.
- **Round-trip flight reservation** and **confirmed accommodation** for the full stay.
- **Proof of funds** — bank statements for 3–6 months, salary slips or business documents.
- A **cover letter** with your itinerary and proof of **ties to Pakistan**.

## How to Apply: Step by Step

1. **Confirm the Netherlands is your main destination** and draft an itinerary.
2. **Complete the application** on the official Netherlands visa portal.
3. **Book a VFS Global appointment** in Pakistan (Islamabad, Karachi, Lahore).
4. **Attend the appointment** to submit documents and give **biometrics**.
5. **Pay the visa fee** plus the VFS service charge.
6. **Track and collect** your passport once decided.

## Costs and Fees (2026)

- **Schengen visa fee:** **€90** for adults (raised from €80 on 11 June 2026); **€45** for children 6–11; free under 6.
- In rupees this is roughly **PKR 28,000**, plus a **VFS service charge**, so budget **PKR 32,000–38,000** all-in per adult before insurance.

Confirm the current amount on the official portal before paying — it's non-refundable.

## Processing Time

The Netherlands is one of the **fastest** Schengen processors, frequently deciding in **5–10 calendar days**. The legal standard is **15 days**, extendable to 45 for extra checks. Apply **3–4 weeks ahead** normally and **6 weeks ahead** for summer travel.

## Visa Validity and Stay

A short-stay Schengen visa allows up to **90 days within any 180-day period**. The consulate sets the validity window and entry type; strong travel history improves the chance of a longer, multiple-entry visa. You cannot work on it. Track your days across all Schengen countries.

## Why Applications Get Refused (and How to Avoid It)

- **Insufficient or unstable funds.**
- **Weak ties to Pakistan.**
- **Inconsistent itinerary** across flights, hotels, and cover letter.
- **Insurance below €30,000.**
- **Wrong consulate** when the Netherlands isn't the main destination.

The fee is non-refundable, so review everything before submitting.

## How Much Will the Whole Trip Cost?

Rough 2026 estimates per person for a Pakistani traveller:

- **Visa:** ~PKR 32,000–38,000 including service charge.
- **Return flights (Pakistan–Amsterdam):** roughly PKR 180,000–320,000 depending on season.
- **Accommodation:** PKR 18,000–45,000 per night in Amsterdam.
- **Daily spend:** PKR 12,000–26,000 per person.
- **Insurance:** PKR 4,000–9,000.

A typical 5–6 day Netherlands trip can land around **PKR 5–8 lakh per person**, with flights and Amsterdam hotels the main variables.

## Best Time to Visit and Practical Tips

The Netherlands is best in **April–May** (tulip season at Keukenhof) and **June–September** for mild, long days. Practical notes for Pakistani travellers:

- **Currency:** the Euro (€); cards (and contactless) are used almost everywhere.
- **Connectivity:** eSIMs and SIMs are easy; trains and trams are punctual and efficient.
- **Getting around:** cycling is the national pastime — rent a bike; Amsterdam is very walkable.
- **Food:** halal food is available in cities; vegetarian options too — South Asian restaurants in Amsterdam.
- **Etiquette:** locals are direct and English-fluent; respect bike lanes when walking.

## What You Can and Can't Do on This Visa

A Schengen Type C visa is a **short-stay visitor visa** for tourism, family, or business, capped at **90 days per 180 days**. You **can** travel across all Schengen countries within your validity, but you **cannot** work, study long-term, or overstay. Overstaying risks fines and bans.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Non-refundable flights** booked before approval.
- **Thin or freshly funded** bank statements.
- **Insurance gaps** or under €30,000.
- **Applying to the Netherlands** when most nights are elsewhere.
- **Weak cover letter** without clear ties to Pakistan.

## What to See in the Netherlands

Most trips centre on **Amsterdam**, where the concentric canal ring (a UNESCO site), the Rijksmuseum, the Van Gogh Museum, and the Anne Frank House sit alongside cosy cafés and bicycle-filled streets. Just outside the city, the **Zaanse Schans** windmills and the spring tulip gardens of **Keukenhof** are classic day trips. Beyond the capital, **Rotterdam** dazzles with bold modern architecture, **The Hague** hosts the royal seat and the seaside at Scheveningen, and **Utrecht** and **Delft** offer charming canalside old towns. The whole country is small and superbly connected by train, so a five-day trip comfortably covers Amsterdam plus two or three nearby cities.

## How to Strengthen Your Netherlands Application

Whether your route is straightforward or strict, the same principles separate smooth approvals from frustrating delays — and they are worth understanding before you apply. At VisitPlane, we've mapped these patterns across dozens of visa routes, and the advice below applies directly to Pakistani travellers heading to the Netherlands.

Start with your **finances**, the single most scrutinised part of almost any application. Officers check that your funds are *genuine and stable*, not just present. A balance that has sat comfortably for three to six months is far more convincing than a large sum that appeared just before you applied. If a relative is sponsoring you, document that relationship clearly rather than leaving an unexplained deposit.

Next, ensure **consistency across your documents**. Flight dates, hotels, cover letter, and statements should tell one story. If your bookings and itinerary don't match, that gap invites questions. Read your file as an officer would before submitting.

Because the Schengen visa assesses intent, **ties to Pakistan** matter enormously — stable employment, a business, property, or dependents reassure the officer you'll return. It's the overall picture that counts.

Finally, be **honest and complete**. Declaring a past refusal is almost always better than hiding it. Submit every requested document, check every field, and apply early.
