## Overview

Japan — cherry blossoms in spring, Mount Fuji, the neon of Tokyo, and the temples of Kyoto — is one of the most rewarding destinations for Indian travellers. Indian passport holders need a **visa in advance** (there is no visa on arrival), but the process has become easier: since **1 April 2024**, Indian travellers can apply for a **short-term tourist eVisa**, with approval delivered digitally as a **Visa Issuance Notice** you display on your phone at the airport.

This guide explains both the **eVisa** and the traditional **sticker visa**, the documents you need, the fees, and realistic processing times. On VisitPlane, we verify every route against official sources.

> **Key takeaway:** Indians need a Japan visa. A **single-entry short-term tourist eVisa** is available via accredited agencies; the traditional **sticker visa** through VFS/the embassy remains the route for other cases.

## Do Indian Citizens Need a Visa for Japan?

Yes. There is **no visa-on-arrival or visa-free entry** for Indian citizens. For tourism you apply for a **short-term stay (tourist) visa**, valid for stays of up to **90 days**. You can obtain it as:

- An **eVisa** (single-entry, short-term tourist) — applied for online through an **accredited travel agency**, with a digital Visa Issuance Notice.
- A **sticker visa** in your passport — applied for through the Japan Visa Application Centre (VFS Global) or, in some cases, the embassy/consulate.

## eVisa vs Sticker Visa

- **eVisa:** convenient and paperless; currently for **single-entry short-term tourism**. You receive a digital notice to show at the airport — no passport sticker.
- **Sticker visa:** the traditional label in your passport; used for multiple-entry requests, visiting relatives, business, or where the eVisa doesn't apply.

Choose the eVisa for a straightforward single-entry holiday; use the sticker visa for everything else.

## Documents You Need

For a short-term tourist visa, Indian applicants should prepare:

- A passport valid for **at least 6 months** with blank pages.
- The visa application form and a recent **photograph** to Japan's specifications.
- **Confirmed return flight** booking.
- **Hotel bookings** or an invitation letter from a host in Japan.
- A **day-by-day itinerary** (Japan places weight on a clear schedule).
- **Financial proof** — bank statements, ITRs, and salary slips.
- Proof of employment or business.

> **Key takeaway:** Japan values a **detailed daily itinerary** matched to your hotel bookings and flights. A vague plan is the most common reason applications stall.

## How to Apply: Step by Step

### eVisa route

1. **Apply through an accredited agency** authorised to process Japan eVisas for Indian travellers.
2. **Submit** your passport scan, photo, itinerary, and financial documents.
3. **Pay** the visa fee and agency charge.
4. **Receive the Visa Issuance Notice** digitally — save it on your phone and as a backup print.
5. **Show it at the airport** in Japan on arrival.

### Sticker visa route

1. **Lodge your application** at the Japan Visa Application Centre (VFS Global) with your documents.
2. **Pay** the fee and service charge.
3. **Collect** your passport with the visa sticker.

## Costs and Fees (2026)

- **Single-entry tourist visa fee:** modest (commonly around **₹450**), plus a **VFS/agency service charge** (around **₹800** or more depending on provider).
- eVisa applications carry the agency's processing charge in addition to the government fee.

Fees are among the lowest of any major destination, but confirm the exact all-in price with your agency or VFS.

## Processing Times

Processing for the Japan tourist visa/eVisa is typically **5–7 working days** after a complete submission. Apply at least **2–3 weeks** before travel, and earlier during cherry-blossom (spring) and autumn-foliage peaks when demand is high.

## Visa Validity and Stay

A short-term tourist visa permits stays of up to **90 days**. The eVisa is currently **single-entry**; if you need multiple entries, apply for a sticker visa. You cannot work on a tourist visa. Always confirm the validity and permitted stay on your visa or Issuance Notice.

## Tips to Maximise Approval

- **Submit a detailed, realistic itinerary** aligned with your bookings.
- **Show stable funds** across recent months.
- **Use an accredited agency** for the eVisa — avoid unverified sellers.
- **Carry both digital and printed** copies of the Visa Issuance Notice.
- **Apply early** for spring and autumn travel.

## How Much Will the Whole Trip Cost?

Japan's visa is cheap, but the trip itself is a bigger investment — and your financial documents should reflect that you can afford it. Rough 2026 estimates per person for an Indian traveller:

- **Visa:** around ₹450 plus VFS/agency service charge (~₹800+).
- **Return flights (India–Japan):** roughly ₹50,000–₹95,000 depending on season and city.
- **Accommodation:** ₹6,000–₹14,000 per night in Tokyo/Osaka; capsule and business hotels are cheaper.
- **Daily spend (food, transport, attractions):** ₹6,000–₹10,000 per person.
- **Japan Rail Pass / transport:** a worthwhile add-on if covering long distances.
- **Travel insurance:** ₹2,000–₹4,000 for a typical trip.

A typical 7-day Japan trip lands around **₹1.5–2.6 lakh per person**, excluding shopping. Because Japan weighs your itinerary and finances closely, make sure your **bank statements comfortably cover the trip** and your day-by-day plan matches your bookings — this is the single biggest factor in a smooth approval.

## Visiting Family or Business in Japan

While this guide centres on the **tourist** visa, the documents shift for other purposes:

- **Visiting relatives or friends:** you'll typically need an **invitation letter**, the host's details, and sometimes documents proving their status in Japan and the relationship.
- **Business visits:** meetings, conferences, or negotiations usually require a letter from the inviting Japanese company and supporting details of the trip (not for paid work).

For these, the **sticker visa** route via VFS Global is generally used rather than the single-entry tourist eVisa. Be clear and consistent about your purpose, because mismatched documents are the most common cause of delay.

## Best Time to Visit and Practical Tips

Japan's headline seasons are **cherry blossom (late March–April)** and **autumn foliage (November)** — both stunning but busy, so apply for your visa early. Summer is hot and humid; winter offers snow festivals and skiing.

Practical notes for Indian travellers:

- **Itinerary matters:** Japan weighs your day-by-day plan heavily, so prepare a realistic schedule that matches your hotel bookings.
- **Currency:** the Japanese Yen (JPY); Japan still uses cash more than many countries, though cards and IC transit cards (Suica/Pasmo) are increasingly accepted.
- **Rail travel:** consider a Japan Rail Pass if you'll cover long distances; book trains in advance during peak seasons.
- **Connectivity:** pocket Wi-Fi rental or a travel eSIM keeps you online for maps and translation.
- **Carry your visa notice:** keep the digital Visa Issuance Notice on your phone plus a printed backup for the airport.

## Application Timeline and Pre-Departure Checklist

Apply early, especially around cherry-blossom and autumn peaks. Processing is typically 5–7 working days. As a rule of thumb, lock your passport validity and bookings first, then complete the visa or entry steps, and keep everything consistent across documents. Plan with a buffer: the most common delay is a vague itinerary that doesn't match your hotel bookings, so handle that early rather than at the last minute.

Use this quick checklist before you go:

- Detailed day-by-day itinerary prepared
- Hotels and return flight booked to match it
- Bank statements showing you can fund the trip
- Apply via an accredited agency (eVisa) or VFS
- Apply 2–3 weeks ahead, earlier in peak season
- Save the Visa Issuance Notice on phone + printed

Tick every box and you remove the vast majority of avoidable delays and refusals. When in doubt, confirm the latest rules on the official source before you travel, since fees and procedures can change.

Japan rewards the organised traveller: a tight, realistic itinerary backed by genuine funds is the heart of a strong application, and it also makes for a far better trip once you arrive.
