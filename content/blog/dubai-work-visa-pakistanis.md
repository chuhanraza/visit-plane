## 🌍 Overview

The Dubai work visa is an employment permit issued by the UAE Ministry of Human Resources and Emiratisation (MOHRE), valid for 1-3 years depending on the employment contract. Unlike tourist visas, work visas require sponsorship from a licensed UAE employer. The process involves securing a job offer, employer sponsorship, medical examination, and document submission. Pakistan ranks among the top nations for UAE employment, with established communities and proven professional networks. Processing times have improved significantly in 2026, with digital procedures accelerating approvals.

**Dubai Work Visa Overview:**
- **Sponsorship:** Required from licensed UAE employer (non-negotiable)
- **Validity:** 1-3 years based on contract
- **Renewal:** Automatic with employer sponsorship
- **Processing Time:** 2-4 weeks from visa application
- **Approval Rate for Pakistanis:** 92% with complete documentation
- **Work Authorization:** Valid immediately upon issue

## ✅ Who Needs This Visa?

Any Pakistani national seeking to work in Dubai or other UAE emirates requires a work visa. This applies to all employment types: engineers, nurses, teachers, laborers, managers, and entrepreneurs. Without an employer sponsor, you cannot obtain a work visa. The work visa is mandatory for all paid employment, regardless of salary level or employment duration. Self-employment and business ownership involve different visa categories (business/investor visas).

**You need a Work Visa if:**
- Securing paid employment in UAE
- Working for any registered UAE company
- Accepting management or professional position
- Employment contract longer than 30 days
- Any full-time or part-time paid work

## 📝 Application Process

The work visa application process is employer-driven but requires Pakistani applicant cooperation. Follow these steps:

1. **Secure Job Offer** — Find employment and receive written offer letter from UAE employer

2. **Verify Employer Credentials** — Confirm employer is registered with MOHRE (Ministry of Human Resources)

3. **Prepare Personal Documents** — Gather passport, photographs, certificates, and medical records

4. **Get Medical Examination** — Visit approved medical center and complete required health tests

5. **Obtain Police Certificate** — Apply through Pakistani police authorities for clearance (takes 2-3 weeks)

6. **Send Documents to Sponsor** — Forward documents to your UAE employer for visa processing

7. **Employer Applies for Visa** — Employer submits application to MOHRE with your documents

8. **Visa Approval** — MOHRE processes and approves (typically 2-4 weeks)

9. **Receive Visa** — Employer provides approved visa documents

10. **Book Travel** — Arrange flights and accommodation in Dubai

11. **Report to Sponsor** — Contact employer upon arrival in UAE

12. **Complete Residency Registration** — Register with GDRFA (General Directorate of Residency and Foreigners Affairs) within 30 days

## 💡 Tips for Approval

Pakistani applicants have excellent approval rates for Dubai work visas. Strengthen your application with these strategies:

**1. Reliable Employer** — Ensure your sponsor is legitimate, properly registered UAE company with good MOHRE standing. Verify through official channels.

**2. Complete Medical Records** — Pass all required health tests. Some conditions may require additional documentation from specialist physicians.

**3. Clean Police Record** — Ensure police clearance is clean with no criminal history. This accelerates approval.

**4. Accurate Documentation** — Ensure all documents match perfectly (name, passport number, dates). Discrepancies cause delays.

**5. Clear Employment Contract** — Contract should clearly specify job title, salary, benefits, and contract duration. Vague contracts raise concerns.

**6. Professional Presentation** — Submit documents in organized, professional manner. Shows respect for the process.

**7. Early Preparation** — Start document collection immediately upon receiving job offer. Don't delay police certificate or medical exam.

**8. Stay in Contact** — Maintain communication with employer sponsor throughout process. Ask for status updates and submit documents promptly.

## ❓ FAQ

**Q: Can I look for jobs in Dubai without a work visa?**
A: Tourist visas allow job hunting (30-90 days). Many Pakistani workers come on tourist visa, secure employment, then apply for work visa.

**Q: How long is the work visa valid for?**
A: Typically 1-3 years matching your employment contract. Upon contract renewal, visa is renewed automatically if employer sponsors.

**Q: What's the average Pakistani salary in Dubai?**
A: Varies widely: AED 1,500-2,500/month (laborers), AED 3,000-6,000 (skilled workers), AED 8,000-15,000+ (professionals).

**Q: Can I transfer to another employer?**
A: Only with written release from current employer. Some contracts include restrictions. Speak with HR before attempting transfer.

**Q: Can family members join me?**
A: Yes, once employed and earning minimum salary (varies by type). You can sponsor spouse, children, parents depending on salary.
