## The List

### 1. Kenya
Safari country at its best — the Maasai Mara, Amboseli's elephants under Kilimanjaro, and the beaches of Mombasa and Diani. A well-trodden, welcoming route for Ghanaian travellers and one of the more accessible safari destinations on the continent.

### 2. Tanzania
The Serengeti, Ngorongoro Crater, and Zanzibar's beaches in one country — pair a safari with island time for a genuinely varied trip. [Check the Ghana → Tanzania visa](/visa/Ghana/Tanzania) for your passport before booking.

### 3. Rwanda
Compact, clean, and increasingly popular for gorilla trekking in Volcanoes National Park and a fast-modernising Kigali — a shorter, more focused African trip than a full safari circuit.

### 4. South Africa
Cape Town, the Garden Route, and Kruger's wildlife in one of Africa's most complete trips — cosmopolitan cities alongside genuine wilderness.

### 5. Ivory Coast
A West African neighbour with the energy of Abidjan, the beaches of Grand-Bassam, and a growing food and arts scene — an easy, nearby first stop for a regional trip.

### 6. Mauritius
Turquoise lagoons, resort-quality beaches, and a distinct Indian Ocean Creole culture — a step up in polish from most West African beach options, at a manageable flight time.

### 7. Seychelles
Granite boulders, palm-fringed beaches, and some of the Indian Ocean's most photographed coastline — a smaller, quieter alternative to Mauritius.

### 8. Thailand
Bangkok's temples and street food, Chiang Mai's mountains, and the southern islands — genuinely excellent value once you're there, and one of the most complete first trips to Southeast Asia.

### 9. Malaysia
Kuala Lumpur's skyline, Penang's food scene, and Borneo's rainforests and islands — a modern, easy-to-navigate introduction to the region.

### 10. Vietnam
Halong Bay's limestone karsts, Hanoi's old quarter, and exceptional street food from north to south — outstanding value for a longer-haul trip.

### 11. United Arab Emirates
Dubai and Abu Dhabi combine striking modern architecture with desert excursions and reliable connections back to West Africa — often a useful stopover as well as a destination in its own right.

### 12. Georgia
Tbilisi's old town, Caucasus mountain scenery, and an emerging wine region — one of the least-crowded, best-value options on this list for travellers who want something different.

## How to Choose the Right One for You

Start with what you actually want from the trip. If it's **wildlife and landscape**, the East and Southern African picks — Kenya, Tanzania, Rwanda, South Africa — deliver the most. If it's **beaches and relaxation**, Mauritius and Seychelles lead, with Zanzibar (via Tanzania) as a strong alternative. If it's a **longer-haul city-and-culture trip**, Thailand, Malaysia, Vietnam, the UAE, and Georgia each offer something distinct. Then weigh three practical filters: the **flight cost and time** from Accra or Kumasi, the **visa route** for your Ghanaian passport, and the **season** you can travel.

## A Money-Saving Booking Checklist

Whichever destination you pick, these habits keep costs down: search flights in **incognito mode** and set **fare alerts**; be flexible with dates and fly **midweek**; travel in the **shoulder or low season**; book accommodation a little **outside the centre** with good transport links; and prefer places with a kitchen or included breakfast. On the ground, eat where locals eat, use public transport, and buy sensible multi-day passes.

## When Is the Best Time to Go?

Timing shapes both price and experience. East and Southern African safaris generally have a **dry-season sweet spot** (roughly June to October) for the best wildlife viewing, though the wetter months are cheaper and greener. Southeast Asia's Thailand, Malaysia, and Vietnam are best in their **cooler, drier months**, while the Gulf's UAE is most comfortable outside the peak summer heat. Georgia suits the warmer months for hiking, and Mauritius and Seychelles are pleasant most of the year with a wetter cyclone-risk season to check. Confirm the specific climate window for your chosen country before booking.

## Practical Tips for Ghanaian Travellers

Keep a **clean, multi-month bank history** so that any visa or e-visa application is straightforward, and hold copies of previous visas and stamps to show a good travel record. Build a **realistic daily budget** for each destination — Mauritius and the UAE cost noticeably more day-to-day than Rwanda or Kenya. Consider **shoulder-season travel** and **midweek flights**, and look at combining nearby countries — Kenya and Tanzania, or Mauritius and Seychelles — to spread a long-haul airfare across more than one stop. Register any required arrival details or e-visa approval before you fly, so nothing holds you up at the airport.
