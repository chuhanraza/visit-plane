## Overview

Azerbaijan — the flame-shaped towers of Baku, the old city walls, the Caspian coast, and mountain villages like Sheki and Gabala — has become a popular, affordable getaway for Pakistani travellers. Best of all, entry is simple: Pakistani passport holders apply for the **ASAN e-visa** entirely online and receive it by email, with **no embassy visit** required.

This guide explains the 2026 process for Pakistani citizens — the fee, documents, processing speed (including a same-day urgent option), validity, and approval tips. On VisitPlane, we verify every route against official sources.

> **Key takeaway:** Pakistani citizens need an Azerbaijan **ASAN e-visa**. Apply at the **official portal (evisa.gov.az)**; standard processing is **3 working days**, with an **urgent (≈3-hour)** option for an extra fee.

## Do Pakistani Citizens Need a Visa for Azerbaijan?

Yes. Azerbaijan does **not** offer visa-on-arrival to Pakistani passport holders. The standard tourist route is the **ASAN e-visa**, a single-entry electronic visa for short stays, applied for online.

## How the ASAN e-Visa Works

ASAN Visa is Azerbaijan's official electronic visa system — widely regarded as one of the smoothest in the world:

- Applied for **online** at evisa.gov.az.
- **Single entry**, usually granting a **30-day stay** within a 90-day validity window.
- **Standard** processing in **3 working days**; an **urgent** option returns a decision in around **3 hours**.
- The e-visa is emailed to you as a PDF.

> **Key takeaway:** Use the **official evisa.gov.az** portal. Lookalike sites charge inflated fees for the same government e-visa.

## Documents You Need

The ASAN e-visa is light on paperwork. Pakistani applicants need:

- A **passport valid for at least 6 months** from arrival.
- A **scan of the passport bio-data page**.
- A valid **email address** and a card for the online fee.
- Your **travel dates**.

Some travellers also keep a return ticket and hotel booking ready, as immigration may ask on arrival.

## How to Apply: Step by Step

1. **Go to evisa.gov.az** and start an application.
2. **Choose standard or urgent** processing.
3. **Enter your details** and upload your passport page.
4. **Pay the fee online**.
5. **Receive the e-visa by email** (3 working days standard, ~3 hours urgent).
6. **Print it** and carry it with your passport.

## Costs and Fees (2026)

- **Standard e-visa:** a modest government fee (around **USD 20–25**) plus the portal service charge.
- **Urgent (≈3-hour) processing:** an additional fee.
- Budget separately for flights, accommodation, and funds to show on arrival.

Confirm the exact amount on the official portal, as fees can change.

## Processing Time

**Standard:** about **3 working days**. **Urgent:** roughly **3 hours**, available even on holidays. Apply at least a few days before travel for the standard option.

## Visa Validity and Stay

The ASAN e-visa is typically a **single-entry** visa allowing a **30-day stay** within a **90-day validity**. You cannot work on it. Don't overstay — fines apply.

## Why Applications Get Refused (and How to Avoid It)

Approval rates are high, but issues arise from:

- **Typos** in passport number or name.
- **Passport validity** under 6 months.
- **Poor-quality** passport scans.

Double-check your details before paying, since the fee isn't refundable.

## Best Time to Visit and Practical Tips

Azerbaijan is loveliest in **April–June** and **September–October**, with mild weather for Baku and the mountains; summers are warm and winters can be cold. Practical notes for Pakistani travellers:

- **Currency:** the Azerbaijani Manat (AZN); cards work in Baku, but carry cash for regions.
- **Connectivity:** local SIMs (Azercell, Bakcell) are easy to buy; Bolt and Uber operate in Baku.
- **Getting around:** Baku is walkable; day trips to Gobustan (mud volcanoes and petroglyphs), Gabala, and Sheki are popular.
- **Etiquette:** Azerbaijan is welcoming to Muslim travellers, with halal food widely available and mosques throughout the country.
- **Language:** Azerbaijani and Russian are common; English is growing in tourist areas.

## What You Can and Can't Do on This Visa

The ASAN e-visa is a **single-entry tourist visa** for short stays (typically 30 days within a 90-day validity). You can sightsee across the country and take day trips. You **cannot** work, and you cannot re-enter on the same single-entry visa after leaving. Overstaying carries fines, so track your days from the entry stamp.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Typos** in passport number or name (the fee is non-refundable).
- **Passport validity** under 6 months.
- **Assuming visa-on-arrival** — it is not available to Pakistani travellers.
- **Low-quality passport scan.**
- **Leaving it too late** for standard 3-day processing (use the urgent option if needed).

## What to See in Azerbaijan

Most trips start in **Baku**, where the futuristic Flame Towers and Heydar Aliyev Center sit beside the UNESCO-listed walled Old City (Icherisheher) with its Maiden Tower and Palace of the Shirvanshahs. Stroll the Caspian-front Boulevard, ride up to the Highland Park for sweeping views, and explore the museums and cafe culture downtown.

Beyond the capital, easy day trips open up a different side of the country. **Gobustan** offers ancient rock petroglyphs and bubbling mud volcanoes within a couple of hours of Baku. To the north and west, **Gabala**, **Sheki** (famous for its Khan's Palace and sweets), and the **Absheron Peninsula** (fire temples and the eternal flames of Yanar Dag) make memorable additions. For a Pakistan-friendly trip, halal food is widely available, the people are warm and hospitable, and distances are short enough that a 5–6 day itinerary comfortably covers Baku plus two or three excursions.

## How to Strengthen Your Azerbaijan Application

Whether your route is straightforward or strict, the same principles separate smooth approvals from frustrating delays — and they are worth understanding before you apply. At VisitPlane, we've mapped these patterns across dozens of visa routes, and the advice below applies directly to Pakistani travellers heading to Azerbaijan.

Start with your **finances**, because they are the single most scrutinised part of almost any application. Officers and systems are not simply checking that you have money today; they are checking that your funds are *genuine and stable*. A balance that has sat comfortably in your account for three to six months tells a far more convincing story than a large sum that appeared a week before you applied. If a relative is supporting your trip, document that relationship clearly rather than leaving an unexplained deposit. Keep your statements clean, recent, and consistent with the trip you are describing.

Next, think about **consistency across your documents**. Your flight dates, hotel bookings, cover letter, and bank statements should all tell one coherent story. If your itinerary says ten days but your hotel booking covers four, or your stated purpose is tourism while your documents suggest something else, that mismatch invites questions and slows everything down. Before you submit, lay your documents side by side and read them as an officer would — every date and detail should line up.

For routes that assess intent, **ties to your home country** matter enormously. Evidence of stable employment, a running business, property, or dependents reassures the decision-maker that you intend to return after your visit to Azerbaijan. This is less about any single document and more about the overall picture your application paints.

Finally, be **honest and complete**. Declaring a previous refusal — for any country — is almost always better than hiding it, because non-disclosure is treated far more seriously than an old rejection. Submit every requested document, double-check every field, and apply with enough time to absorb processing and any follow-up requests.
