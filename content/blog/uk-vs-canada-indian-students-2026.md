## Overview

For Indian students, the UK and Canada are two of the most popular study-abroad destinations — and choosing between them is one of the biggest decisions of the journey. Both offer world-class universities, English-language instruction, and post-study work, but they differ sharply on course length, cost, work rights, and the path to permanent residence.

This guide compares the UK and Canada head-to-head for Indian students in 2026, so you can match the choice to your goals and budget. On VisitPlane, we verify every route against official sources.

> **Key takeaway:** Choose the **UK** for **shorter, faster degrees** (one-year master's) and a quicker, cheaper route to a foreign qualification. Choose **Canada** for a **clearer long-term path to permanent residence** and lower tuition over a longer study period. Both are excellent — the right answer depends on whether your priority is speed or settlement.

## Tuition and Course Length

The **UK** is famous for its **one-year master's degrees** — you finish faster and start earning sooner, which can offset higher annual tuition. Bachelor's degrees are typically three years.

**Canada** programmes generally run **longer** (two-year master's and four-year bachelor's are common), but **tuition per year is often lower**, and the longer stay builds the Canadian experience that immigration pathways reward.

If you want a qualification quickly and at a predictable total cost, the UK's compressed timeline is attractive. If you're treating study as the first step toward living abroad long-term, Canada's longer runway is an advantage.

## Visa Cost and Requirements

For the **UK Student visa**, expect the **£558 application fee** plus the **Immigration Health Surcharge at £776 per year**, paid upfront — a significant cost for a multi-year course, though manageable for a one-year master's. You'll need a CAS, proof of funds held for 28 days, and a TB test.

For the **Canada study permit**, the fee is modest — **CAD 150 plus CAD 85 biometrics** — but the real hurdle is **proof of funds**, with the living-cost benchmark now around **CAD 20,635 per year** plus tuition, often shown via a GIC, alongside a **Provincial Attestation Letter (PAL)**.

So the UK front-loads cost through the IHS; Canada keeps fees low but demands robust proof of funds.

## Work Rights While Studying

Both allow part-time work during study, with full-time in breaks:

- **UK:** generally up to **20 hours per week** in term time.
- **Canada:** recently up to around **24 hours per week** off-campus during term.

The two are broadly comparable, with Canada currently allowing slightly more term-time hours.

## Post-Study Work and PR Pathway

This is where the decision often turns.

- The **UK Graduate Route** lets eligible graduates stay and work for **2 years** (3 for PhDs). It's a generous work window, but the onward path to settlement requires moving onto a skilled-worker visa with an eligible job and salary.
- **Canada's PGWP** can run up to **3 years** depending on your programme, and Canada has **well-trodden PR pathways** (Express Entry, Provincial Nominee Programs) that many international graduates use to settle. For students whose ultimate goal is permanent residence, Canada's structured route is a major draw.

## Which Is Right for You?

Pick the **UK** if you want a **fast, prestigious qualification**, prefer a one-year master's, and are comfortable with the upfront IHS cost — especially if your plan is to gain UK experience and then decide.

Pick **Canada** if your goal is **long-term settlement**, you want lower annual tuition over a longer stay, and a clear, published PR pathway matters to you.

Neither is "better" in the abstract — they suit different goals. Be honest about whether you're optimising for **speed and cost** (UK) or **settlement and runway** (Canada).

## How to Decide: A Simple Framework

Ask yourself three questions. **First, what's my time horizon?** If you want to be qualified and home (or working) within a year or two, the UK fits; if you're planning a multi-year life-building project, Canada fits. **Second, what's my budget shape?** The UK costs more per year but for fewer years; Canada spreads cost over a longer period with lower tuition but a big proof-of-funds requirement. **Third, is PR the goal?** If yes, Canada's pathways are clearer; if you're undecided or just want the credential, the UK keeps options open.

## Costs Beyond Tuition

Both are expensive cities-and-living destinations. Budget for accommodation (high in London and Toronto/Vancouver), transport, and setup. The UK's higher cost of living in the capital can rival Canada's, so compare your specific city rather than the country as a whole. Factor flights, insurance/IHS, and the GIC (for Canada) into your first-year cash needs.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Choosing on prestige alone** rather than your actual time horizon and PR goals.
- **Underestimating the UK IHS** across a multi-year course.
- **Underestimating Canada's proof-of-funds** requirement.
- **Missing the PAL** for Canada or the **TB test** for the UK.
- **Applying late** and missing your intake.

## Head-to-Head Scorecard

Here's how the two stack up dimension by dimension for Indian students:

- **Tuition (total):** Canada often lower per year, but the UK's one-year master's can mean a lower *total* — call it a tie that depends on your programme.
- **Course length:** UK wins for speed (one-year master's).
- **Upfront visa cost:** Canada wins (low fee) — the UK's multi-year IHS is heavy.
- **Proof-of-funds burden:** UK's 28-day rule is simpler; Canada's benchmark is higher — UK slightly easier.
- **Work while studying:** Canada slightly ahead on hours.
- **Post-study work window:** Canada (up to 3 years PGWP) beats the UK's 2-year Graduate Route.
- **Clear PR pathway:** Canada wins decisively (Express Entry / PNPs).
- **Speed to qualification:** UK wins.

On balance, the UK is the "fast credential" choice and Canada the "settle abroad" choice.

## Verdict by Scenario

**You want a master's and to be back (or working) within ~2 years:** the UK's one-year model is ideal — qualify fast, use the Graduate Route, then decide.

**Your goal is to settle abroad permanently:** Canada's PGWP-to-Express-Entry route is the clearest published pathway, and the longer study runway works in your favour.

**You're cost-sensitive year by year:** Canada's lower annual tuition helps, but remember the big proof-of-funds requirement; the UK costs more per year but for fewer years.

**You're undecided about staying:** the UK keeps options open with a strong qualification and flexible 2-year work rights, without committing you to a long settlement project.

Be honest about which scenario is yours — that, more than rankings, should drive the decision.

## Cost Snapshot (2026)

Rough first-year figures for an Indian student, to make the trade-off concrete:

- **UK:** visa £558 + IHS £776/year, tuition typically £14,000–£30,000, living costs £12,000–£16,000+ (higher in London). A one-year master's keeps the *total* contained.
- **Canada:** permit CAD 150 + biometrics CAD 85, tuition roughly CAD 15,000–40,000/year, living-cost benchmark ~CAD 20,635/year, plus a GIC. Lower tuition per year, but spread over a longer programme.

The headline: the UK costs more per year but for fewer years; Canada costs less per year over a longer stay. Run the numbers on your specific programme and city — that total, not the per-year figure, is what matters for the decision.
