## The List

### 1. Malaysia
A short hop with modern cities, rainforest, and island beaches — Kuala Lumpur, Penang's food scene, and Borneo's wildlife all in one easy neighbour.

### 2. Philippines
Over 7,000 islands' worth of beaches, diving, and distinct regional cultures, easily reached from Jakarta or Surabaya. [Check the Indonesia → Philippines visa](/visa/Indonesia/Philippines) for your passport before booking.

### 3. Singapore
A compact, ultra-modern city-state that works well as a short break or a stopover on a longer Southeast Asia trip.

### 4. Thailand
Bangkok's temples and street food, Chiang Mai's mountains, and the southern islands — one of the region's most complete, best-value trips.

### 5. Vietnam
Halong Bay's karsts, Hanoi's old quarter, and exceptional street food from north to south — outstanding value and easy to reach.

### 6. Cambodia
Angkor Wat alone justifies the trip, paired with very low day-to-day costs and a genuinely different pace from its ASEAN neighbours.

### 7. Turkey
Istanbul's bazaars, Cappadocia's hot-air balloons, and the Aegean coast — a longer-haul trip that punches well above its visa difficulty.

### 8. Morocco
Marrakech's medinas, the Atlas Mountains, and the Sahara's dunes — atmospheric and affordable, with a genuinely different feel from Southeast Asia.

### 9. Qatar
Doha's museums, souqs, and waterfront skyline, with the Gulf's usual mix of modern architecture and desert excursions nearby.

### 10. Georgia
Tbilisi's old town, dramatic Caucasus scenery, and an emerging wine region — one of the least-crowded, best-value longer-haul options here.

### 11. Jordan
Petra, Wadi Rum's desert, and the Dead Sea packed into a compact, welcoming country — a strong single-country history trip.

### 12. Maldives
Once a honeymoon-only splurge, now accessible via local-island guesthouses — turquoise water and reef diving without the five-star price tag.

## How to Choose the Right One for You

Start with what you actually want from the trip. If it's a **quick, easy Southeast Asia break**, the six ASEAN neighbours need the least planning. If it's **history and culture on a longer trip**, Turkey, Morocco, and Jordan each deliver a genuinely different world. If it's **beaches and relaxation**, the Maldives is the standout, with Qatar and the Gulf useful as a stopover. Then weigh three practical filters: the **flight cost and time** from your city, the **visa route** for your Indonesian passport, and the **season** you can travel.

## A Money-Saving Booking Checklist

Whichever destination you pick, these habits keep costs down: search flights in **incognito mode** and set **fare alerts**; be flexible with dates and fly **midweek**; travel in the **shoulder or low season**; book accommodation a little **outside the centre** with good transport links; and prefer places with a kitchen or included breakfast. On the ground, eat where locals eat, use public transport, and buy sensible multi-day passes.

## When Is the Best Time to Go?

Timing shapes both price and experience. The **ASEAN neighbours** are generally best avoiding the peak monsoon window in each destination — check the specific country, since Southeast Asia's wet seasons don't all line up. Turkey and Georgia suit the warmer months for sightseeing and hiking; Morocco is most comfortable in spring and autumn, avoiding summer's Sahara-edge heat; and the Maldives has a drier season roughly from December to April. Confirm the specific climate window for your chosen country before booking.

## Practical Tips for Indonesian Travellers

Keep a **clean, multi-month bank history** so that any visa or e-visa application is straightforward, and hold copies of previous visas and stamps to show a good travel record. Build a **realistic daily budget** for each destination — the Maldives and Qatar cost noticeably more day-to-day than Cambodia or Vietnam. Consider **shoulder-season travel** and **midweek flights**, and look at combining nearby countries — Thailand and Cambodia, or Turkey and Georgia — to spread a long-haul airfare across more than one stop. Register any required arrival details or e-visa approval before you fly, so nothing holds you up at the airport.
