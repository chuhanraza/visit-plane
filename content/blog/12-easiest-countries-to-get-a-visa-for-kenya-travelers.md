## The List

### 1. Tanzania
The Serengeti, Ngorongoro Crater, and Zanzibar's beaches — an easy overland or short-flight trip from Kenya, and a natural extension of an East African safari. [Check the Kenya → Tanzania visa](/visa/Kenya/Tanzania) for your passport before booking.

### 2. Uganda
Gorilla trekking in Bwindi, the source of the Nile at Jinja, and a compact, easy-to-reach neighbour for a shorter regional trip.

### 3. Rwanda
Volcanoes National Park's gorilla trekking and a fast-modernising, spotlessly clean Kigali — a shorter, more focused trip than a full safari circuit.

### 4. Ghana
A West African trip with Accra's energy, the coastal forts of Cape Coast, and a growing arts and food scene — a longer but genuinely rewarding regional detour.

### 5. South Africa
Cape Town, the Garden Route, and Kruger's wildlife in one of Africa's most complete trips — cosmopolitan cities alongside genuine wilderness.

### 6. Zambia
Victoria Falls from the Zambian side, plus South Luangwa's walking safaris — quieter and less touristed than the well-worn East African circuit.

### 7. Mauritius
Turquoise lagoons and resort-quality beaches with a distinct Indian Ocean Creole culture — a step up in polish, at a manageable flight time from Nairobi.

### 8. Seychelles
Granite boulders, palm-fringed beaches, and some of the Indian Ocean's most photographed coastline — smaller and quieter than Mauritius.

### 9. Malaysia
Kuala Lumpur's skyline, Penang's food scene, and Borneo's rainforests and islands — a modern, easy introduction to Southeast Asia.

### 10. Singapore
A compact, ultra-modern city-state that works well as a short break or a stopover on a longer Asia trip.

### 11. Thailand
Bangkok's temples and street food, Chiang Mai's mountains, and the southern islands — one of the region's most complete, best-value trips.

### 12. United Arab Emirates
Dubai and Abu Dhabi combine striking modern architecture with desert excursions, and Nairobi's direct connections make it an easy add-on or stopover.

## How to Choose the Right One for You

Start with what you actually want from the trip. If it's a **quick regional safari extension**, Tanzania, Uganda, and Rwanda need the least planning. If it's a **bigger single-country trip**, South Africa delivers the most variety. If it's **beaches**, Mauritius and Seychelles lead, or Zanzibar via Tanzania. If it's a **longer-haul city trip**, Malaysia, Singapore, Thailand, and the UAE each offer something different. Then weigh the **flight cost and time** from Nairobi or Mombasa, the **visa route** for your Kenyan passport, and the **season** you can travel.

## A Money-Saving Booking Checklist

Whichever destination you pick, these habits keep costs down: search flights in **incognito mode** and set **fare alerts**; be flexible with dates and fly **midweek**; travel in the **shoulder or low season**; book accommodation a little **outside the centre** with good transport links; and prefer places with a kitchen or included breakfast. On the ground, eat where locals eat, use public transport, and buy sensible multi-day passes.

## When Is the Best Time to Go?

Timing shapes both price and experience. East African safari destinations — Tanzania, Uganda, Rwanda — have a **dry-season sweet spot** roughly June to October for the best wildlife viewing, though the wetter months are cheaper and greener. Southern Africa's South Africa and Zambia are best in the drier winter months (roughly May to September) for wildlife, while Mauritius and Seychelles are pleasant most of the year with a wetter, cyclone-risk season to check. Southeast Asia's Malaysia, Singapore, and Thailand suit the cooler, drier months. Confirm the specific climate window for your chosen country before booking.

## Practical Tips for Kenyan Travellers

Keep a **clean, multi-month bank history** so that any visa or e-visa application is straightforward, and hold copies of previous visas and stamps to show a good travel record. Build a **realistic daily budget** for each destination — Mauritius, Seychelles, and the UAE cost noticeably more day-to-day than Uganda or Rwanda. Consider **shoulder-season travel** and **midweek flights**, and look at combining nearby countries — Tanzania and Uganda, or South Africa and Zambia — to spread a long-haul airfare across more than one stop. Register any required arrival details or e-visa approval before you fly, so nothing holds you up at the airport.
