## Overview

There has never been an easier time for Pakistani travellers to visit China. From **10 November 2025 through 31 December 2026**, Pakistani passport holders can enter mainland China **visa-free for up to 30 days** for tourism, business, or transit — no advance visa, and **no visa fee**.

This is a major change. Below, we explain exactly how the visa-free policy works for Pakistani citizens, what you must carry on arrival, the limits of the scheme, and the standard **visa route** to use if you need a longer or different type of stay. On VisitPlane, we verify every route against official sources.

> **Key takeaway:** Pakistani citizens can enter China **visa-free for up to 30 days** for tourism, business, or transit until **31 December 2026**. Carry your passport, return ticket, and proof of accommodation and funds.

## Do Pakistani Citizens Need a Visa for China?

For short visits during the policy window, **no**. Under the current **visa-free arrangement (10 Nov 2025 – 31 Dec 2026)**, Pakistani travellers can enter for up to **30 days** without a visa. You will still need a visa if you plan to:

- Stay **longer than 30 days**.
- Travel for **work/employment, long-term study, or journalism**.
- Enter for purposes beyond tourism, ordinary business, or transit.

For those cases, apply for the appropriate Chinese visa in advance (see below).

## What to Carry for Visa-Free Entry

Visa-free does not mean check-free. Pakistani travellers should arrive with:

- A **passport valid for at least 6 months** with blank pages.
- A **confirmed return or onward air ticket** within 30 days.
- **Proof of accommodation** in China (hotel bookings or host address).
- **Proof of sufficient funds** for the trip.
- A clear idea of your **itinerary**, in case the officer asks.

> **Key takeaway:** Immigration officers can still question or refuse travellers who can't show onward travel, accommodation, and funds — keep these ready even though no visa is required.

## Limits of the Visa-Free Scheme

- **30 days maximum** per entry under the policy.
- **No work** or paid activity.
- The policy is **time-limited** (currently to 31 December 2026) and could be changed or extended by the Chinese authorities.

Always confirm the policy is still active for your travel dates before booking — if it lapses, Pakistani travellers revert to needing a visa.

## If You Need a Chinese Visa (Longer or Other Purposes)

If your trip falls outside the visa-free scope, apply for the relevant visa **before** travelling:

- **L visa** for tourism beyond 30 days, **M visa** for business, and other categories for study/work.
- Apply at the **Chinese Embassy/Consulate or visa centre** in Pakistan (Islamabad, Karachi).
- A notable Pakistan-specific requirement: a **Police Character Certificate (non-criminal record)**, attested by the Ministry of Foreign Affairs of Pakistan, is generally **mandatory** for visa applications.
- Typical documents: passport, form, photo, flights, accommodation, bank statement, and the police certificate.
- Processing is commonly around **4 working days**.

## Step by Step: Travelling Visa-Free

1. **Confirm the visa-free policy** is active for your dates (currently to 31 Dec 2026).
2. **Check passport validity** (6+ months).
3. **Book return/onward flights** within 30 days and accommodation.
4. **Prepare proof of funds** and a basic itinerary.
5. **Arrive in China**, present your passport and supporting documents, and clear immigration.
6. **Enjoy up to 30 days** — and leave before your limit expires.

## Costs

The headline benefit: **visa-free entry is free** for eligible Pakistani travellers during the policy window. Your only mandatory spending is the trip itself. If you instead need a visa (longer/other purposes), a government fee and service charge apply, plus the cost of the attested police certificate.

## Tips for a Smooth Trip

- **Re-check the policy** close to departure in case it changes.
- **Carry digital and printed copies** of your bookings and funds proof.
- **Don't overstay** the 30-day limit — penalties apply.
- **Use official apps** for payments and transport, as China is largely cashless.
- **Keep your passport on you**, as hotels and some sites require it.

## Business, Study, or Visiting Relatives

The visa-free policy covers tourism, ordinary business, and transit for up to 30 days. For other purposes — or longer stays — apply for the relevant visa in advance:

- **M visa** — for commerce and trade beyond a short business visit; usually needs an invitation from a Chinese company or trade body.
- **X visa** — for study; requires admission documents from a Chinese institution.
- **Z visa** — for work; requires a work permit and employer sponsorship.
- **Q visa** — for visiting Chinese-citizen or permanent-resident relatives; needs an invitation letter.

Remember the Pakistan-specific step: visa applications generally require a **Police Character Certificate** attested by Pakistan's Ministry of Foreign Affairs, so start that process early.

## Best Time to Visit and Practical Tips

China is enormous, so timing depends on the region, but **spring (April–May)** and **autumn (September–October)** are generally most comfortable for sightseeing in Beijing, Shanghai, Xi'an, and along the Great Wall. Northern winters are cold; southern summers are hot and humid.

Practical notes for Pakistani travellers:

- **Go cashless:** set up mobile payments (Alipay, WeChat Pay) and link an international card before you travel — cash and foreign cards are accepted in fewer places than expected.
- **Connectivity:** many familiar apps are restricted; download maps, translation, and a lawful VPN before arrival, and get a roaming or local SIM.
- **Carry your passport:** required for hotels, trains, and many attractions.
- **Language:** English is limited outside major cities; a translation app is essential.
- **Confirm the policy:** re-check the visa-free arrangement close to departure, as it is time-limited to 31 December 2026.

## Application Timeline and Pre-Departure Checklist

With visa-free entry, the timeline is about readiness, not paperwork. No advance visa is needed for a 30-day visit during the policy window. As a rule of thumb, lock your passport validity and bookings first, then complete the visa or entry steps, and keep everything consistent across documents. Plan with a buffer: the main risk is arriving without onward travel, accommodation, or funds proof, so handle that early rather than at the last minute.

Use this quick checklist before you go:

- Confirm the visa-free policy is active for your dates
- Passport valid 6+ months with blank pages
- Return/onward ticket within 30 days
- Accommodation and a basic itinerary ready
- Proof of funds accessible
- Mobile payments set up before you fly

Tick every box and you remove the vast majority of avoidable delays and refusals. When in doubt, confirm the latest rules on the official source before you travel, since fees and procedures can change.

The visa-free window through 2026 is a rare opportunity for Pakistani travellers — take advantage of it, but always re-confirm the policy is still active close to your travel date before booking.
