## What Flight Reservation Is and Why It Matters

A flight reservation (a held booking, not always a paid ticket) shows your intended travel dates. Don't buy non-refundable tickets before approval. Officers use it as one piece of a bigger picture — so what matters most is that it is genuine, legible, and lines up with everything else in your file. A document that contradicts your other paperwork does more harm than no document at all, which is why consistency is the theme running through everything below.

## What to Include

A strong flight reservation contains a reservation showing your name, route, and dates that match your visa application and accommodation. Think of it as answering the officer's unspoken question in advance: it should leave no ambiguity about the facts it's meant to establish. Anything vague, undated, or unsigned (where a signature is expected) weakens it, so aim for a version that is specific, complete, and verifiable.

## The Right Format

Presentation matters more than people expect. Keep your flight reservation clean and professional: official letterhead or issuing authority where relevant, clear dates, legible text, and a certified translation if it isn't in the destination's official language. Provide originals or properly certified copies as required, and never alter a document — even a small "tidy-up" can be read as tampering and is the single most damaging thing you can do.

## A Sample Structure

While exact formats vary by issuer, a reliable flight reservation follows a simple logic: it **identifies** who it concerns, **states the key facts** clearly (dates, amounts, relationships, or specifications as relevant), and is **verifiable** — signed, stamped, dated, or issued by a recognised authority. Lay it out so an officer can grasp the essential facts in seconds, and make sure every detail on it matches your passport, your application form, and your supporting documents.

## How It Fits the Wider File

Your flight reservation never stands alone. Its real job is to **corroborate** the rest of your application — your funds, your ties, your purpose, and your identity. The strongest files are internally consistent: the names, dates, and figures on your flight reservation match those on every other document. Before you submit, lay your documents side by side and check that this one supports, rather than contradicts, the others. That cross-check is the highest-value few minutes you can spend.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Buying a non-refundable ticket before the visa is approved.**
- **Dates that don't match your itinerary.**
- **A reservation that has already expired.**
- **Inconsistencies** with your passport, form, or other documents.
- **Submitting an altered or fabricated version** — an instant, lasting problem.

## How to Get It Right

Start early so you're not chasing a signature or a translation at the last minute. Gather the source information, prepare the document to the correct format, double-check every name, date, and figure against your other paperwork, and keep both originals and clear copies. If a document must come from an employer, bank, host, or authority, request it in good time and review it carefully before you submit.
