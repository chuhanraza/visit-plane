## What "Visa-On-Arrival" Actually Means

A visa-on-arrival lets you obtain your visa at the destination's airport or border on landing, rather than applying in advance at an embassy. You still meet entry conditions — a valid passport, a return ticket, proof of funds, and sometimes a fee — but you skip the advance paperwork.

It's important not to confuse the categories. **Visa-free** means no visa at all. **Visa-on-arrival** means a visa issued at the border. An **e-visa** (or e-Travel Authorisation) means a quick online approval before you fly. Many travellers lump these together, but the documents and steps differ, so always check which one applies.

## What You Still Need at the Border

Even with visa-on-arrival access, entry is never automatic — a border officer makes the final decision. Carry the essentials: a passport valid well beyond your stay, evidence of **onward or return travel**, proof of **funds**, your **accommodation** details, and any required **insurance**. Be ready to answer briefly about your purpose and length of stay. "Visa-On-Arrival" means "no advance visa", not "no questions".

## Visa-Free vs e-Visa vs Visa-on-Arrival

Picture the three "easy" categories on a spectrum of convenience. **Visa-free** is simplest: turn up with your passport, meet the conditions, and you're in. **Visa-on-arrival** adds one step — a short form and a fee at the border. An **e-visa** sits in between: apply online before you fly, get approval within days, then travel with that confirmation. All three beat a full embassy application, but they involve different steps, costs, and lead times — so for each destination, find out which applies to a Nigeria passport.

## Respect the Permitted Stay

One habit protects your visa-on-arrival access more than any other: never overstay. The permitted period — often 30 to 90 days — is a firm limit, and overstaying can mean fines, deportation, or a ban that follows you into future applications. Note your allowed days on arrival, keep proof of your entry date, and plan your departure with a comfortable margin.
