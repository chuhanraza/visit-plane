## Overview

Qatar — Doha's futuristic skyline, the Museum of Islamic Art, desert safaris, and a major airline hub — is highly accessible for Pakistani travellers thanks to the country's **Hayya** digital visa platform. Pakistani passport holders apply **online** for the **Hayya entry visa (A1 tourist)** and receive it electronically — no embassy or consulate visit required.

This guide explains the Hayya A1 process for Pakistani citizens in 2026: eligibility, the **Discover Qatar accommodation** requirement, documents, fees, validity, and tips for a smooth trip. On VisitPlane, we verify every route against official sources.

> **Key takeaway:** Pakistani citizens apply for the **Hayya A1 tourist visa** online (~**QAR 100**). For Pakistani applicants, **booking accommodation through Discover Qatar is typically mandatory** as part of the Hayya A1 application.

## Do Pakistani Citizens Need a Visa for Qatar?

Yes. Unlike some nationalities that receive a visa waiver, Pakistani travellers need to obtain a visa before arrival. The standard tourist route is the **Hayya A1 entry visa**, applied for through the official **Hayya Portal** or the Hayya mobile app. It allows tourism, family, and business visits.

## What Is Hayya?

**Hayya** is Qatar's official entry-permit and services platform (originally launched around major events and now Qatar's general entry system). For tourists, the **A1 visa** is the relevant category. Applying through Hayya means:

- No embassy appointment — everything is **online**.
- A digital permit linked to your passport.
- Integration with **Discover Qatar** for the mandatory accommodation booking.

## The Discover Qatar Accommodation Requirement

A key point for Pakistani applicants: the **Hayya A1** typically requires you to **book accommodation through Discover Qatar** (Qatar's official destination management company) as part of the application. This means your hotel/stay is arranged through the official channel rather than any third-party booking. Factor this into your budget and plans.

> **Key takeaway:** Don't book a random hotel and assume it qualifies — the Hayya A1 for Pakistani travellers generally ties your visa to a **Discover Qatar** accommodation booking.

## Documents and Requirements

For the Hayya A1 tourist visa, Pakistani applicants need:

- A **passport valid for at least 6 months** from your date of entry.
- A clear **passport bio-page** scan and a **photo**.
- **Accommodation booked through Discover Qatar**.
- A **confirmed return/onward flight** ticket.
- **Proof of sufficient funds** for your stay.
- Valid **travel health insurance** (recommended/required).

> **Key takeaway:** Have your onward ticket and funds ready — even with an approved Hayya visa, you should be prepared to satisfy entry checks at Doha.

## How to Apply: Step by Step

1. **Create a Hayya account** on the official portal or app.
2. **Start an A1 tourist visa application** and enter your details.
3. **Book accommodation through Discover Qatar** as prompted.
4. **Upload** your passport and photo.
5. **Pay the fee** (around QAR 100).
6. **Receive your Hayya visa** electronically — save and print it.
7. **Travel**, presenting your Hayya visa and passport at Doha.

## Costs and Fees (2026)

- **Hayya A1 tourist visa fee:** approximately **QAR 100** (around **PKR 7,600**, depending on exchange rate).

## Validity and Length of Stay

The Hayya A1 entry visa is generally valid for up to **30 days** as a **single entry**, and stays can usually be **extended once** for a further 30 days, subject to approval and a fee. You cannot work on a tourist visa. Read your Hayya approval for the exact validity and entry conditions.

## Tips for a Smooth Trip

- **Apply early** and complete the Discover Qatar accommodation step correctly.
- **Carry your onward ticket and funds proof** for arrival.
- **Respect local laws and customs** — Qatar enforces conservative public dress and behaviour, and strict rules on alcohol and prohibited items.
- **Track your 30 days** and extend before they lapse if needed.

## Common Reasons Hayya Applications Are Delayed

Most Hayya A1 issues for Pakistani applicants come down to a few avoidable mistakes:

- **Skipping or mismatching the Discover Qatar booking** — for Pakistani applicants this accommodation step is generally required and must be completed correctly.
- **Passport validity under 6 months** — renew before applying.
- **Poor-quality passport or photo scans** — upload clear, full-page images.
- **No onward ticket or funds proof** — keep these ready for both the application and arrival.
- **Leaving it too late** — apply with enough buffer before your travel date.

Getting the accommodation and document steps right the first time is the single biggest factor in a smooth Hayya approval.

## Best Time to Visit and Practical Tips

Qatar is best enjoyed from **November to March**, when the weather is mild and ideal for the Corniche, desert safaris, and outdoor dining. Summers (June–September) are intensely hot, though indoor attractions, malls, and museums are fully air-conditioned.

Practical notes for Pakistani travellers:

- **Currency:** the Qatari Riyal (QAR); cards are widely accepted, and ATMs are easy to find.
- **Culture and law:** dress modestly in public, respect prayer times, and note strict rules on alcohol (licensed venues only) and prohibited items — penalties are severe.
- **Connectivity:** local SIMs are available on arrival; ride-hailing and taxis are reliable.
- **Stopovers:** Qatar Airways passengers can sometimes add official stopover packages with discounted hotels.
- **Track your 30 days** from the entry date and extend before they lapse if you wish to stay longer.
