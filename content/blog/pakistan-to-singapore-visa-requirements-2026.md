## Overview

Singapore is a top short-haul destination for Pakistani travellers — the futuristic Marina Bay Sands and Gardens by the Bay, Sentosa's beaches and theme parks, world-class shopping, and an exceptionally clean, safe, halal-friendly city. To visit, Pakistani passport holders need a **Singapore visa**, which is applied for through an **authorised visa agent or a local sponsor** rather than an individual online portal.

This guide explains the 2026 process for Pakistani citizens — how to apply, the fee, documents, processing, and approval tips. On VisitPlane, we verify every route against official sources.

> **Key takeaway:** Pakistanis need a **Singapore visa**. Applications are submitted through an **authorised visa agent (a Singapore-authorised strategic partner)** or a local sponsor; the government visa fee is around **SGD 30**, and processing usually takes a few working days.

## Do Pakistani Citizens Need a Visa for Singapore?

Yes. Pakistani passport holders are an "assessment-level" nationality and must obtain a visa before travelling. There is no visa-on-arrival. Unlike a simple individual e-visa, Singapore requires applications to be lodged through an **authorised channel** — a local contact/sponsor or an authorised visa agent.

## How the Visa Works

- Submitted via an **authorised visa agent** or a **local sponsor** in Singapore (a citizen/PR or registered company), who lodges it through the official ICA system.
- Usually a **single or multiple entry** visa valid for several weeks, with stays granted on arrival (commonly up to 30 days).
- Processed in a **few working days** and issued electronically.

> **Key takeaway:** You cannot simply apply on your own at the airport — arrange an **authorised agent or sponsor** before you travel.

## Documents You Need

- A **passport** valid at least **6 months** from arrival, with blank pages.
- A completed **Form 14A** and a recent **photo** (to Singapore specifications).
- A **confirmed return flight** and **hotel booking**.
- **Proof of funds** (bank statements) and, where relevant, an **invitation/sponsor letter**.
- Evidence of **ties to Pakistan** (employment, business, family).

## How to Apply: Step by Step

1. **Arrange an authorised visa agent or local sponsor** in Singapore.
2. **Complete Form 14A** and gather your documents.
3. **Submit through the authorised channel**, which lodges it via ICA.
4. **Pay the visa fee** (around SGD 30) plus any agent service charge.
5. **Wait for processing** (usually a few working days).
6. **Receive the e-visa** and print it for travel.

## Costs and Fees (2026)

- **Government visa fee:** around **SGD 30** (roughly PKR 6,500).
- **Agent service charge:** varies — budget a few thousand rupees more.
- In total, expect roughly **PKR 10,000–18,000** all-in depending on the agent.

Confirm the current amounts before paying; the visa fee is non-refundable.

## Processing Time

Singapore visas are usually decided within **a few working days**, though it can be longer in busy periods or if extra checks apply. Apply **at least a week or two ahead** of travel.

## Visa Validity and Stay

The visa validity (the window to enter) is typically several weeks, and the **length of stay is granted on arrival** by an immigration officer — commonly up to **30 days** for tourists. You cannot work on a tourist visa. Track your permitted stay and don't overstay.

## Why Applications Get Refused (and How to Avoid It)

- **Weak ties to Pakistan** or unclear travel purpose.
- **Insufficient funds** for the trip.
- **Incomplete Form 14A** or poor-quality photo.
- **No return ticket or accommodation** evidence.

The fee is non-refundable, so prepare a complete, consistent file.

## Best Time to Visit and Practical Tips

Singapore is warm and humid year-round; **February–April** is relatively drier. Practical notes for Pakistani travellers:

- **Currency:** the Singapore Dollar (SGD); cards and contactless are used everywhere.
- **Connectivity:** tourist SIMs and eSIMs are easy; the MRT metro is superb and cheap.
- **Getting around:** the MRT and buses reach almost everywhere; the city is very walkable.
- **Food:** halal food is widely available (look for halal-certified hawker stalls); vegetarian options too.
- **Etiquette:** Singapore enforces strict rules — no littering, jaywalking, or chewing-gum import; respect them to avoid fines.

## What You Can and Can't Do on This Visa

A Singapore tourist visa is for **tourism and short visits**. You **can** sightsee and take day trips; you **cannot** work, and you must respect the stay length granted on arrival. Overstaying is treated seriously in Singapore, so leave before your permitted stay ends.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Assuming you can apply alone** without an authorised agent or sponsor.
- **A photo that doesn't meet** Singapore's specifications.
- **Insufficient funds** or weak ties to Pakistan.
- **No return ticket or hotel proof.**
- **Using an unregistered "agent"** instead of an authorised one.
- **Misjudging the budget** — Singapore is one of Asia's pricier cities.
- **Booking non-refundable flights** before approval.

## What to See in Singapore

Most trips centre on **Marina Bay**, where the iconic **Marina Bay Sands**, the Merlion, and the futuristic **Gardens by the Bay** (Supertree Grove and the climate-controlled domes) light up the skyline. The **Singapore Flyer** and a river cruise round out the bay area.

Families and thrill-seekers head to **Sentosa Island** for Universal Studios, S.E.A. Aquarium, and beaches, while culture lovers explore **Chinatown**, **Little India**, and **Kampong Glam** with its Sultan Mosque. The **Singapore Zoo** and **Night Safari** are world-renowned, and **Orchard Road** is a shopper's paradise. Compact and superbly connected, Singapore packs a lot into a short trip — four or five days comfortably covers the highlights, with halal food easy to find throughout.
