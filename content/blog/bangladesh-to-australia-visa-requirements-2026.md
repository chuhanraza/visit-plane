## Overview

Australia is a major long-haul destination for Bangladeshi travellers — Sydney's Opera House and Harbour Bridge, Melbourne's culture, the Great Barrier Reef, and a growing Bangladeshi community that drives family visits year-round. To visit, Bangladeshi passport holders need a **Visitor visa (subclass 600)**, applied for **online** through Australia's **ImmiAccount** — there is no visa-on-arrival.

This guide explains the 2026 process for Bangladeshi citizens — the fee, documents, the Genuine Temporary Entrant test, processing, and approval tips. On VisitPlane, we verify every route against official sources.

> **Key takeaway:** Bangladeshis need a **subclass 600 Visitor visa** for Australia. Apply **online via ImmiAccount**; the charge is around **AUD 200** (Tourist stream, offshore), biometrics are usually required, and processing typically runs **about 20–40 days**.

## Do Bangladeshi Citizens Need a Visa for Australia?

Yes. Bangladesh is not on Australia's ETA or eVisitor list, so Bangladeshi passport holders must apply for a **subclass 600 Visitor visa** before travelling. It covers **tourism, visiting family or friends, and short business activities**, for stays of **3, 6, or 12 months** depending on your circumstances.

## How the Subclass 600 Visa Works

- Applied for **online** via a free **ImmiAccount**.
- The **Tourist stream** is the usual choice for holidays and family visits.
- Decided on the **Genuine Temporary Entrant (GTE)** requirement — you must intend to visit temporarily and leave before it expires.
- Linked **electronically** to your passport once granted.

> **Key takeaway:** The **GTE test** is central — your job, finances, family, and ties to Bangladesh must show you'll return home after the visit.

## Documents You Need

- A **passport** valid for the duration of your intended stay.
- A recent **photo** and completed online application.
- **Proof of funds** — bank statements (3–6 months), salary slips, tax documents, or business papers.
- **Evidence of ties to Bangladesh** — employment letter, business ownership, property, family.
- A **travel itinerary** and, if visiting family, an **invitation letter** with the host's details and status in Australia.
- **Biometrics** at a VFS Global Australia centre when requested.

## How to Apply: Step by Step

1. **Create an ImmiAccount** on the Department of Home Affairs website.
2. **Complete the subclass 600 application** (Tourist stream) and upload documents.
3. **Pay the application charge** online by card.
4. **Provide biometrics** at the VFS Global centre in Bangladesh if requested.
5. **Attend a health examination** if asked.
6. **Wait for the decision**, delivered electronically and linked to your passport.

## Costs and Fees (2026)

- **Visa application charge:** around **AUD 200** for the Tourist stream (offshore).
- **Biometrics and VFS service fees** are additional.
- In taka, the base charge is roughly **BDT 16,000**, before service and biometric fees.

Fees are **non-refundable** even if the visa is refused, so prepare carefully. Confirm the current charge on the official site before paying.

## Processing Time

The Tourist stream typically processes in about **20–40 days**, varying with your documents and demand. Apply **at least 6–8 weeks ahead** of travel to allow for biometrics, any health checks, and requests for more information.

## Visa Validity and Stay

A subclass 600 visa allows stays of **3, 6, or 12 months** per entry, as specified on your grant, and can be single or multiple entry. You **cannot work** on it, and study is limited to short courses. Always check your grant conditions and don't overstay.

## Why Applications Get Refused (and How to Avoid It)

- **Weak ties to Bangladesh** — no clear reason to return.
- **Insufficient or unexplained funds.**
- **Thin or generic invitation** letters.
- **Inconsistent itinerary** or purpose.
- **Incomplete forms** or missing documents.

Address each pillar directly so the non-refundable fee isn't wasted.

## How Much Will the Whole Trip Cost?

Rough 2026 estimates per person for a Bangladeshi traveller:

- **Visa:** ~BDT 16,000+ including biometrics and service fees.
- **Return flights (Bangladesh–Australia):** roughly BDT 130,000–230,000 depending on season and routing.
- **Accommodation:** BDT 12,000–30,000 per night in Sydney/Melbourne (or stay with family).

A typical 2-week Australia trip can land around **BDT 4–8 lakh per person**, with long-haul flights the biggest factor.

## Best Time to Visit and Practical Tips

Australia's seasons are reversed: its **summer is December–February** (great for beaches) and **winter is June–August**. Spring and autumn are mild and ideal. Practical notes for Bangladeshi travellers:

- **Currency:** the Australian Dollar (AUD); cards and contactless are used everywhere.
- **Connectivity:** prepaid SIMs and eSIMs are easy at airports and stores.
- **Getting around:** domestic flights cover long distances; cities have good public transport.
- **Food:** halal food is widely available in major cities, with South Asian communities and restaurants.
- **Etiquette:** Australians are casual and friendly; tipping is optional.

## What You Can and Can't Do on This Visa

The subclass 600 is a **temporary visitor visa** for tourism, family visits, and short business activities. You **can** sightsee, visit relatives, and attend meetings; you **cannot** work, and study is limited to short courses. Respect the stay length on your grant — overstaying harms future applications and can trigger bans.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Booking non-refundable flights** before the visa is granted.
- **Underfunded or unexplained** bank statements.
- **A weak GTE case** — failing to show ties to Bangladesh.
- **Generic invitation letters** without the host's full details.
- **Leaving requested documents** or health checks until the last minute.

## What to See in Australia

Most trips start in **Sydney**, where the **Opera House**, **Harbour Bridge**, Bondi Beach, and harbour ferries define the skyline. **Melbourne** counters with laneway cafés, sport, and the scenic **Great Ocean Road**. Up north, **Queensland** delivers the **Great Barrier Reef** (Cairns and the Whitsundays) and rainforest, while **Uluru** in the Red Centre offers an unforgettable desert and cultural experience. **Brisbane** and the **Gold Coast** add theme parks and surf beaches. Distances are large, so plan domestic flights and focus on one or two regions per trip; halal food and established communities make every major city comfortable.

## How to Strengthen Your Australia Application

Whether your route is straightforward or strict, the same principles separate smooth approvals from frustrating delays — and they are worth understanding before you apply. At VisitPlane, we've mapped these patterns across dozens of visa routes, and the advice below applies directly to Bangladeshi travellers heading to Australia, where the Genuine Temporary Entrant test sits at the centre of the decision.

Start with your **finances**, the single most scrutinised part of almost any application. Officers check that your funds are *genuine and stable*, not just present. A balance that has sat comfortably for three to six months is far more convincing than a large sum that appeared just before you applied. If a relative in Australia is supporting your trip, document that relationship and their status clearly.

Next, ensure **consistency** — your itinerary, invitation, accommodation, and statements should tell one coherent story. Mismatches invite questions and slow the decision. Because subclass 600 turns on intent, **ties to Bangladesh** matter enormously: stable employment, a business, property, or dependents reassure the officer you'll return.

Finally, be **honest and complete** — declare any previous refusal truthfully, and respond to any request promptly.
