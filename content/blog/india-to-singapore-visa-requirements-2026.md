## Overview

Singapore — with Marina Bay, Sentosa, Gardens by the Bay, and one of the world's best airports — is a perennial favourite for Indian travellers, whether for a short city break, a family holiday, or a business trip. Unlike some Southeast Asian neighbours, Singapore does **not** offer visa-free entry or visa on arrival to Indian passport holders. You must obtain a visa **before** you travel.

The good news: Singapore's visa is issued entirely electronically as an **e-Visa**, with **no interview and no biometrics**. The slight catch is that Indian applicants cannot apply directly to the government on their own — applications must be submitted through an **authorised agent / Strategic Partner**, or by a **local sponsor** in Singapore through the Immigration & Checkpoints Authority (ICA) e-Service. This guide explains both routes, the documents you need, the real costs, and how to maximise approval. On VisitPlane, we verify every route against official sources.

> **Key takeaway:** Indians need a visa for Singapore. It is an **e-Visa** (no interview, no biometrics) submitted via an **authorised agent** or a **local sponsor** through ICA — you cannot submit it directly to ICA as an individual overseas applicant.

## Do Indian Citizens Need a Visa for Singapore?

Yes. Indian passport holders are an "assessment-required" nationality and must hold a valid Singapore visa before arrival. The standard option for tourism and family visits is the **short-term visit e-Visa**.

There is one important convenience to know about: if you already hold a **valid visa or long-term pass for the USA, UK, Canada, Australia, Germany, Switzerland, or New Zealand**, you may be eligible for the **Visa-Free Transit Facility (VFTF)** for short transits through Singapore — but this is limited to transit, not a general tourist stay. For a normal holiday, you need the e-Visa.

## Who Can Submit Your Application?

This is the part that confuses many first-time applicants. An Indian tourist cannot simply log in to ICA and submit their own visa application from India. Your application must be lodged by one of the following:

- An **authorised visa agent / Strategic Partner** (for example, large travel agencies and visa service providers).
- A **local contact in Singapore** — a Singapore citizen or Permanent Resident (friend, relative, or business associate) who submits your application through the **ICA e-Service**.
- A **registered Singapore company** sponsoring a business visit.

If you have a relative or friend who is a Singapore citizen or PR, having them sponsor your application through ICA is the cheapest route — you pay only the government fee.

> **Key takeaway:** The fastest path for most tourists without a Singapore contact is a reputable authorised agent. The cheapest path, if you have one, is a **local sponsor** filing through ICA.

## Documents You Need

For a short-term visit e-Visa, Indian applicants should prepare:

- A **passport valid for at least 6 months** from your date of travel, with blank pages.
- A recent **passport-size colour photograph** (taken within the last 3 months, white background, meeting Singapore's specifications).
- A completed **Form 14A** (the visa application form), usually handled by your agent or sponsor.
- A copy of your passport bio-data page.
- **Confirmed return/onward flight** details and **accommodation** booking (or your host's details).
- **Proof of funds** — recent bank statements showing you can support your stay.
- If sponsored, a **Letter of Introduction (LOI)** from your local contact or company.

Requirements can vary slightly by agent; confirm the exact list before paying.

## How to Apply: Step by Step

1. **Choose your route** — authorised agent, local sponsor via ICA, or company sponsor.
2. **Gather documents** — passport, photo, Form 14A, flights, accommodation, and funds proof.
3. **Submit the application** through your agent or sponsor's ICA e-Service account.
4. **Pay the fees** — the government visa fee plus any agent service charge.
5. **Wait for processing** — typically **3–5 working days**.
6. **Receive your e-Visa** — it is emailed to you as a PDF. **Print a copy** to carry with your passport; immigration may ask to see it on arrival.

## Costs and Fees (2026)

- **Government visa fee:** approximately **SGD 30** (around ₹1,900–₹2,100).
- **Agent service charge:** if you apply through an authorised agent, expect a total of roughly **₹2,500–₹4,500** including the government fee, depending on the provider and any add-ons.
- **Local sponsor via ICA:** you pay only the SGD 30 government fee.

Fees change periodically and vary by agent, so confirm the all-in price before you commit.

## Processing Time and Validity

Most Singapore e-Visas for Indian applicants are processed within **3–5 working days**, though it is wise to apply at least **2 weeks** before travel during peak seasons. The visa is typically issued with a validity ranging from a few weeks up to **2 years** (multiple-entry visas are granted at ICA's discretion), but the **maximum stay per visit is decided by the immigration officer on arrival** — commonly up to **30 days**.

> **Key takeaway:** Your e-Visa validity is not the same as your permitted length of stay. The officer at the checkpoint grants the actual stay (often up to 30 days) via the entry endorsement.

## Tourism vs Business Visits

The short-term visit e-Visa covers both tourism and short business trips, but the supporting evidence differs. For **tourism**, focus on your itinerary, accommodation, return flights, and personal funds. For a **business visit** — meetings, conferences, or negotiations (not paid employment) — you will usually need a **Letter of Introduction (LOI)** from the inviting Singapore company, details of the event or meetings, and sometimes a covering letter from your employer in India. Be clear and consistent about which purpose you are applying under, because mismatched documents are a common cause of delay.

## Multiple-Entry Visas Explained

Singapore may issue either a **single-entry** or a **multiple-entry** visa, at ICA's discretion. A multiple-entry visa lets you enter Singapore several times within its validity (which can run up to two years), which is convenient if you travel through Changi often or combine Singapore with regional trips. You don't choose this directly — ICA decides based on your profile, travel history, and the documents submitted. A clean travel history and strong financials improve your chances of a longer multiple-entry grant.

Remember the key distinction: **validity** is how long the visa can be used to seek entry; **length of stay** is what the officer grants at the checkpoint each time you arrive (commonly up to 30 days).

## Transiting Through Changi Airport

If you are only **transiting** through Singapore — not leaving the transit area — you may not need a visa at all. In addition, Indian nationals who hold a **valid visa or long-term pass for the USA, UK, Canada, Australia, Germany, Switzerland, or New Zealand** may qualify for the **Visa-Free Transit Facility (VFTF)**, allowing a short transit stay (typically up to 96 hours) without a Singapore visa, subject to conditions. This is strictly for transit — if you want to enter Singapore for a holiday, you still need the e-Visa.

## Tips to Maximise Approval

- **Apply through a reputable agent or a genuine sponsor** — avoid unverified "instant visa" sellers.
- **Show stable funds** across recent statements, not a sudden deposit.
- **Provide a clear itinerary** with confirmed return travel and accommodation.
- **Match your stated purpose** (tourism vs. business) to your documents.
- **Carry a printed e-Visa** and your supporting documents at the checkpoint.

## What Happens at the Singapore Checkpoint

Holding an e-Visa does not guarantee entry — it allows you to *travel to* Singapore and seek entry. At the checkpoint, the immigration officer reviews your passport, e-Visa, and may ask about your trip, accommodation, funds, and return plans. Indian travellers should be ready to present:

- A **printed copy of the e-Visa** and passport (valid 6+ months).
- **Return or onward tickets** and **accommodation** confirmation.
- Evidence of **sufficient funds** for the stay.

Singapore also uses an electronic arrival card system — submit the **SG Arrival Card** online within the permitted window before arrival (separate from your visa). Once cleared, the officer endorses your **length of stay**, commonly up to 30 days. Keep your belongings declaration accurate; Singapore enforces strict customs and security rules, and penalties for prohibited items are severe.

## Best Time to Visit and Practical Tips

Singapore is a year-round city destination with a tropical climate; **February to April** tends to be slightly drier. The currency is the Singapore Dollar (SGD), cards and contactless payments are accepted almost everywhere, and English is an official language — making it one of the smoothest first international trips for Indian families and solo travellers alike.
