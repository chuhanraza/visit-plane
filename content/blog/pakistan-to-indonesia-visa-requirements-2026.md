## Overview

Indonesia — Bali's beaches and temples, the islands of Lombok and the Gili chain, and the culture of Yogyakarta — is a dream destination for Pakistani travellers. The key thing to know in 2026: **Pakistani passport holders are not eligible for the standard visa on arrival** that many other nationalities enjoy. Instead, you apply for either the **B1 e-VOA (electronic visa on arrival)** online before travel, or a **sponsored C1 tourist visa** (formerly B211A) for longer stays.

This guide explains both routes for Pakistani citizens — fees, validity, the Bali tourist levy, and the step-by-step process. On VisitPlane, we verify every route against official sources.

> **Key takeaway:** Pakistanis are **not** on Indonesia's visa-on-arrival list. The simplest route is the **B1 e-VOA (IDR 500,000, ~USD 35)** applied for online; for longer stays, use the sponsor-based **C1 tourist visa**.

## Do Pakistani Citizens Need a Visa for Indonesia?

Yes. Pakistan does not have a visa-exemption or visa-on-arrival agreement with Indonesia, so you cannot simply turn up and buy a VoA at the counter. Pakistani travellers have two main options:

1. **B1 e-VOA** — the electronic visa on arrival, applied for **online before travel**. Best for short holidays.
2. **C1 tourist visa** (previously **B211A**) — applied for with an **Indonesian sponsor**, for longer stays.

## Option 1: B1 e-VOA (Recommended for Short Trips)

The **B1 e-VOA** is the most straightforward route for a Bali or Indonesia holiday:

- Applied for **online** before you fly (allow a few days; the document is valid for **90 days from issue** within which you must enter).
- **Single entry**, granting a **30-day** stay on arrival.
- **Extendable once** for a further 30 days.
- Fee: **IDR 500,000** (about **USD 35**).

This is the closest equivalent to the VoA that other nationalities get — but because Pakistanis aren't on the VoA list, you must obtain it as an **e-VOA in advance**.

## Option 2: C1 Tourist Visa (Sponsored, Longer Stays)

For longer or more flexible visits, the **C1 tourist visa** is required:

- Must be applied for via an **Indonesian sponsor** — a family member, friend, or an authorised legal entity/agency in Indonesia.
- **Single entry**, valid for **60 days**, and **extendable up to two times** (each for 60 days), allowing a maximum stay of around **180 days**.
- Requires more documentation and a sponsor's involvement.

Choose C1 if you plan to stay beyond what the e-VOA allows.

> **Key takeaway:** For a 1–2 week Bali trip, the **B1 e-VOA** is simplest. Only use the **C1** if you need to stay longer than ~60 days or your trip requires a sponsor.

## The Bali Tourist Levy

Separately from any visa, Bali charges a **mandatory tourist levy of IDR 150,000** (about USD 10) **per person, per entry**, for all international visitors. Pay it online via Bali's official Love Bali platform (or on arrival) and **keep the receipt/QR** to show if asked. This applies to Bali specifically.

## Documents and Requirements

For the B1 e-VOA, Pakistani travellers need:

- A **passport valid for at least 6 months** from arrival, with at least one blank page.
- A **return or onward flight** booking.
- **Accommodation** proof.
- Means to pay the **e-VOA fee** and **Bali levy**.
- Sufficient **funds** for your stay.

The C1 visa additionally requires sponsor documents and may ask for bank statements and a covering letter.

## How to Apply (B1 e-VOA): Step by Step

1. **Apply online** on Indonesia's official immigration e-VOA portal a few days before travel.
2. **Upload** your passport bio-page and photo, and enter travel details.
3. **Pay** the IDR 500,000 fee online.
4. **Receive approval** by email — save and print it.
5. **Pay the Bali tourist levy** (if visiting Bali) and keep the receipt.
6. **Arrive in Indonesia**, present your e-VOA and passport, and clear immigration.

## Costs (2026)

- **B1 e-VOA:** **IDR 500,000** (~USD 35), 30 days, single entry.
- **Bali tourist levy:** **IDR 150,000** (~USD 10) per person, per entry (Bali only).
- **C1 tourist visa:** higher government fee plus any sponsor/agency charge.
- **Extension:** additional fee per extension.

## Validity, Stay and Extensions

The B1 e-VOA gives **30 days**, extendable once for another 30. The C1 gives **60 days**, extendable twice. Apply for any extension **before** your current permission expires — Indonesia charges **daily overstay fines**. You cannot work on either tourist route.

## Tips for a Smooth Trip

- **Apply for the e-VOA early** — don't assume a counter VoA, which Pakistanis can't use.
- **Pre-pay the Bali levy** and keep the receipt.
- **Carry onward-ticket and accommodation proof** for immigration.
- **Don't overstay** — daily fines apply.
- **Respect local customs**, especially at temples and religious sites.

## What You Can and Can't Do on These Visas

Both the B1 e-VOA and the C1 tourist visa are for **tourism and short visits**. You can sightsee, enjoy the beaches, visit temples, and island-hop. You **cannot** take up employment, run a business, or do paid work — including remote work that breaches the tourist purpose. For work or long-term stays, a different permit is required, arranged through a sponsor or employer.

Overstaying is penalised with a **daily fine**, and serious overstays can lead to detention and deportation. Track your permitted days from the arrival stamp and extend in good time. Because Pakistanis can't use the on-arrival VoA, never plan to "sort it at the airport" — secure your e-VOA or C1 before you fly.

## Best Time to Visit and Practical Tips

Bali and much of Indonesia enjoy a **dry season (roughly April–October)** ideal for beaches and outdoor activities, with a wetter season from November to March. Mid-year and year-end holidays are the busiest.

Practical notes for Pakistani travellers:

- **Currency:** the Indonesian Rupiah (IDR); cards work in hotels and larger venues, but carry cash for warungs, markets, and small towns.
- **Pre-pay the Bali levy:** complete it online via Love Bali and keep the QR/receipt.
- **Transport:** ride-hailing apps (Gojek, Grab) are widely used; rent scooters only with a valid licence and care.
- **Respect customs:** dress modestly at temples (a sarong is often required) and follow etiquette at religious sites.
- **Apply ahead:** process your B1 e-VOA online a few days before travel — it's valid for 90 days from issue, so you have flexibility to apply early.

## Application Timeline and Pre-Departure Checklist

Apply for your e-VOA a few days before departure — it stays valid for 90 days from issue. e-VOA approval usually comes within a few business days. As a rule of thumb, lock your passport validity and bookings first, then complete the visa or entry steps, and keep everything consistent across documents. Plan with a buffer: the most common mistake is assuming Pakistanis can buy a VoA on arrival (they cannot), so handle that early rather than at the last minute.

Use this quick checklist before you go:

- B1 e-VOA applied for online before travel
- Bali tourist levy paid (keep the receipt)
- Passport valid 6+ months with a blank page
- Return/onward ticket booked
- Accommodation confirmed
- Track your 30 days and extend in time if needed

Tick every box and you remove the vast majority of avoidable delays and refusals. When in doubt, confirm the latest rules on the official source before you travel, since fees and procedures can change.
