## Overview

The UK and Australia both attract large numbers of Pakistani students — English-speaking, globally ranked, and with strong post-study work options. They differ on course length, upfront visa cost, climate and lifestyle, and how their post-study and migration routes work.

This guide compares the UK and Australia head-to-head for Pakistani students in 2026 so you can match the choice to your goals and budget. On VisitPlane, we verify every route against official sources.

> **Key takeaway:** Choose the **UK** for a **fast one-year master's** and a predictable, document-based visa. Choose **Australia** for **lifestyle, strong graduate employability, and a points-based migration system** — accepting a much higher upfront visa cost (around AUD 2,000).

## Tuition and Course Length

The **UK** offers **one-year master's degrees**, reducing both tuition years and living costs. Bachelor's degrees run three years.

**Australia** master's degrees usually run **1.5–2 years**, with **higher tuition**, balanced by strong employability in many fields and a lifestyle many students rate highly.

For the lowest predictable total cost, the UK's one-year model wins; Australia competes on outcomes and lifestyle.

## Visa Cost and Requirements

The **UK Student visa** needs the **£558 fee** plus the **IHS at £776/year**, a CAS, 28-day funds, and a **mandatory TB test** for Pakistani applicants.

The **Australia Student visa (subclass 500)** carries a much higher **application charge of around AUD 2,000**, plus **OSHC** health cover, evidence of living costs (benchmark around **AUD 29,710/year**), and the **Genuine Student** requirement.

So Australia's upfront cost is notably higher; the UK front-loads cost through the IHS but is otherwise predictable.

## Work Rights While Studying

- **UK:** generally up to **20 hours per week** in term time.
- **Australia:** up to **48 hours per fortnight** during term, unlimited in breaks.

Broadly comparable part-time earning potential in both.

## Post-Study Work

- The **UK Graduate Route** gives **2 years** of open work (3 for PhDs), with onward settlement via a skilled-worker visa.
- **Australia's Temporary Graduate visa (subclass 485)** lets eligible graduates stay and work, feeding a **points-tested skilled migration** system that rewards in-demand occupations.

Both are genuine post-study routes; Australia's onward migration is occupation- and points-driven, the UK's depends on landing an eligible skilled job.

## Which Is Right for You?

Pick the **UK** for a **fast, lower-total-cost qualification** and a predictable visa.

Pick **Australia** for **lifestyle, climate, and graduate employability**, if your field aligns with in-demand occupations and you can absorb the higher upfront cost.

## How to Decide: A Simple Framework

Weigh three factors. **Budget:** the UK is cheaper overall thanks to the one-year model; Australia costs more upfront. **Migration goal:** if your occupation is in demand in Australia, its points system can reward you; the UK route depends on a skilled job offer. **Lifestyle:** climate, city, and community fit matter for a multi-year stay — be honest about what you'll enjoy.

## Costs Beyond Tuition

Both have expensive student cities (London; Sydney/Melbourne). Budget accommodation, the IHS (UK) or OSHC (Australia), flights, the TB test (UK), and setup. Compare your **specific city and programme**, since costs vary widely within each country.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Underestimating Australia's upfront visa cost** (around AUD 2,000).
- **Underestimating the UK IHS** across a multi-year course.
- **Missing the TB test** (UK) or **OSHC** (Australia).
- **A weak Genuine Student statement** for Australia.
- **Choosing on lifestyle alone** without checking migration fit.

## Head-to-Head Scorecard

Here's how the two stack up dimension by dimension for Pakistani students:

- **Course length:** UK wins (one-year master's).
- **Total cost:** UK often lower thanks to the one-year model.
- **Upfront visa cost:** UK wins (Australia's ~AUD 2,000 charge is steep).
- **Mandatory extras:** UK needs a TB test; Australia needs OSHC and a Genuine Student statement.
- **Work while studying:** broadly equal.
- **Post-study work:** UK Graduate Route (2 years) vs Australia's 485 — comparable, occupation-driven in Australia.
- **Migration pathway:** Australia's points system rewards in-demand occupations; the UK needs a skilled job.
- **Climate & lifestyle:** Australia wins for most students.

The UK is the fast, lower-cost, predictable route; Australia wins on lifestyle and occupation-led migration.

## Verdict by Scenario

**You want a fast, affordable master's:** the UK's one-year model minimises tuition years and the upfront visa cost.

**Your occupation is in demand in Australia and you value lifestyle:** Australia's points-tested migration and climate are strong draws, despite the higher upfront cost.

**You're cost-constrained upfront:** the UK's fee structure beats Australia's ~AUD 2,000 charge, though the multi-year IHS adds up on longer courses.

**You prioritise weather and outdoor life:** Australia is the obvious pick.

Match the choice to whether **speed-and-cost (UK)** or **lifestyle-and-occupation-fit (Australia)** matters more.

## Cost Snapshot (2026)

Rough first-year figures for a Pakistani student:

- **Australia:** visa ~AUD 2,000, OSHC ~AUD 600–1,000/year, tuition roughly AUD 20,000–45,000/year, living-cost benchmark ~AUD 29,710/year.

The UK's one-year master's contains the total despite high annual costs; Australia carries a steep upfront visa charge and higher tuition, balanced by lifestyle and graduate outcomes. Compare your specific programme and city, and weigh the total cost against your migration goals.

## The Bottom Line

For Pakistani students, the UK and Australia both deliver respected degrees and real post-study opportunities — the right pick depends on your budget and your migration goals. The UK's one-year master's keeps the total cost and timeline tight, with a predictable, document-based visa (plus the mandatory TB test). Australia asks for a much larger upfront commitment — the roughly AUD 2,000 visa charge, OSHC, and higher tuition — but rewards it with lifestyle, climate, and a points-tested migration system that favours in-demand occupations.

Ask yourself whether you're optimising for speed and cost (UK) or for lifestyle and occupation-led settlement (Australia). Then prepare accordingly: season your finances, complete the UK TB test or arrange Australian OSHC and a Genuine Student statement, line up your academic and English documents, and apply well ahead of your intake. Both routes serve a motivated Pakistani student well — make the decision on fit, not prestige, and commit with confidence.

## Long-Term Outlook: Careers and Staying On

For Pakistani students weighing the long game, the two systems work differently. The UK's Graduate Route offers two years of open work; settlement then depends on moving into a Skilled Worker role that meets salary and eligibility rules — quick for those who secure a sponsoring employer. Australia's Temporary Graduate visa feeds a points-tested skilled-migration system that rewards in-demand occupations; if your field is on the relevant skills lists, the path can move efficiently, but a non-listed profile can find it competitive.

Your field and the timing of policy both matter. Research the current Australian skilled-occupation lists and the UK's salary thresholds before committing, since both change. Plan from day one: pick a programme and city that strengthen your post-study options, gain relevant experience, and keep documentation immaculate. If your occupation is in demand in Australia and you value lifestyle, its system can reward you; if you want a fast credential and flexible work with a clearer salaried route, the UK fits. The graduates who stay successfully are those who treated study as the first move in a researched, deliberate plan — not a decision made on prestige alone.
