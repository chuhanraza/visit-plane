## Overview

Portugal has become a favourite European destination for Indian travellers — Lisbon's hills and trams, Porto's riverside and port wine, the beaches of the Algarve, and a relaxed, affordable feel compared with much of Western Europe. To visit, Indian passport holders need a **Schengen short-stay visa (Type C)**, and the cost is the standard Schengen fee plus the usual service charges.

This guide breaks down exactly what an Indian applicant pays for a Portugal Schengen visa in 2026 so you can budget accurately. On VisitPlane, we verify every route against official sources.

> **Key takeaway:** A Portugal **Schengen visa** costs **€90** (about **₹8,500**) in 2026. Add the VFS service charge, insurance, and biometrics, and most Indian applicants spend **₹11,000–₹13,000** all-in before flights and hotels.

## The Headline Visa Fee (2026)

For a Portugal **short-stay Schengen visa**:

- **Adult fee:** **€90** (raised from €80 on 11 June 2026).
- **Children 6–11:** **€45**.
- **Under 6:** free.

In rupees, €90 is roughly **₹8,500**, depending on the exchange rate at payment.

## The Full Cost Breakdown

A realistic Indian applicant's budget includes:

- **Schengen visa fee:** ~₹8,500.
- **VFS Global service charge:** around ₹2,000.
- **Travel medical insurance (≥ €30,000):** ₹1,500–₹3,000.
- **Photographs and document prep:** minimal.
- **Biometrics:** included in the appointment; premium slots cost extra.

Add these up and the **all-in cost is typically ₹11,000–₹13,000** per adult.

## What the Visa Covers

A Portugal Schengen visa isn't just for Portugal — it's valid across the entire **29-country Schengen area**, allowing up to **90 days within any 180-day period**. Apply to Portugal when it's your **main destination** (most nights) or your first point of entry. This makes it great value if you're combining Portugal with Spain or other European stops.

## Optional Extras That Add to the Cost

- **VFS premium / prime-time appointments:** additional convenience charges.
- **Courier return** of your passport: a small add-on.
- **Photo and photocopy services** at the centre: minor.

These are optional — the standard service works for most travellers who apply early.

## How to Pay

You pay the visa fee and VFS service charge at or before your appointment, by card. The **visa fee is non-refundable** even if your application is refused, so prepare your documents carefully before paying.

## What the Whole Trip Costs

Beyond the visa, budget for the trip:

- **Return flights (India–Portugal):** roughly ₹45,000–₹95,000 depending on season.
- **Accommodation:** ₹5,000–₹14,000 per night in Lisbon or Porto (Portugal is more affordable than much of Western Europe).
- **Daily spend (food, transport, attractions):** ₹3,500–₹8,000 per person.
- **Insurance:** ₹1,500–₹3,000.

A typical 7-day Portugal trip can land around **₹1.3–2.5 lakh per person**, with flights and hotels the main variables — generally cheaper than France, Italy, or Switzerland.

## Ways to Keep the Cost Down

- **Skip premium VFS services** unless you're short on time.
- **Travel off-peak** (spring/autumn) for cheaper flights and hotels.
- **Combine Portugal with Spain** on one Schengen visa.
- **Book refundable** flights/hotels until the visa is granted.

## How Portugal Compares

For Indian travellers, the Portugal Schengen visa costs the **same €90 as any Schengen country**, but Portugal itself is one of the **most affordable** Western European destinations once you're there — so your overall trip budget often stretches further than in France, Italy, or Switzerland.

## Documents That Justify the Spend

Because the fee is non-refundable, build a strong application: a passport valid 3+ months beyond return with two blank pages, travel insurance of at least €30,000, flight and hotel reservations, bank statements over 3–6 months, a cover letter, and proof of ties to India. ## What's Refundable — and What Isn't

The **Schengen visa fee is non-refundable** once you apply, whether approved or refused, and VFS service charges are too. The smart order of operations is to secure **refundable flights and hotels** first, submit a strong application, and only convert to non-refundable bookings once the visa is granted — protecting the much larger trip budget.

## Sample Budgets

To make the numbers concrete, here are two realistic all-in budgets for an Indian traveller (visa plus the trip):

- **City break (6 nights, Lisbon + Porto):** visa ~₹12,000 + flights ~₹55,000 + hotels ~₹60,000 + daily spend ~₹35,000 = roughly **₹1.6–1.8 lakh per person**.
- **Two-week tour (Lisbon, Porto, Algarve):** visa ~₹12,000 + flights ~₹65,000 + hotels ~₹1.1 lakh + transport and daily spend ~₹70,000 = roughly **₹2.5–3 lakh per person**.

Portugal's lower prices mean your daily budget stretches noticeably further than in France, Italy, or Switzerland — a real advantage once you're past the fixed Schengen visa cost.

## Currency and Payment Tips

Portugal uses the **Euro (€)**. Cards and contactless are accepted almost everywhere, with cash handy for small cafés and markets. Pay the visa fee through the official VFS channel only. For the trip, a forex card plus a low-foreign-fee card works well. Watch the **EUR/INR rate** when you pay the €90 fee, since the rupee cost moves with it. Tipping is modest (round up or 5–10%), and Portugal is one of the better-value Eurozone countries for dining and transport.

Whether your route is straightforward or strict, the same principles separate smooth approvals from frustrating delays — and they protect the money you spend. Start with your **finances**, the single most scrutinised part of almost any application. Officers check that your funds are *genuine and stable*, not just present. A balance that has sat comfortably for three to six months is far more convincing than a large sum that appeared just before you applied. ## Processing Time and How It Affects Planning

Budgeting isn't only about money — timing matters, because a delayed visa can force costly last-minute bookings. A Portugal Schengen visa follows the standard **15-day legal window**, extendable to 30–45 days, and can take longer in peak summer when demand across Schengen consulates surges. The VFS **appointment slot** itself is often the real bottleneck in busy months.

The practical implication for your wallet: **apply early and keep travel bookings refundable** until the visa is granted, so a slow decision never forces you into pricey rebookings. For a summer trip, aim to submit 6–8 weeks ahead and book your VFS appointment as soon as slots open. Premium VFS services can speed up appointment handling and courier return, but they don't shorten the consular decision — so the most reliable money-saver is simply applying with time to spare and a complete file that won't trigger requests for more documents. This habit protects far more than any saving on the visa fee.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Looking only at the €90 fee** and forgetting service and insurance costs.
- **Applying to Portugal** when most nights are elsewhere.
- **Thin or freshly funded** bank statements that look arranged for the trip.
- **Insurance below €30,000.**
- **Leaving the VFS appointment booking** until the last minute in summer.
- **Booking non-refundable flights** before approval.
