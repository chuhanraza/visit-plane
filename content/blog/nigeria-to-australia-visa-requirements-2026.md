## Overview

Australia is a sought-after long-haul destination for Nigerian travellers — Sydney's Opera House and Harbour Bridge, Melbourne's culture, the Great Barrier Reef, and a growing Nigerian diaspora that drives family visits. To visit, Nigerian passport holders need a **Visitor visa (subclass 600)**, applied for **online** through Australia's **ImmiAccount** — there is no visa-on-arrival.

This guide explains the 2026 process for Nigerian citizens — the fee, documents, the Genuine Temporary Entrant test, processing, and approval tips. On VisitPlane, we verify every route against official sources.

> **Key takeaway:** Nigerians need a **subclass 600 Visitor visa** for Australia. Apply **online via ImmiAccount**; the charge is around **AUD 200** (Tourist stream, offshore), biometrics are usually required, and processing typically runs **about 20–45 days**.

## Do Nigerian Citizens Need a Visa for Australia?

Yes. Nigeria is not on Australia's ETA or eVisitor list, so Nigerian passport holders must apply for a **subclass 600 Visitor visa** before travelling. It covers **tourism, visiting family or friends, and short business activities**, for stays of **3, 6, or 12 months** depending on your circumstances.

## How the Subclass 600 Visa Works

- Applied for **online** via a free **ImmiAccount**.
- The **Tourist stream** is the usual choice for holidays and family visits.
- Decided on the **Genuine Temporary Entrant (GTE)** requirement — you must intend to visit temporarily and leave before it expires.
- Linked **electronically** to your passport once granted.

> **Key takeaway:** The **GTE test** is central — your job, finances, family, and ties to Nigeria must show you'll return home after the visit.

## Documents You Need

- A **passport** valid for the duration of your intended stay.
- A recent **photo** and completed online application.
- **Proof of funds** — bank statements (3–6 months), salary slips, tax documents, or business papers.
- **Evidence of ties to Nigeria** — employment letter, business ownership, property, family.
- A **travel itinerary** and, if visiting family, an **invitation letter** with the host's details and status in Australia.
- **Biometrics** at a VFS Global Australia centre when requested.

## How to Apply: Step by Step

1. **Create an ImmiAccount** on the Department of Home Affairs website.
2. **Complete the subclass 600 application** (Tourist stream) and upload documents.
3. **Pay the application charge** online by card.
4. **Provide biometrics** at the VFS Global centre in Nigeria if requested.
5. **Attend a health examination** if asked.
6. **Wait for the decision**, delivered electronically and linked to your passport.

## Costs and Fees (2026)

- **Visa application charge:** around **AUD 200** for the Tourist stream (offshore).
- **Biometrics and VFS service fees** are additional.
- Confirm the current charge — and convert it to Naira at the day's rate — before paying.

Fees are **non-refundable** even if the visa is refused, so prepare carefully.

## Processing Time

The Tourist stream typically processes in about **20–45 days**, varying with your documents and demand. Apply **at least 6–8 weeks ahead** of travel to allow for biometrics, any health checks, and requests for more information.

## Visa Validity and Stay

A subclass 600 visa allows stays of **3, 6, or 12 months** per entry, as specified on your grant, and can be single or multiple entry. You **cannot work** on it, and study is limited to short courses. Always check your grant conditions and don't overstay.

## Why Applications Get Refused (and How to Avoid It)

- **Weak ties to Nigeria** — no clear reason to return.
- **Insufficient or unexplained funds.**
- **Thin or generic invitation** letters.
- **Inconsistent itinerary** or purpose.
- **Incomplete forms** or missing documents.

Address each pillar directly so the non-refundable fee isn't wasted.

## Best Time to Visit and Practical Tips

Australia's seasons are reversed: its **summer is December–February** (great for beaches) and **winter is June–August**. Spring and autumn are mild and ideal. Practical notes for Nigerian travellers:

- **Currency:** the Australian Dollar (AUD); cards and contactless are used everywhere.
- **Connectivity:** prepaid SIMs and eSIMs are easy at airports and stores.
- **Getting around:** domestic flights cover long distances; cities have good public transport.
- **Food:** diverse cuisine in major cities, with African and halal options available.
- **Etiquette:** Australians are casual and friendly; tipping is optional.

## What You Can and Can't Do on This Visa

The subclass 600 is a **temporary visitor visa** for tourism, family visits, and short business activities. You **can** sightsee, visit relatives, and attend meetings; you **cannot** work, and study is limited to short courses. Respect the stay length on your grant — overstaying harms future applications and can trigger bans.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Booking non-refundable flights** before the visa is granted.
- **Underfunded or unexplained** bank statements.
- **A weak GTE case** — failing to show ties to Nigeria.
- **Generic invitation letters** without the host's full details.
- **Leaving requested documents** or health checks until the last minute.

## What to See in Australia

Most trips start in **Sydney**, where the **Opera House**, **Harbour Bridge**, Bondi Beach, and harbour ferries define the skyline. **Melbourne** counters with laneway cafés, sport, and the scenic **Great Ocean Road**. Up north, **Queensland** delivers the **Great Barrier Reef** (Cairns and the Whitsundays) and rainforest, while **Uluru** in the Red Centre offers an unforgettable desert and cultural experience. **Brisbane** and the **Gold Coast** add theme parks and surf beaches. Distances are large, so plan domestic flights and focus on one or two regions per trip.

## How to Strengthen Your Australia Application

Whether your route is straightforward or strict, the same principles separate smooth approvals from frustrating delays — and they are worth understanding before you apply. At VisitPlane, we've mapped these patterns across dozens of visa routes, and the advice below applies directly to Nigerian travellers heading to Australia, where the Genuine Temporary Entrant test sits at the centre of the decision.

Start with your **finances**, the single most scrutinised part of almost any application. Officers check that your funds are *genuine and stable*, not just present. A balance that has sat comfortably for three to six months is far more convincing than a large sum that appeared just before you applied. If a relative in Australia is supporting your trip, document that relationship and their status clearly.

Next, ensure **consistency** — your itinerary, invitation, accommodation, and statements should tell one coherent story. Mismatches invite questions and slow the decision. Because subclass 600 turns on intent, **ties to Nigeria** matter enormously: stable employment, a business, property, or dependents reassure the officer you'll return.

Finally, be **honest and complete** — declare any previous refusal truthfully, and respond to any request promptly.
