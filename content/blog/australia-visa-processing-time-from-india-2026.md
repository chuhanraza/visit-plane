## Overview

One of the most common questions Indian travellers ask before booking flights to Australia is simple: how long will the visa actually take? The honest answer is that processing for the **Visitor visa (subclass 600)** varies — published estimates and real-world waits aren't always the same, and your own documents heavily influence the outcome.

This guide explains the realistic 2026 timeline for Indian applicants, the stages your application moves through, what speeds it up or slows it down, and exactly when to apply so you're never racing the clock. On VisitPlane, we verify every route against official sources.

> **Key takeaway:** Most Indian subclass 600 Tourist stream applications are decided in **about 20–40 days**, though well-documented cases can be faster and complex ones slower. Apply **6–8 weeks before travel** to leave room for biometrics and any extra checks.

## The Headline Processing Time

The Australian Department of Home Affairs processes most **Tourist stream** subclass 600 visas in a range that generally falls around **20–40 days** for Indian applicants. The department publishes indicative global processing times that shift with demand, so treat any single number as a guide, not a guarantee.

## Stage by Stage: Where the Time Goes

1. **Preparation (your control):** gathering funds evidence, invitation letters, and itinerary — days to weeks.
2. **Online lodgement via ImmiAccount:** instant once documents are ready.
3. **Biometrics:** attend a VFS Global centre when requested — book promptly to avoid delay.
4. **Assessment:** the case officer reviews your Genuine Temporary Entrant (GTE) case and documents — the main waiting period.
5. **Possible request for more information** or a health examination — can add 1–3 weeks.
6. **Decision:** delivered electronically and linked to your passport.

## What Speeds It Up

- **A complete, well-organised application** with clear funds and ties to India.
- **Prompt biometrics** — don't delay your VFS appointment.
- **A clean travel history** and previous visas to credible countries.
- **Responding quickly** if the department requests more documents.

## What Slows It Down

- **Incomplete documents** or unexplained finances.
- **Peak demand periods** (holidays, school breaks).
- **Health checks** required for some applicants.
- **A weak GTE case** that prompts extra scrutiny.

## Are There Faster Options?

Australia doesn't sell a general "priority" tourist visa the way some countries do. The best way to get a faster decision is simply to **submit a strong, complete file** and **attend biometrics immediately**. Genuine emergencies can sometimes be flagged, but you can't pay to jump the queue for a standard visit visa.

## When Should You Apply?

Apply **6–8 weeks before** your intended travel date. This buffer covers biometrics, the assessment window, and any request for more information or a health check. You can apply up to several months ahead, which is wise for peak-season travel.

## How to Track Your Application

You can monitor status through your **ImmiAccount**, where the department posts updates and any requests. Keep an eye on the linked email address and respond to any correspondence quickly — delays in your response extend the timeline.

## What to Do If It's Delayed

- **Check ImmiAccount** for any outstanding requests you may have missed.
- **Ensure biometrics and health checks** are complete.
- **Avoid lodging duplicate applications**, which can confuse processing.
- **Don't book non-refundable travel** until the visa is granted.

## Documents That Affect Your Timeline

Some documents do more than satisfy a checklist — they actively shorten or lengthen your wait. **Bank statements** that clearly show stable, sufficient funds reduce the chance of a clarification request. A **detailed itinerary and invitation letter** (with your host's status in Australia) pre-empts the questions an officer would otherwise have to ask. **Previous travel history** — visas to the UK, US, Schengen, or similar — signals lower risk and tends to smooth assessment. Conversely, gaps, inconsistencies, or thin documentation are what trigger the back-and-forth that adds weeks.

Health requirements deserve special attention. For some applicants — particularly for longer stays or certain backgrounds — a **health examination** is requested mid-assessment. If you can anticipate this and complete it promptly when asked, you avoid a common multi-week delay. Keep your passport valid well beyond your travel dates, since a near-expiry passport can complicate the grant.

## If You're Travelling Soon

If your trip is only a few weeks away and you haven't applied, prioritise a **complete, well-documented lodgement** over speed shortcuts — a clean file is your fastest path. Attend biometrics the moment you're invited, monitor ImmiAccount daily, and respond to any request within hours rather than days. Avoid booking non-refundable flights until the visa is granted; if you must hold a booking, keep it refundable. For genuine emergencies, you can explain the urgency, but there's no guaranteed paid fast-track for a standard visitor visa.

## How to Strengthen (and Speed Up) Your Application

The fastest applications are almost always the **most complete** ones, so the same principles that win approvals also save time. At VisitPlane, we've mapped these patterns across dozens of visa routes, and the advice below applies directly to Indian travellers heading to Australia.

Start with your **finances**, the single most scrutinised part of almost any application. Officers check that your funds are *genuine and stable*, not just present. A balance that has sat comfortably for three to six months is far more convincing than a large sum that appeared just before you applied — and a convincing file is assessed faster. If a relative in Australia is supporting your trip, document that relationship and their status clearly.

Next, ensure **consistency across your documents**. Your itinerary, invitation, accommodation, and statements should tell one coherent story. Mismatches trigger requests for more information, which is the single biggest avoidable cause of delay.

Because subclass 600 turns on intent, **ties to India** matter enormously. Evidence of stable employment, a business, property, or dependents reassures the officer you'll return — and reduces the need for further questions. Finally, be **honest and complete**, and respond to any request immediately to keep the clock moving.

## Common Mistakes That Cost Time

- **Applying too late** and racing your travel date.
- **Delaying biometrics** after lodgement.
- **Submitting an incomplete file** that triggers information requests.
- **Ignoring ImmiAccount messages** or the linked email.
- **Booking non-refundable flights** before the grant.

## Sample Timelines: What to Expect

It helps to picture the whole journey rather than just the assessment window. A well-prepared applicant might look like this:

- **Weeks 1–2:** gather funds evidence, invitation letter, and itinerary; create ImmiAccount and lodge.
- **Within days of lodging:** receive the biometrics request; book and attend the VFS appointment that same week.
- **Weeks 2–5:** case officer assessment — the main waiting period.
- **Around week 4–6:** decision, or a request for more information / health exam that adds 1–3 weeks.

A complex case (limited travel history, a health exam, or a document request) can push the total toward **6–8 weeks**, which is exactly why the 6–8 week head start matters.

## When to Apply Across the Year

Demand isn't constant. Indian application volumes rise around **summer holidays (May–July)**, **Diwali and year-end (October–December)**, and major event periods. If your travel falls in these windows, apply at the **earlier end** of the 6–8 week guidance — or earlier still. For off-peak travel, the standard buffer is comfortable. You can lodge several months ahead, and a visa granted early simply waits for your trip.

## How Australia Compares on Speed

Against other long-haul destinations, Australia's 20–40 day window is broadly comparable to a US visitor visa (where interview-slot waits can dominate) and slower than a Schengen visa's 15-day legal standard. Unlike Schengen, however, Australia frequently grants **12-month, multiple-entry** visitor visas, so the wait buys a longer, more flexible travel document — worthwhile for those who visit family regularly.
