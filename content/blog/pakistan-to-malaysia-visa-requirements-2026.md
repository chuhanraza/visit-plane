It's affordable, easy to reach, and welcoming. To visit, Pakistani passport holders apply for a **Malaysia eVISA** online and receive it by email — no embassy queue required.

## Do Pakistani Citizens Need a Visa for Malaysia?

Yes. Pakistani passport holders need a visa to enter Malaysia for tourism. The standard route is the **eVISA** — a fully online electronic visa for short tourist stays. There is no visa-on-arrival for Pakistani tourists, so apply before you travel.

## How the eVISA Works

- Applied for **online** at the official Malaysian eVISA portal.
- Usually **single entry**, allowing a **30-day stay**.
- Processed in a few working days and delivered as a **PDF by email**.
- You print it and carry it with your passport for entry.

> **Key takeaway:** Apply only through the **official eVISA portal** or an authorised agent; unofficial sites add large mark-ups for the same visa.

## Documents You Need

The Malaysia eVISA needs a modest document set:

- A **passport** valid at least **6 months** from arrival, with blank pages.
- A **scan of the passport bio-data page** and a recent **photo**.
- A **confirmed return/onward flight** and **hotel booking**.
- **Proof of funds** for your stay (bank statement).
- A valid **email** and a card for the fee.

## How to Apply: Step by Step

1. **Visit the official Malaysia eVISA portal** and create an application.
2. **Upload your passport page, photo, flight, and hotel** documents.
3. **Pay the fee** online by card.
4. **Wait for processing** (usually a few working days).
5. **Receive the eVISA by email** and print it.
6. **Carry it** with your passport for entry into Malaysia.

## Costs and Fees (2026)

- **eVISA fee:** the government charge plus a service fee — budget roughly **PKR 12,000–18,000** all-in, depending on the channel.
- **Express processing** (if offered) costs more.

Exact amounts depend on the portal/agent and the exchange rate, so confirm before paying. The fee is non-refundable.

## Processing Time

Standard eVISA processing typically takes **a few working days** (often 2–5). Apply **at least a week or two ahead** of travel to allow for any delays or document requests. Avoid leaving it to the last minute.

## Visa Validity and Stay

The Malaysia eVISA is typically **single-entry**, allowing a **30-day stay** within a validity window (commonly 90 days to enter). You cannot work on it. Track your days and don't overstay, which carries fines.

## Why Applications Get Refused (and How to Avoid It)

Approval rates are high; the few issues come from:

- **Typos** in name or passport number.
- **Passport validity** under 6 months.
- **Low-quality** passport scans or photos.
- **No return ticket or hotel** booking.

The fee is non-refundable, so review every field before submitting.

## Best Time to Visit and Practical Tips

Malaysia is warm year-round; the **drier months (roughly December–February on the west coast)** are ideal, though the country is a great destination any time. Practical notes for Pakistani travellers:

- **Currency:** the Malaysian Ringgit (MYR); cards are widely accepted, cash useful for markets.
- **Connectivity:** tourist SIMs are cheap; Grab is the main ride-hailing app.
- **Getting around:** KL has trains and monorail; domestic flights reach Langkawi and Borneo.
- **Food:** halal food is the norm — Malaysia is one of the easiest destinations for Muslim travellers; vegetarian options too.
- **Etiquette:** dress modestly at mosques; locals are warm and hospitable.

## What You Can and Can't Do on This Visa

The Malaysia eVISA is a **single-entry tourist visa**, typically a 30-day stay. You **can** sightsee and take domestic trips, but you **cannot** work, and you cannot re-enter on the same visa once you leave. Track your days and avoid overstaying.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Typos** in name or passport number on the non-refundable form.
- **Passport validity** under 6 months.
- **Using unofficial visa websites** that overcharge.
- **No return ticket or hotel proof.**
- **Applying too late** for processing before your travel date.
- **Choosing the wrong facility** (eVISA vs eNTRI) for your trip.

## What to See in Malaysia

Most trips begin in **Kuala Lumpur**, where the soaring **Petronas Twin Towers**, the KL Tower, Batu Caves, and bustling Bukit Bintang define the skyline and street life. A short hop away, the cool **Cameron Highlands** offer tea plantations and strawberry farms, while **Genting Highlands** brings theme parks and casinos.

Beyond the capital, **Penang** (George Town) is a UNESCO-listed feast of street art, colonial architecture, and some of Asia's best food, and **Langkawi** delivers duty-free beaches, cable cars, and island-hopping. For the adventurous, **Borneo** (Sabah and Sarawak) offers rainforests, orangutans, and Mount Kinabalu. With halal food everywhere and short domestic flights, a week comfortably covers KL plus an island or highland add-on.

## eVISA vs eNTRI vs Sticker Visa

Malaysia has offered different entry facilities over time, and it helps to know which applies to you. The **eVISA** is the standard online tourist visa for Pakistani travellers — a single-entry electronic visa for short stays. At times Malaysia has also run an **eNTRI** facility for certain nationalities, and a traditional **sticker visa** through the High Commission exists as well. For most Pakistani tourists in 2026, the **eVISA is the route to use**, applied for through the official portal. Always confirm the current options on the official Immigration Department portal before applying, since facilities can change, and avoid third-party sites that present an unofficial "visa" at a marked-up price.
