## Overview

The Maldives is a dream honeymoon and beach destination for Indian travellers — overwater villas, turquoise lagoons, and world-class diving and snorkelling. And here's the best part for budgeting: the **tourist visa itself is completely free** for Indian passport holders, granted **on arrival** for 30 days. There's no application fee, no embassy visit, and no online portal to pay.

This guide explains what's free, what isn't, and what an Indian traveller actually spends on a Maldives trip in 2026. On VisitPlane, we verify every route against official sources.

> **Key takeaway:** The Maldives gives Indians a **free 30-day visa on arrival** — the visa costs **₹0**. You only need a passport, a confirmed hotel/resort booking, a return ticket, and sufficient funds. Budget instead for the **trip itself**, which is where the real cost lies.

## The Visa Cost: Zero

Unlike most destinations, there is **no tourist visa fee** for the Maldives:

- **Visa on arrival:** free for all nationalities, including Indians.
- **Validity:** 30 days from entry.
- **No advance application** — it's stamped at Velana International Airport on arrival.

If you want to stay longer, you can apply to **extend up to 90 days** in total, which does carry a fee paid locally to immigration.

## What You Must Have on Arrival

Even though the visa is free, entry isn't automatic — you must show:

- A **passport** valid at least **6 months** from arrival.
- A **confirmed hotel/resort or guesthouse booking** for your stay.
- A **return or onward ticket**.
- **Sufficient funds** to cover your trip.
- A completed **Traveller Declaration** (an online form, IMUGA) within the window before arrival.

## The Real Costs of a Maldives Trip

Since the visa is free, your budget is all about the trip. Rough 2026 estimates per person for an Indian traveller:

- **Return flights (India–Malé):** roughly ₹25,000–₹55,000 depending on season and city.
- **Accommodation:** this is the big one — ₹15,000–₹60,000+ per night for resorts; local guesthouses on inhabited islands are far cheaper (₹5,000–₹12,000).
- **Speedboat / seaplane transfers:** resort transfers can cost ₹10,000–₹40,000 return — a major hidden cost.
- **Daily spend (food, activities, diving):** ₹5,000–₹15,000 per person.

A typical 4–5 night Maldives trip can range from **₹80,000 per person** (guesthouse style) to **₹3 lakh+ per person** (luxury resort), with accommodation and transfers the biggest variables.

## The Tourism Taxes to Know

While the visa is free, the Maldives charges tourism-related taxes that are built into your bills:

- **Green Tax:** a per-night environmental tax charged by resorts and hotels.
- **Departure tax / airport service charge:** usually included in your flight ticket.
- **GST** on goods and services.

These are paid through your accommodation and tickets rather than as a separate visa fee, but factor them into your budget.

## Ways to Keep the Cost Down

- **Choose local-island guesthouses** instead of private-island resorts to cut costs dramatically.
- **Travel in the off-season** (May–November) for lower rates.
- **Pick resorts with speedboat (not seaplane) transfers** to save on transfer costs.
- **Book half-board or all-inclusive** if dining options are limited on your island.

## How the Maldives Compares

For Indian travellers, the Maldives is one of the **easiest** destinations on paper — a free visa, no embassy, no application. The catch is the **trip cost**: it's a premium beach destination, so while you save entirely on the visa, accommodation and transfers make it one of the pricier holidays per night.

## Sample Budgets

Since the visa is free, your budget is entirely about the trip. Two realistic all-in examples for an Indian traveller:

The visa saving is real but small next to accommodation and transfers — which is where smart choices make the biggest difference.

## Currency and Payment Tips

The Maldives uses the **Maldivian Rufiyaa (MVR)**, but resorts and most tourist services bill in **US Dollars**, and cards are accepted almost everywhere on resort islands. Carry a low-foreign-fee card and some USD cash for local islands and tips. Resort extras (diving, excursions, spa) add up fast and are charged to your room — review the bill, since **Green Tax, service charge, and GST** are added on top. There's no visa fee to budget for, so direct that saving toward transfers or an extra night.

## Choosing Your Island: The Biggest Cost Decision

Because the visa is free, the single decision that most shapes your Maldives budget is **what kind of island you stay on**. There are two broad options. **Private-island resorts** offer the classic overwater-villa experience — stunning, all-inclusive, and expensive, often ₹25,000–₹60,000+ per night, usually reached by costly seaplane or speedboat transfer. **Local (inhabited) islands** like Maafushi let you stay in guesthouses for a fraction of the price (₹5,000–₹12,000 per night), with public ferries or shared speedboats keeping transfers cheap, plus day trips to sandbanks and snorkelling spots.

A growing number of Indian travellers mix both — a few nights on a local island for value, then a splurge night at a resort. Whatever you choose, **transfers are a hidden cost** that can rival a night's accommodation, so check how you'll reach your island and what it costs before booking. Getting the island choice right matters far more to your total spend than the (free) visa ever could.

## When to Visit and How It Affects Cost

The Maldives has two seasons: the **dry season (roughly November–April)** brings the best weather and the highest prices, while the **wet season (May–October)** offers lower rates and fewer crowds, with rain often passing in short bursts. Travelling in the shoulder months can cut accommodation costs significantly while still giving good weather — another way to stretch a budget that has no visa fee to worry about.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Forgetting the IMUGA declaration** before arrival.
- **Underestimating transfer costs** (seaplane/speedboat).
- **No confirmed accommodation** or return ticket at immigration.
- **Assuming "free visa" means a cheap trip** — the trip itself is premium.
- **Overstaying** the 30-day visa without an extension.
