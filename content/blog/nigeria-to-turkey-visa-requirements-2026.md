## Overview

Turkey — straddling Europe and Asia, with Istanbul's mosques and bazaars, the fairy chimneys of Cappadocia, and the turquoise coast — is a bucket-list destination for many Nigerian travellers. But the entry process is more involved than for some nationalities: **Nigerian passport holders are NOT eligible for the Turkey e-Visa**. You must obtain a **sticker visa** through the Turkish embassy/consulate or an authorised visa application centre.

This guide explains the 2026 process for Nigerian citizens — eligibility, the documents (including a key supporting-visa requirement), the fee, processing time, and approval tips. On VisitPlane, we verify every route against official sources.

> **Key takeaway:** Nigerians **cannot** use the Turkey e-Visa. You need a **sticker (stamped) visa** via the Turkish mission/VFS — and you generally must hold a **valid Schengen, US, UK, or Ireland visa/residence** as a supporting document.

## Do Nigerian Citizens Need a Visa for Turkey?

Yes — a visa is mandatory for tourism and business. Critically, the convenient online **e-Visa is not available to Nigerian passport holders**. Applications must be made for a **sticker visa** through the Turkish Embassy in Abuja, the Consulate, or an authorised visa application centre.

## The Supporting-Visa Requirement

A defining feature of this route: Nigerian applicants are generally required to hold a **valid visa or residence permit from a Schengen country, the United Kingdom, the United States, or Ireland**. This supporting document is a common precondition for the Turkish tourist visa, so confirm you meet it before applying.

> **Key takeaway:** Without a valid Schengen/US/UK/Ireland visa or residence, a Turkish tourist visa is difficult to obtain for Nigerian applicants. Check this requirement first — it's the single biggest gating factor.

## Documents You Need

For the Turkey sticker visa, Nigerian applicants typically need:

- A **passport valid for at least 5–6 months** beyond arrival, with blank pages.
- The completed **visa application form**.
- **Passport-size photographs** to specification.
- A **valid Schengen/US/UK/Ireland visa or residence permit** (supporting document).
- **Confirmed return flights** and **hotel bookings**.
- **Bank statements** showing sufficient funds.
- **Proof of employment/business** and ties to Nigeria.
- **Travel health insurance**.

> **Key takeaway:** Strong **ties to Nigeria** and solid **financials** are decisive — the consulate wants confidence that you'll return home.

## How to Apply: Step by Step

1. **Confirm eligibility** — ensure you hold the required supporting visa/residence.
2. **Book an appointment** at the Turkish Embassy/Consulate or authorised visa centre.
3. **Complete the form** and assemble your documents.
4. **Attend your appointment**, submit documents, and give biometrics if required.
5. **Pay the visa fee** (see below).
6. **Wait for processing** (commonly 15–21 working days).
7. **Collect your passport** with the sticker visa.

## Costs and Fees (2026)

- **Single-entry tourist visa:** around **USD 250** for Nigerian applicants.
- **Service/appointment charges** may apply at the visa centre.
- Budget separately for insurance, flights, and accommodation.

Fees can change; confirm with the embassy or authorised centre.

## Processing Time

Turkish sticker visas for Nigerian applicants typically take **15–21 working days**. Apply **well in advance** — at least 4–6 weeks before travel — and account for appointment availability.

## Visa Validity and Stay

Tourist visas commonly grant stays of **30, 60, or 90 days**, depending on the case. Read your visa for the exact validity, number of entries, and permitted stay. You cannot work on a tourist visa, and overstaying leads to fines and entry bans.

## Why Applications Get Refused (and How to Avoid It)

For Nigerian applicants, refusals most often stem from:

- **Missing the supporting Schengen/US/UK/Ireland visa** requirement.
- **Weak financial evidence** or unstable balances.
- **Weak ties to Nigeria** (employment, business, family).
- **Incomplete documentation** or inconsistent travel plans.

Confirm eligibility and present a complete, consistent file to maximise approval.

## Best Time to Visit and Practical Tips

Turkey is best in **April–June** and **September–October**, with mild weather for Istanbul, Cappadocia, and the coast; summers are hot and popular, winters cooler. Practical notes for Nigerian travellers:

- **Currency:** the Turkish Lira (TRY); cards are widely accepted, with cash handy for bazaars.
- **Connectivity:** tourist SIMs are available (note Turkey registers phones after ~120 days); Uber/BiTaksi operate in Istanbul.
- **Getting around:** domestic flights are cheap and link Istanbul with Cappadocia (Nevsehir/Kayseri), Antalya, and Izmir.
- **Etiquette:** dress modestly at mosques (cover shoulders/knees; women cover hair); remove shoes before entering.
- **Documents:** carry your visa, supporting Schengen/US/UK visa, and bookings, as you may be asked at entry.

## What You Can and Can't Do on This Visa

The sticker tourist visa is for **tourism and business visits** — not paid work. You can sightsee and attend meetings for the visa's permitted stay (commonly 30, 60, or 90 days). You **cannot** work or overstay. Because eligibility hinges on holding a supporting Schengen/US/UK/Ireland visa, ensure that document stays valid for your travel dates.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Assuming the e-Visa is available** — it is not, for Nigerian passport holders.
- **Missing the supporting Schengen/US/UK/Ireland visa** requirement.
- **Weak ties to Nigeria** or thin financial evidence.
- **Applying too late** for the 15–21 day processing and appointment lead time.
- **Inconsistent travel plans** across documents.

## How to Strengthen Your Turkey Application

Whether your route is straightforward or strict, the same principles separate smooth approvals from frustrating delays — and they are worth understanding before you apply. At VisitPlane, we've mapped these patterns across dozens of visa routes, and the advice below applies directly to Nigerian travellers heading to Turkey.

Start with your **finances**, because they are the single most scrutinised part of almost any application. Officers and systems are not simply checking that you have money today; they are checking that your funds are *genuine and stable*. A balance that has sat comfortably in your account for three to six months tells a far more convincing story than a large sum that appeared a week before you applied. If a relative is supporting your trip, document that relationship clearly rather than leaving an unexplained deposit. Keep your statements clean, recent, and consistent with the trip you are describing.

Next, think about **consistency across your documents**. Your flight dates, hotel bookings, cover letter, and bank statements should all tell one coherent story. If your itinerary says ten days but your hotel booking covers four, or your stated purpose is tourism while your documents suggest something else, that mismatch invites questions and slows everything down. Before you submit, lay your documents side by side and read them as an officer would — every date and detail should line up.

For routes that assess intent, **ties to your home country** matter enormously. Evidence of stable employment, a running business, property, or dependents reassures the decision-maker that you intend to return after your visit to Turkey. This is less about any single document and more about the overall picture your application paints.

Finally, be **honest and complete**. Declaring a previous refusal — for any country — is almost always better than hiding it, because non-disclosure is treated far more seriously than an old rejection. Submit every requested document, double-check every field, and apply with enough time to absorb processing and any follow-up requests.
