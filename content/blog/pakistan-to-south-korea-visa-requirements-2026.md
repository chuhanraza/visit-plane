## Overview

South Korea is a rising destination for Pakistani travellers — Seoul's blend of palaces and K-pop modernity, the food, the cherry blossoms and autumn colours, Jeju Island, and a culture made globally famous by Korean dramas and music. To visit, Pakistani passport holders need a **C-3 short-term tourist visa**, applied for **in person** at the Korean Embassy or its consular office.

This guide explains the 2026 process for Pakistani citizens — the documents, processing, validity, and approval tips. On VisitPlane, we verify every route against official sources.

> **Key takeaway:** Pakistanis need a **C-3 tourist visa** for South Korea. Pakistan is **not** on the K-ETA / visa-waiver list, so apply **in person** at the **Korean Embassy in Islamabad or the Karachi office**; processing takes **at least 3 weeks**, for a short-term stay of up to **30 days**.

## Do Pakistani Citizens Need a Visa for South Korea?

Yes. Pakistani passport holders with an ordinary passport must obtain a visa to enter the Republic of Korea. Because Pakistan is **not** visa-exempt, you cannot use the K-ETA travel authorisation — you need a proper **C-3 short-term general/tourist visa**, applied for in person.

## How the Visa Works

- Applied for **in person** at the **Korean Embassy in Islamabad** or the **Karachi consular office**.
- A **C-3 short-term visa**, typically allowing a stay of up to **30 days**.
- Processed in **at least 3 weeks** (sometimes longer).
- Strong applicants may, in some cases, receive longer-validity multiple-entry visas.

> **Key takeaway:** Plan well ahead — the in-person process and 3-week-plus timeline mean this is not a last-minute visa.

## Documents You Need

- A **passport** valid for the trip, with blank pages, plus a copy.
- The **completed visa application form** and a recent **photo** (to Korea's specifications).
- A **confirmed return flight** booking and a **travel itinerary**.
- **Confirmed hotel bookings** for the whole stay.
- **Bank statements** (usually last 6 months) and **proof of employment or business**.
- Evidence of **ties to Pakistan**, and an **invitation letter** if visiting family/friends.

## How to Apply: Step by Step

1. **Prepare your itinerary** and book refundable flights and hotels.
2. **Complete the application form** and gather your documents.
3. **Submit in person** at the Korean Embassy (Islamabad) or Karachi office during visa hours.
4. **Pay the visa fee**.
5. **Wait for processing** (at least 3 weeks).
6. **Collect your passport** with the visa once decided.

## Costs and Fees (2026)

- **Visa fee:** the consular fee for a short-term single-entry visa (confirm the current amount at the embassy), with multiple-entry costing more.
- **Photos, document prep, and travel** to Islamabad/Karachi: additional.

The fee is non-refundable, so prepare a complete file.

## Processing Time

Processing takes **at least 3 weeks**, and can be longer in busy periods such as spring (cherry blossoms) and autumn. Apply **4–6 weeks ahead** of travel, and don't book non-refundable flights until the visa is granted.

## Visa Validity and Stay

A C-3 short-term tourist visa typically allows a stay of up to **30 days**. The validity window and whether it's single or multiple entry depend on the case and your profile. You cannot work on it. Track your days and don't overstay.

## Why Applications Get Refused (and How to Avoid It)

- **Weak or unstable finances.**
- **A vague itinerary** or unclear travel purpose.
- **Weak ties to Pakistan.**
- **Incomplete documents** or an improperly filled form.

The fee is non-refundable, so prepare a complete, consistent file.

## Best Time to Visit and Practical Tips

South Korea is loveliest in **spring (April, cherry blossoms)** and **autumn (October–November, foliage)**. Practical notes for Pakistani travellers:

- **Currency:** the South Korean Won (KRW); cards are accepted almost everywhere.
- **Connectivity:** rent a pocket Wi-Fi or buy an eSIM/SIM at the airport.
- **Getting around:** Seoul's metro is excellent; KTX trains and domestic flights reach other cities and Jeju.
- **Food:** halal restaurants exist in Seoul (especially Itaewon); carry a translation app for ingredients.
- **Etiquette:** use two hands when giving/receiving; remove shoes indoors where required.

## What You Can and Can't Do on This Visa

A C-3 tourist visa is for **tourism and short visits**. You **can** sightsee and take day trips; you **cannot** work, and you must respect the permitted stay. Overstaying carries penalties and affects future visas, so leave on time.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Leaving it late** — the in-person, 3-week process needs planning.
- **A vague itinerary** or weak ties to Pakistan.
- **Underfunded** bank statements.
- **Booking non-refundable flights** before approval.
- **An improperly completed** form or non-spec photo.
- **Assuming K-ETA applies** — it does not, since Pakistan is not visa-exempt.
- **Travelling to the wrong office** — check whether Islamabad or Karachi serves your region.

## What to See in South Korea

Most trips begin in **Seoul**, where grand palaces like **Gyeongbokgung**, the traditional **Bukchon Hanok Village**, the buzzing markets of Myeongdong and Namdaemun, and the views from **N Seoul Tower** sit beside cutting-edge districts like Gangnam. Day trips reach the **DMZ** and the folk village at Suwon's Hwaseong Fortress. Beyond the capital, **Busan** offers beaches, seafood, and the colourful Gamcheon Culture Village, while **Jeju Island** delivers volcanic landscapes, waterfalls, and hiking. In spring the cherry blossoms are spectacular; in autumn the mountains glow. With fast trains and an easy metro, a week comfortably covers Seoul plus Busan or Jeju.

## How to Strengthen Your South Korea Application

Whether your route is straightforward or strict, the same principles separate smooth approvals from frustrating delays — and they are worth understanding before you apply. At VisitPlane, we've mapped these patterns across dozens of visa routes, and the advice below applies directly to Pakistani travellers heading to South Korea.

Start with your **finances**, the single most scrutinised part of almost any application. Officers check that your funds are *genuine and stable*, not just present. A balance that has sat comfortably for three to six months is far more convincing than a large sum that appeared just before you applied. If a relative is sponsoring you, document that relationship clearly.

Next, present a clear, realistic **itinerary** that matches your flight and hotel bookings. Consistency across your documents signals a genuine tourist and smooths the decision; mismatches invite questions and delay.

Because the tourist visa assesses intent, **ties to Pakistan** matter — stable employment, a business, property, or family reassure the officer you'll return.
