## Overview

Studying in Australia is a major goal for many Indian students, and the Subclass 500 student visa is the gateway. This 2026 guide walks you through the whole process step by step — eligibility, the funds you must show, the documents, the application, your work rights, and how to avoid the mistakes that cause refusals.

## Eligibility: Who Can Apply

To qualify, you need a genuine offer from a recognised Australia institution, the funds to cover your studies and living costs, the required language ability, and a credible intention to study. Indian students meet the same core bar as everyone else — the key is documenting each part clearly and consistently.

## The Funds You Must Show

This is the part that trips up the most applicants. For Australia, you must show **AUD 29,710 a year** for living costs plus your first-year tuition and Overseas Student Health Cover (OSHC). Build this balance **early** so your statements show a settled, multi-month history rather than a last-minute deposit, and document the source of any large credit. If a sponsor funds you, connect the dots with proof of relationship and their income. Funds that appear suddenly are the fastest route to a refusal.

## Step-by-Step: How to Apply

The process for the Australia Subclass 500 student visa runs roughly like this: secure a Confirmation of Enrolment (CoE), a Genuine Student statement, OSHC, and an online ImmiAccount application. Work through these in order, because each step depends on the one before — and don't book travel until your visa is approved.

## Documents You'll Need

Prepare your **admission letter**, evidence of **funds** (in the required form), your **academic transcripts and certificates**, your **language test** result, a valid **passport**, **photos** to specification, and any **health insurance** or medical requirements. Add a clear **statement of purpose** explaining your course choice and plans. Organise everything so a reviewer can follow your story without hunting for a document.

## Your Statement of Purpose

A strong statement of purpose ties the file together. Explain **why this course and institution**, how it builds on your background, **why Australia**, how you'll fund it, and your plans after graduating. Be specific and honest — name modules and reasons rather than generic praise — and keep every claim consistent with your documents. For Indian students, a credible, well-evidenced study plan is often what separates an approval from a refusal.

## Work Rights While Studying

The Australia Subclass 500 student visa generally allows up to 48 hours per fortnight during term. Treat work as a supplement, not your funding plan — you must still show you can support yourself without relying on term-time earnings. Always stay within the permitted hours, because breaching them can jeopardise your visa and future applications.

## Processing and Timeline

Apply **well ahead** of your course start date, as processing varies by season and demand and biometrics or interview slots can be limited. Check the current official estimate when you apply, build in a comfortable buffer, and keep travel bookings flexible until your visa is confirmed.

## After You're Approved

Once approved, check the visa details — name, validity, and conditions — as soon as you receive it. Sort out accommodation, arrival logistics, and any registration step (some countries require you to register or validate your status after arrival). Arrive with your key documents accessible, since you may be asked about your course and funding at the border.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Funds that appear suddenly** before applying, with no source documentation.
- **A vague or generic study plan** that doesn't explain your course choice.
- **Inconsistencies** between your application, funds, and documents.
- **Leaving the application late**, against appointment and processing waits.
- **Relying on planned term-time work** to meet the funds requirement.
- **Booking travel** before the visa is approved.
