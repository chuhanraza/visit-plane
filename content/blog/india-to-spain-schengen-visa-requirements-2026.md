## Overview

Spain is one of Europe's most rewarding destinations for Indian travellers — Barcelona's Gaudí architecture, Madrid's museums and nightlife, the Alhambra in Granada, the beaches of the Costa del Sol, and a food-and-football culture that's hard to beat. To visit, Indian passport holders need a **Schengen short-stay visa (Type C)** issued by Spain for tourism, family visits, or business.

> **Key takeaway:** Indians need a **Schengen visa** for Spain. Apply through Spain's official partner (usually **BLS International**, with VFS for some categories) up to **6 months** ahead; the fee rose to **€90** on 11 June 2026, and a decision usually takes **about 15 days**, longer in peak season.

## Do Indian Citizens Need a Visa for Spain?

Yes. Indian passport holders must obtain a Schengen visa before travelling; there is no visa-on-arrival. For tourism or family visits, you apply for a **short-stay Type C visa** allowing up to **90 days within any 180-day period** across the Schengen area.

## How the Schengen Visa Works

- It is a **short-stay visa** valid for the whole **29-country Schengen area**.
- You apply at the consulate of your **main destination** — if Spain is where you'll spend the most days, you apply to Spain.
- Stays are capped at **90 days in any rolling 180-day window**.
- Entries can be single or multiple, at the consulate's discretion.

> **Key takeaway:** Apply to **Spain** only if it's your main destination (most nights) or first entry point — choosing the wrong country is a common refusal reason.

## Documents You Need

For a Spanish Schengen tourist visa, prepare:

- A **passport** valid at least **3 months beyond** departure, with two blank pages, issued within 10 years.
- The **completed Schengen form** and a recent **biometric photo**.
- **Travel medical insurance** covering at least **€30,000**.
- **Round-trip flight reservation** and **confirmed accommodation** for the full stay.
- **Proof of funds** — bank statements for 3–6 months, salary slips or ITRs.
- A **cover letter** with your itinerary and proof of **ties to India**.

## How to Apply: Step by Step

1. **Confirm Spain is your main destination** and draft an itinerary.
2. **Complete the application** on Spain's official visa channel.
3. **Book an appointment** at the Spanish visa centre (BLS International / VFS depending on city and category).
4. **Attend the appointment** to submit documents and give **biometrics**.
5. **Pay the visa fee** plus the service charge.
6. **Track and collect** your passport once decided.

## Costs and Fees (2026)

- **Schengen visa fee:** **€90** for adults (raised from €80 on 11 June 2026); **€45** for children 6–11; free under 6.
- That's roughly **₹8,500**, plus a **service charge** of around **₹2,000**, so budget **₹10,000–₹12,000** all-in per adult before insurance.

Confirm the current amount on the official portal before paying — it's non-refundable.

## Processing Time

The Schengen legal standard is **15 calendar days**, extendable to 45 if extra checks apply. Spanish consulates generally decide within **2–3 weeks**, with **longer waits in summer**. Apply **at least 4–6 weeks ahead**, earlier for June–August travel.

## Visa Validity and Stay

A short-stay Schengen visa allows up to **90 days within any 180-day period**. The consulate sets the validity window and entry type; strong travel history improves the chance of a longer, multiple-entry visa. You cannot work on it. Track your days across all Schengen countries.

## Why Applications Get Refused (and How to Avoid It)

Common Spanish Schengen refusals come from:

- **Insufficient or unstable funds.**
- **Weak ties to India.**
- **Inconsistent itinerary** across flights, hotels, and cover letter.
- **Insurance below €30,000.**
- **Wrong consulate** when Spain isn't the main destination.

The fee is non-refundable, so review everything before submitting.

## How Much Will the Whole Trip Cost?

Rough 2026 estimates per person for an Indian traveller:

- **Visa:** ~₹10,000–₹12,000 including service charge.
- **Return flights (India–Spain):** roughly ₹45,000–₹95,000 depending on season.
- **Accommodation:** ₹5,500–₹14,000 per night in Barcelona or Madrid.

A typical 7-day Spain trip can land around **₹1.4–2.8 lakh per person**, with flights and hotels the main variables.

## Best Time to Visit and Practical Tips

Spain shines in **April–June** and **September–October** — warm, sunny, and less crowded than peak summer. Practical notes for Indian travellers:

- **Currency:** the Euro (€); cards are widely used, with cash handy for tapas bars and markets.
- **Connectivity:** eSIMs and SIMs are cheap; the AVE high-speed train links Madrid, Barcelona, Seville, and more.
- **Getting around:** trains and metros are excellent; cities are walkable.
- **Food:** vegetarian options are growing; halal is available in cities — Indian restaurants too.
- **Etiquette:** days run late (dinner after 9pm); watch for pickpockets in tourist areas.

## What You Can and Can't Do on This Visa

A Schengen Type C visa is a **short-stay visitor visa** for tourism, family, or business, capped at **90 days per 180 days**. You **can** travel across all Schengen countries within your validity, but you **cannot** work, study long-term, or overstay. Overstaying risks fines and bans.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Non-refundable flights** booked before approval.
- **Thin or freshly funded** bank statements.
- **Insurance gaps** or under €30,000.
- **Applying to Spain** when most nights are elsewhere.
- **Weak cover letter** without clear ties to India.

## What to See in Spain

Most trips centre on **Barcelona**, where Antoni Gaudí's surreal Sagrada Família, Park Güell, and Casa Batlló sit beside the Gothic Quarter and Mediterranean beaches. **Madrid**, the capital, offers the Prado and Reina Sofía museums, the Royal Palace, and buzzing plazas and tapas bars.

Andalusia in the south is unmissable: **Granada**'s Alhambra palace, **Seville**'s cathedral and Plaza de España, and **Córdoba**'s Mezquita showcase Spain's Moorish heritage. Beach lovers head to the **Costa del Sol** or the **Balearic and Canary Islands**, while **Valencia** blends futuristic architecture with paella's birthplace. For Indian travellers, distances are easy by high-speed train, vegetarian food is increasingly common, and a week comfortably covers Barcelona and Madrid plus a southern add-on.

## How to Strengthen Your Spain Application

Whether your route is straightforward or strict, the same principles separate smooth approvals from frustrating delays — and they are worth understanding before you apply. At VisitPlane, we've mapped these patterns across dozens of visa routes, and the advice below applies directly to Indian travellers heading to Spain.

Start with your **finances**, the single most scrutinised part of almost any application. Officers check that your funds are *genuine and stable*, not just present. A balance that has sat comfortably for three to six months is far more convincing than a large sum that appeared just before you applied. If a relative is sponsoring you, document that relationship clearly rather than leaving an unexplained deposit.

Next, ensure **consistency across your documents**. Flight dates, hotels, cover letter, and statements should tell one story. If your itinerary and bookings don't match, that gap invites questions. Read your file as an officer would before submitting.

Because the Schengen visa assesses intent, **ties to India** matter enormously. Stable employment, a business, property, or dependents reassure the officer you'll return. It's the overall picture that counts.

Finally, be **honest and complete**. Declaring a past refusal is almost always better than hiding it. Submit every requested document, check every field, and apply early enough to absorb delays.
