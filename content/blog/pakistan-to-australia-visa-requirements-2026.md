## Overview

Australia is a top long-haul destination for Pakistani travellers — Sydney's Opera House and Harbour Bridge, Melbourne's laneways and coffee culture, the Great Barrier Reef, and a huge, welcoming Pakistani diaspora that draws family visits year-round. To visit, Pakistani passport holders need a **Visitor visa (subclass 600)**, applied for **online** through the Australian government's **ImmiAccount** system — there is no visa-on-arrival.

This guide explains the 2026 process for Pakistani citizens — the fee, the documents, the all-important Genuine Temporary Entrant test, processing times, and approval tips. On VisitPlane, we verify every route against official sources.

> **Key takeaway:** Pakistanis need a **subclass 600 Visitor visa** for Australia. Apply **online via ImmiAccount**; the application charge is around **AUD 200** (Tourist stream, offshore), biometrics are usually required, and processing typically runs **about 20–33 days**.

## Do Pakistani Citizens Need a Visa for Australia?

Yes. Pakistan is not on Australia's eVisitor or ETA list, so Pakistani passport holders must apply for a **subclass 600 Visitor visa** before travelling. It covers **tourism, visiting family or friends, and short business activities**, and is granted for stays of **3, 6, or 12 months** depending on your circumstances.

## How the Subclass 600 Visa Works

- Applied for **online** by creating a free **ImmiAccount**.
- Has several **streams** — the **Tourist stream** is the usual choice for holidays and family visits.
- Decided on the basis of the **Genuine Temporary Entrant (GTE)** requirement — you must intend to visit temporarily and leave before it expires.
- Linked **electronically** to your passport once granted (no sticker needed).

> **Key takeaway:** The **GTE test** is the heart of the decision — your job, finances, family, and ties to Pakistan must show you'll return home after the visit.

## Documents You Need

For a subclass 600 Tourist stream application, prepare:

- A **passport** valid for the duration of your intended stay.
- A recent **photo** and completed online application.
- **Proof of funds** — bank statements (3–6 months), salary slips, tax returns, or business documents.
- **Evidence of ties to Pakistan** — employment letter, business ownership, property, family.
- A **travel itinerary** and, if visiting family, an **invitation letter** with the host's details and status in Australia.
- **Travel/health insurance** (recommended), and any documents requested for your circumstances.
- **Biometrics** (fingerprints and photo) at a VFS Global Australia Visa Application Centre when asked.

## How to Apply: Step by Step

1. **Create an ImmiAccount** on the Department of Home Affairs website.
2. **Complete the subclass 600 application** (Tourist stream) and upload documents.
3. **Pay the application charge** online by card.
4. **Provide biometrics** at the VFS Global centre in Pakistan if requested.
5. **Attend a health examination** if asked (more likely for longer stays).
6. **Wait for the decision**, delivered electronically and linked to your passport.

## Costs and Fees (2026)

- **Visa application charge:** around **AUD 200** for the Tourist stream (offshore). Onshore applications are higher.
- **Biometrics and VFS service fees** are additional.
- In rupees, the base charge is roughly **PKR 37,000–40,000**, before service and biometric fees.

Fees are **non-refundable** even if the visa is refused, so prepare your file carefully. Confirm the current charge on the official site before paying.

## Processing Time

The Tourist stream typically processes in about **20–33 days**, though it can be faster or slower depending on your documents and demand. Apply **at least 6–8 weeks ahead** of travel to allow for biometrics, any health checks, and possible requests for more information.

## Visa Validity and Stay

A subclass 600 visa allows stays of **3, 6, or 12 months** per entry, as specified on your grant. It can be single or multiple entry. You **cannot work** on it, and study is limited to short courses (up to 3 months). Always check the conditions on your grant notice and don't overstay.

## Why Applications Get Refused (and How to Avoid It)

Most subclass 600 refusals trace back to the GTE test and documentation:

- **Weak ties to Pakistan** — no clear reason to return.
- **Insufficient or unexplained funds.**
- **Thin or generic invitation** letters from family in Australia.
- **Inconsistent itinerary** or purpose.
- **Incomplete forms** or missing requested documents.

Address each pillar directly and the non-refundable fee is far less likely to be wasted.

## How Much Will the Whole Trip Cost?

Rough 2026 estimates per person for a Pakistani traveller:

- **Visa:** ~PKR 40,000+ including biometrics and service fees.
- **Return flights (Pakistan–Australia):** roughly PKR 350,000–600,000 depending on season and routing.
- **Accommodation:** PKR 25,000–60,000 per night in Sydney or Melbourne (or stay with family).
- **Daily spend:** PKR 15,000–35,000 per person.
- **Insurance:** PKR 8,000–20,000 for a longer trip.

A typical 2-week Australia trip can land around **PKR 8–15 lakh per person**, with long-haul flights the biggest factor.

## Best Time to Visit and Practical Tips

Remember Australia's seasons are reversed: its **summer is December–February** (great for beaches) and **winter is June–August**. Spring (September–November) and autumn (March–May) are mild and ideal. Practical notes for Pakistani travellers:

- **Currency:** the Australian Dollar (AUD); cards and contactless are used everywhere.
- **Connectivity:** prepaid SIMs and eSIMs are easy at airports and stores.
- **Getting around:** domestic flights cover long distances; cities have good public transport.
- **Food:** halal food is widely available in major cities; large South Asian communities mean plenty of Pakistani restaurants.
- **Etiquette:** Australians are casual and friendly; tipping is optional.

## What You Can and Can't Do on This Visa

The subclass 600 is a **temporary visitor visa** for tourism, family visits, and short business activities. You **can** sightsee, visit relatives, and attend meetings; you **cannot** work, and study is limited to short courses. Respect the stay length and conditions on your grant — overstaying harms future applications and can trigger bans.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Booking non-refundable flights** before the visa is granted.
- **Underfunded or unexplained** bank statements.
- **A weak GTE case** — failing to show ties to Pakistan.
- **Generic invitation letters** without the host's full details.
- **Leaving requested documents** or health checks until the last minute.

## What to See in Australia

Most trips start in **Sydney**, where the **Opera House**, **Harbour Bridge**, Bondi Beach, and harbour ferries define the skyline. **Melbourne** counters with laneway cafés, street art, sport, and the scenic **Great Ocean Road** day trip to the Twelve Apostles.

Up north, **Queensland** delivers the **Great Barrier Reef** (Cairns and the Whitsundays) and tropical rainforest, while **Uluru** in the Red Centre offers an unforgettable desert and cultural experience. **Brisbane** and the **Gold Coast** add theme parks and surf beaches, and **Tasmania** rewards nature lovers. For Pakistani travellers, halal food and established communities make every major city comfortable; distances are large, so plan domestic flights and focus on one or two regions per trip.

## How to Strengthen Your Australia Application

Whether your route is straightforward or strict, the same principles separate smooth approvals from frustrating delays — and they are worth understanding before you apply. At VisitPlane, we've mapped these patterns across dozens of visa routes, and the advice below applies directly to Pakistani travellers heading to Australia, where the Genuine Temporary Entrant test sits at the centre of the decision.

Start with your **finances**, the single most scrutinised part of almost any application. Officers check that your funds are *genuine and stable*, not just present. A balance that has sat comfortably for three to six months is far more convincing than a large sum that appeared just before you applied. If a relative in Australia is supporting your trip, document that relationship and their status clearly rather than leaving an unexplained deposit.

Next, ensure **consistency across your documents**. Your itinerary, invitation letter, accommodation, and statements should tell one coherent story. If your stated plans and your bookings don't match, that gap invites questions and slows the decision.

Because subclass 600 turns on intent, **ties to Pakistan** matter enormously. Evidence of stable employment, a running business, property, or dependents reassures the officer that you'll return home after your visit. This is less about any single document and more about the overall picture your application paints.

Finally, be **honest and complete**. Declaring a previous refusal — for any country — is almost always better than hiding it, because non-disclosure is treated far more seriously than an old rejection. Submit every requested document, double-check every field, and apply with enough time to absorb biometrics, health checks, and any follow-up.
