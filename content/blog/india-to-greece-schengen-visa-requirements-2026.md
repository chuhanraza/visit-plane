## Overview

Greece is a dream Mediterranean escape for Indian travellers — the whitewashed cliffs of Santorini, the ancient Acropolis in Athens, the beaches and nightlife of Mykonos, and island-hopping across the Aegean. It's increasingly popular for honeymoons and family holidays. To visit, Indian passport holders need a **Schengen short-stay visa (Type C)** issued by Greece for tourism, family visits, or business.

This guide explains the 2026 process for Indian citizens — the new fee, the documents, where you apply, how long it takes, and how to maximise approval. On VisitPlane, we verify every route against official sources.

> **Key takeaway:** Indians need a **Schengen visa** for Greece. Apply through the official partner (**Global Visa Center World / VFS**) up to **6 months** ahead; the fee rose to **€90** on 11 June 2026, and a decision usually takes **about 15 days**, longer in peak summer.

## Do Indian Citizens Need a Visa for Greece?

Yes. Indian passport holders must obtain a Schengen visa before travelling; there is no visa-on-arrival. For tourism or family visits, you apply for a **short-stay Type C visa** allowing up to **90 days within any 180-day period** across the Schengen area.

## How the Schengen Visa Works

- It is a **short-stay visa** valid for the whole **29-country Schengen area**.
- You apply at the consulate of your **main destination** — if Greece is where you'll spend the most days, you apply to Greece.
- Stays are capped at **90 days in any rolling 180-day window**.
- Entries can be single or multiple, at the consulate's discretion.

> **Key takeaway:** Apply to **Greece** only if it's your main destination (most nights) or first entry point — applying to the wrong country is a common refusal reason.

## Documents You Need

For a Greek Schengen tourist visa, prepare:

- A **passport** valid at least **3 months beyond** departure, with two blank pages, issued within 10 years.
- The **completed Schengen form** and a recent **biometric photo**.
- **Travel medical insurance** covering at least **€30,000**.
- **Round-trip flight reservation** and **confirmed accommodation** (including ferries/island stays) for the full trip.
- **Proof of funds** — bank statements for 3–6 months, salary slips or ITRs.
- A **cover letter** with your itinerary and proof of **ties to India**.

## How to Apply: Step by Step

1. **Confirm Greece is your main destination** and draft an itinerary (including island ferries).
2. **Complete the application** on the official Greek visa channel.
3. **Book an appointment** at the Greek visa centre (Global Visa Center World / VFS depending on city).
4. **Attend the appointment** to submit documents and give **biometrics**.
5. **Pay the visa fee** plus the service charge.
6. **Track and collect** your passport once decided.

## Costs and Fees (2026)

- **Schengen visa fee:** **€90** for adults (raised from €80 on 11 June 2026); **€45** for children 6–11; free under 6.
- That's roughly **₹8,500**, plus a **service charge** of around **₹2,000**, so budget **₹10,000–₹12,000** all-in per adult before insurance.

Confirm the current amount on the official portal before paying — it's non-refundable.

## Processing Time

The Schengen legal standard is **15 calendar days**, extendable to 45 if extra checks apply. Greek consulates generally decide within **2–3 weeks**, with **longer waits in summer** (its busiest tourist season). Apply **at least 4–6 weeks ahead**, earlier for June–September travel.

## Visa Validity and Stay

A short-stay Schengen visa allows up to **90 days within any 180-day period**. The consulate sets the validity window and entry type; strong travel history improves the chance of a longer, multiple-entry visa. You cannot work on it. Track your days across all Schengen countries.

## Why Applications Get Refused (and How to Avoid It)

Common Greek Schengen refusals come from:

- **Insufficient or unstable funds.**
- **Weak ties to India.**
- **Inconsistent itinerary** — especially island-hopping plans that don't match bookings.
- **Insurance below €30,000.**
- **Wrong consulate** when Greece isn't the main destination.

The fee is non-refundable, so review everything before submitting.

## How Much Will the Whole Trip Cost?

Rough 2026 estimates per person for an Indian traveller:

- **Visa:** ~₹10,000–₹12,000 including service charge.
- **Return flights (India–Athens):** roughly ₹45,000–₹95,000 depending on season.
- **Accommodation:** ₹6,000–₹18,000 per night (Santorini/Mykonos are pricier in summer).
- **Daily spend:** ₹4,500–₹11,000 per person.
- **Ferries between islands:** modest add-ons.

A typical 7-day Greece trip can land around **₹1.5–3 lakh per person**, with flights and island hotels the main variables.

## Best Time to Visit and Practical Tips

Greece is best in **April–June** and **September–October** — warm seas, sunshine, and fewer crowds than peak July–August. Practical notes for Indian travellers:

- **Currency:** the Euro (€); cards work in cities, but carry cash on smaller islands.
- **Connectivity:** eSIMs and SIMs are cheap; ferries link the islands (book ahead in summer).
- **Getting around:** Athens has a metro; islands rely on ferries, scooters, and buses.
- **Food:** vegetarian options are plentiful (Greek salads, mezze); Indian restaurants exist in Athens.
- **Etiquette:** dress modestly at monasteries; respect siesta hours in smaller towns.

## What You Can and Can't Do on This Visa

A Schengen Type C visa is a **short-stay visitor visa** for tourism, family, or business, capped at **90 days per 180 days**. You **can** travel across all Schengen countries within your validity, but you **cannot** work, study long-term, or overstay. Overstaying risks fines and bans.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Non-refundable flights/ferries** booked before approval.
- **Thin or freshly funded** bank statements.
- **Insurance gaps** or under €30,000.
- **Applying to Greece** when most nights are elsewhere.
- **Island itinerary** that doesn't match your hotel and ferry bookings.

## What to See in Greece

Most trips begin in **Athens**, where the **Acropolis** and its Parthenon crown the city above the lively Plaka district, the Ancient Agora, and the superb Acropolis Museum. From the port of Piraeus, ferries fan out to the islands.

**Santorini** is the showstopper — whitewashed villages, blue-domed churches, and caldera sunsets at Oia. **Mykonos** brings windmills, beaches, and nightlife, while **Crete** offers Minoan ruins, gorges, and great food. History lovers add **Delphi** and the **Peloponnese** (Mycenae, Nafplio), and **Meteora**'s clifftop monasteries are unforgettable. For Indian travellers, vegetarian-friendly Greek cuisine, warm hospitality, and easy island connections make a week-long trip covering Athens plus one or two islands very doable.

## How to Strengthen Your Greece Application

Whether your route is straightforward or strict, the same principles separate smooth approvals from frustrating delays — and they are worth understanding before you apply. At VisitPlane, we've mapped these patterns across dozens of visa routes, and the advice below applies directly to Indian travellers heading to Greece.

Start with your **finances**, the single most scrutinised part of almost any application. Officers check that your funds are *genuine and stable*, not just present. A balance that has sat comfortably for three to six months is far more convincing than a large sum that appeared just before you applied. If a relative is sponsoring you, document that relationship clearly rather than leaving an unexplained deposit.

Next, ensure **consistency across your documents** — this matters even more for Greece because island-hopping plans must line up. Flights, ferries, hotels, cover letter, and statements should tell one story. If your bookings and itinerary don't match, that gap invites questions. Read your file as an officer would before submitting.

Because the Schengen visa assesses intent, **ties to India** matter enormously. Stable employment, a business, property, or dependents reassure the officer you'll return. It's the overall picture that counts.

Finally, be **honest and complete**. Declaring a past refusal is almost always better than hiding it. Submit every requested document, check every field, and apply early enough to absorb delays.
