## What Is the Turkey eVisa?

Turkey's eVisa (Electronic Visa) is an online travel authorisation that grants Pakistani citizens the right to enter Turkey for tourism or business purposes. It replaced the old stamp-on-arrival visa in 2013 and is now mandatory for eligible nationalities.

**Key facts:**
- Applied for 100% online at **evisa.gov.tr** (no embassy visit)
- Issued within 24–72 hours (often within hours)
- Allows **single or multiple entries**
- Valid for **180 days** from the date of issue
- Allows **maximum 30 days per stay**
- Fee: **$90** (as of 2025, following a fee increase)

## Eligibility: Can Pakistani Citizens Get a Turkey eVisa?

Yes. Pakistani citizens holding ordinary (green) Pakistani passports are eligible to apply for a Turkey eVisa. 

**Official passport holders (dark blue)** and **diplomatic passport holders** have different visa arrangements and should check with the Turkish embassy directly.

Requirements:
- Valid Pakistani ordinary passport
- Passport validity: minimum **150 days beyond your entry date** into Turkey
- Return or onward ticket
- Proof of accommodation in Turkey
- Sufficient funds for your stay
- Valid credit/debit card for the fee

## Step-by-Step: Applying for Turkey eVisa

**Step 1: Go to the official portal**
Visit **evisa.gov.tr** — the only official Turkish government eVisa website. Bookmark this URL and do not use any other site.

**Step 2: Select your nationality and travel document**
Select "Pakistan" from the nationality dropdown. Confirm you're applying for a tourist/business eVisa.

**Step 3: Fill in your personal details**
Enter your name exactly as it appears on your passport. Date of birth, passport number, and expiry date must all be accurate. Any discrepancy causes rejection at the border.

**Step 4: Enter travel details**
Enter your planned entry date and accommodation details (hotel name and address in Turkey).

**Step 5: Review and pay**
Review all entries before payment. Pay $90 with a Visa, Mastercard, or Union Pay card. The transaction should process within seconds.

**Step 6: Receive confirmation**
You'll receive an email with your eVisa PDF within 24–72 hours. Print it or save it to your phone.

**Step 7: Travel**
Show your eVisa at check-in and at Turkish border control. The eVisa is typically scanned, not physically stamped.

The eVisa application requires minimal documentation:

- Hotel booking confirmation
- Bank statements (if asked at immigration)

## Common Rejection Reasons for Turkey eVisa

Turkey eVisa rejections are relatively rare but do happen. Common reasons:

1. **Passport details entered incorrectly** — name spelling, date formats
2. **Passport validity below 150 days** on entry date
3. **Previous overstay or visa violation** in Turkey
4. **Security-related flags** — prior deportation history
5. **Payment failure** — ensure sufficient card funds before applying

If rejected, you will typically receive a brief explanation. You can reapply after correcting the issue. If repeatedly rejected, contact the Turkish consulate in Karachi or Islamabad.

## Extending Your Turkey Stay Beyond 30 Days

The Turkish eVisa allows 30 days per stay. If you want to stay longer:

- **Apply for a short-term residence permit** at the local Directorate of Migration Management (Göç İdaresi) within Turkey
- Cost: approximately TRY 3,500–7,000 (~$100–$200)
- You can apply online at **e-ikamet.goc.gov.tr**

Alternatively, do a "visa run" — exit Turkey briefly (e.g., to Greece or Georgia) and re-enter, though this is increasingly scrutinised.

### How much does the Turkey eVisa cost for Pakistani citizens in 2026?

### How long does it take to get a Turkey eVisa for Pakistanis?

Most Turkey eVisa applications for Pakistani citizens are processed within **24–72 hours**. During peak tourist season (July–August), processing can take up to 5–7 business days. You'll receive the eVisa directly to your email as a PDF.

### Can Pakistani citizens travel to Turkey without a visa?

No. Pakistani citizens require a visa to enter Turkey. The most convenient option is the eVisa, which is applied for entirely online at evisa.gov.tr. There is no visa on arrival for Pakistani passport holders — the eVisa must be obtained before travel.

### Is the Turkey eVisa a single or multiple entry visa?

The Turkey eVisa for Pakistani citizens is typically issued as a **single-entry visa**, allowing one entry and a maximum 30-day stay. Multiple-entry options are available but depend on your travel history and the system's assessment at the time of application.

### What if my Turkey eVisa application is rejected?

If your Turkey eVisa is rejected, the fee is non-refundable. You can reapply after correcting the issue that caused rejection (commonly, a data entry error or passport validity issue). If repeated applications fail, contact the Turkish Consulate in Karachi (for southern Pakistan) or Islamabad for guidance on alternative visa pathways.
