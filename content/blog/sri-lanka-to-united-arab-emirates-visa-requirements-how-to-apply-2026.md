## Who Needs a Visa

Sri Lankan citizens need a visa before travelling to the UAE — there is no visa-on-arrival for Sri Lanka passport-holders. You apply through an **airline** (Emirates or Etihad offer visa services), a **hotel or tour operator**, a UAE-based **sponsor/relative**, or the official **ICP / GDRFA** channels. A typing centre or travel agent commonly handles the paperwork.

There's no shortcut around this: confirm your eligibility route first, because applying through the wrong channel is a common cause of delay and refusal.

## Fees

Tourist visas come in **30-day** and **60-day** options (single or multiple entry), costing roughly **AED 350–650** depending on length and entry type, plus service charges. Confirm the current fee with your chosen channel.

Fees change, so treat these as a guide and confirm the current amount on the official portal before you pay. Keep every payment receipt — they form part of a tidy application file.

## Documents You'll Need

You'll typically need: passport valid 6+ months with a blank page, a clear passport-style photo, a confirmed return ticket, hotel booking or host details, and proof of funds for the trip. Organise these into a clear set so a reviewer can find anything quickly; a tidy file signals a genuine, prepared applicant.

## Step-by-Step: How to Apply

First, **confirm your eligibility route** and gather your documents. Second, **complete the application** through the correct official channel. Third, **pay the fee** and keep the receipt. Fourth, **give biometrics or attend your interview** where required, in Colombo. Fifth, **track your application** and, once approved, check every detail on the visa before you travel. Don't book non-refundable travel until your visa is confirmed.

## Proof of Funds and Finances

You must show you can **afford the trip** without working illegally. Provide bank statements covering the **past three to six months** — not just a recent balance — and document the source of any large deposit. If a sponsor or relative supports your trip, include their invitation, status, and financial documents. Funds that appear suddenly or sit unexplained in a third party's account are routinely questioned.

## Ties to Sri Lanka: The Quiet Decider

For most visit routes, the officer's central question is whether you'll **return home**. Answer it before it's asked: show stable **employment** or a **business**, **family** responsibilities, **property**, and a clear reason your life continues in Sri Lanka after the trip. A short, honest cover letter tying these together — who you are, why you're travelling, when you'll return, and what brings you back — is one of the most valuable pages in the file.

## Processing Time

UAE tourist visas are usually processed quickly — often **2–5 working days** — though it varies by channel and season. Apply a couple of weeks ahead to be safe. Whatever the route, apply **well ahead** of your travel dates and avoid committing to non-refundable bookings until you have the visa in hand.

## Validity and Length of Stay

It helps to know what the visa actually grants before you apply. Tourist visas run 30 or 60 days from entry, single or multiple entry, and short extensions are sometimes possible from inside the country — check the rules before you overstay, as fines are charged per day. Whatever the route, the **visa is permission to travel, not a guarantee of entry** — a border officer makes the final call and can ask about your purpose, funds, and plans. Keep your supporting documents accessible when you travel so you can answer briefly and confidently, and never overstay: even a short overstay can damage future applications and trigger fines or bans.

## Tourism, Visiting Family, or Business

The visa may be the same, but the **supporting story** differs with your purpose. For **tourism**, show a rough itinerary, accommodation, and funds for the trip. For **visiting family or friends**, the centrepiece is an **invitation letter** from your host stating who they are, your relationship, the purpose and length of your visit, and where you'll stay — backed by their status and, if they're helping financially, their funds. For **business**, include a letter from your Sri Lanka employer and the inviting organisation explaining the meetings, conference, or training. Matching your documents to your real purpose is what makes the application read as genuine rather than generic.

## A Realistic Cost Snapshot

Budget beyond the visa fee itself. Plan for the **application fee**, any **biometrics or service charges**, the cost of a compliant **photo**, document courier or translation where needed, and — once approved — your travel and insurance. Keep receipts for everything official; they're part of a tidy file. And remember the distinction that trips up many applicants: the money you must *show* to prove you can afford the trip is separate from, and larger than, the fees you *pay* to apply. Plan both so neither catches you short.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **A sudden large deposit** just before applying, with no source documentation.
- **Weak or vague ties** to Sri Lanka, so the officer doubts you'll return.
- **Applying through the wrong channel** or under the wrong visa type.
- **Booking non-refundable travel** before approval.
- **Inconsistencies** between your form, your funds, and your stated plans.
- **Applying too late** for the current processing or appointment wait.

## After You Apply: What to Expect

Once you've submitted and given biometrics or interviewed, the decision arrives through the official channel for your route. If you're approved, verify the **name, validity, and entry type** on the visa as soon as you receive it, and only then convert refundable bookings into paid ones. If you're refused, find the **specific reason**, fix that weakness — most often funds or ties — and reapply with a stronger file rather than resubmitting the same one. A refusal addressed properly often becomes an approval.

## A Strong Travel History Helps

One of the quietest advantages you can bring to any visa application is a **clean record of previous travel**. If you've held visas for the US, the UK, Schengen, or other major destinations and returned home on time each occasion, include copies of those visas and entry/exit stamps. They demonstrate that you've been trusted with a visa before and complied with its terms — which speaks directly to the officer's main concern about whether you'll return to Sri Lanka. First-time travellers aren't disadvantaged, but they should compensate with especially clear ties and a well-documented purpose, since there's no track record for the officer to lean on.
