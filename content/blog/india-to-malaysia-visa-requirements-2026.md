## Overview

Malaysia is one of the easiest and most affordable international destinations for Indian travellers in 2026 — and right now, it is also one of the most convenient. Under a visa-exemption arrangement, **Indian citizens can enter Malaysia visa-free for social and tourism visits of up to 30 days** until **31 December 2026**.

"Visa-free," however, does not mean "no paperwork." You still need to complete the mandatory **Malaysia Digital Arrival Card (MDAC)** online before you fly, carry proof of funds, and hold confirmed onward travel. This guide explains exactly what Indian travellers must do in 2026, what happens if the exemption is not extended, and how to avoid being turned away at immigration. On VisitPlane, we verify every route against official immigration sources.

> **Key takeaway:** Indians do **not** need a visa for short tourist visits to Malaysia until 31 December 2026 — but the **MDAC is compulsory** and must be submitted within 3 days before arrival.

## Do Indian Citizens Need a Visa for Malaysia?

For most short trips in 2026, **no**. Malaysia extended visa-free entry for Indian (and Chinese) nationals, allowing social/tourist visits of up to **30 days** without a sticker or e-visa. This arrangement, first introduced on 1 December 2023, currently runs until **31 December 2026**.

You will still need a visa or pass if you plan to:

- Stay **longer than 30 days**.
- Travel for **work, business employment, study, or medical treatment** beyond what a social visit allows.
- Enter for purposes outside the scope of a social visit pass.

For those cases, Malaysia offers the **eVisa** and **eNTRI**-style facilities through its official platforms.

> **Key takeaway:** The visa-free window is a policy that is renewed periodically. Always confirm the current status before you book — if it lapses, Indians revert to needing a Malaysia eVisa.

## The Malaysia Digital Arrival Card (MDAC)

Every foreign visitor — including visa-free Indian tourists — must submit the **MDAC** online. This is free and quick, but it is not optional.

- Submit it **within 3 days before your arrival date** in Malaysia.
- Complete it on the official Malaysian Immigration MDAC portal — avoid paid third-party "MDAC services" that simply re-enter your data for a fee.
- You will provide passport details, travel dates, flight information, and accommodation.

Carry a copy (digital or printed) of your MDAC confirmation to show at immigration if asked.

## Entry Requirements at the Border

Visa-free entry is granted by the immigration officer on arrival, not guaranteed automatically. To be admitted smoothly, Indian travellers should be ready to show:

- A **passport valid for at least 6 months** beyond your date of entry, with blank pages.
- Your **MDAC** confirmation.
- **Proof of sufficient funds** — generally at least **USD 500** (or equivalent) in cash or accessible funds for your stay.
- A **confirmed return or onward ticket** departing within 30 days of arrival.
- **Accommodation details** (hotel booking or host address).

> Immigration officers can refuse entry to anyone who cannot demonstrate the purpose of their visit, funds, and onward travel — even under a visa-free scheme. Treat the visa-free entry like a light-touch visa, not a free pass.

## Step by Step: Travelling to Malaysia Visa-Free

1. **Confirm the exemption is still active** for your travel dates (it currently runs to 31 December 2026).
2. **Check your passport validity** — at least 6 months remaining.
3. **Book return/onward flights** within the 30-day window and accommodation for your stay.
4. **Submit the MDAC online** within 3 days before arrival via the official portal.
5. **Prepare proof of funds** (around USD 500 equivalent) and your trip documents.
6. **Arrive in Malaysia**, present your passport and MDAC, and answer the officer's questions about your trip.
7. **Receive a social visit pass** stamp for up to 30 days.

## If You Need a Malaysia eVisa (Longer or Non-Tourist Stays)

If your trip falls outside the visa-free scope, you will apply for a Malaysia **eVisa** online:

- Apply through Malaysia's official immigration visa platform.
- Typical documents: passport scan, photograph, confirmed flights, accommodation, bank statement, and a covering letter.
- The eVisa generally allows a stay of up to **30 days** and is processed within a few working days.
- Government fees vary; budget additionally for any agent service charges.

## Costs

The biggest advantage of the current arrangement is cost: **visa-free entry means no visa fee** for eligible Indian tourists, and the MDAC is free. Your only mandatory spending is the trip itself — flights, accommodation, insurance, and the funds you must show on arrival. If you instead need an eVisa, expect a government fee plus any service charge from an authorised agent.

## How Long Can Indians Stay in Malaysia?

Under the visa-free arrangement, the social visit pass allows **up to 30 days per visit**. You cannot work on this pass, and overstaying carries fines and possible bans. If you need longer, apply for the appropriate visa or pass before travelling.

## What You Can and Can't Do on a Social Visit Pass

The visa-free social visit pass is for tourism and short social visits only. On it you **can**: sightsee, visit family and friends, attend short non-paid events, and travel freely around Peninsular and East Malaysia (note that **Sabah and Sarawak** have their own immigration controls and you will be re-checked when entering those states).

You **cannot**: take up employment, run a business, enrol in a long course of study, or work remotely in a way that breaches the social-visit purpose. If your trip involves any of these, you need the appropriate visa or pass. Engaging in unauthorised work on a social visit pass is a serious offence and can lead to detention, fines, deportation, and entry bans.

## Travelling with Family or Children

Each traveller — including infants and children — needs their **own passport** and their **own MDAC** submission. When travelling with minors, carry the child's birth certificate and, if only one parent is travelling, a consent letter from the other parent can help avoid questions at immigration. Families should also ensure the **proof of funds** reasonably covers everyone in the group, as the per-person guideline (around USD 500 equivalent) is assessed for the household's stay.

## Arrival and Customs Tips

- **Autogates:** eligible travellers may be able to use Malaysia's automated immigration gates after registration, but first-time visitors usually clear a manual counter.
- **Customs limits:** declare cash above the reportable threshold and be mindful of strict rules on prohibited items. Malaysia enforces **severe penalties for drug offences**, so never carry anything on someone else's behalf.
- **Keep your documents handy:** MDAC confirmation, return ticket, and hotel booking should be easy to show on arrival.
- **Track your 30 days** from the date of the entry stamp, not your flight date.

## Tips for a Smooth Entry

- **Submit the MDAC on time** — not earlier than 3 days before arrival, and not after you land.
- **Keep funds accessible** and be ready to show them.
- **Have your return ticket and hotel booking ready** at immigration.
- **Don't overstay** — track your 30-day limit from the date of entry.
- **Re-check the policy** close to your travel date in case the exemption changes.

## Overstaying, Extensions and Re-entry

The social visit pass is granted for up to 30 days, and the date stamped on entry is what counts. Malaysia takes **overstaying** seriously — penalties include fines, detention, deportation, and potential bans on future entry, so plan your departure well within the limit. The visa-free social visit pass generally **cannot be extended** for tourism; if you need longer, you must leave and, where appropriate, apply for the correct pass or visa.

Frequent back-to-back visits ("visa runs") to reset the 30-day clock can attract scrutiny from immigration officers, who may question the genuineness of your visits. If you legitimately travel to Malaysia often, keep evidence of your purpose and ties to India ready to show on arrival.

## Best Time to Visit and Practical Tips

Malaysia is a year-round destination, but the west coast (Kuala Lumpur, Penang, Langkawi) is driest between **December and April**, while the east coast is best from **March to October**. The currency is the Malaysian Ringgit (MYR); cards are widely accepted in cities, but carry some cash for smaller towns and street food. English is widely spoken, making it an easy first international trip for many Indian travellers.
