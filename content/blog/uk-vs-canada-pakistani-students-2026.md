## Overview

For Pakistani students, the UK and Canada are two of the most popular study-abroad destinations — both English-speaking, both with respected universities and post-study work. The decision usually comes down to course length, total cost, the size of the financial requirement, and which country offers the clearer long-term path.

This guide compares the UK and Canada head-to-head for Pakistani students in 2026 so you can match the choice to your goals and budget. On VisitPlane, we verify every route against official sources.

> **Key takeaway:** Choose the **UK** for a **fast, one-year master's** and a quicker foreign qualification. Choose **Canada** for **lower tuition over a longer stay and a clearer permanent-residence pathway**. Both are excellent; the right answer depends on whether you prioritise speed or settlement.

## Tuition and Course Length

The **UK** is known for **one-year master's degrees** — you qualify faster and start earning sooner, offsetting higher annual tuition. Bachelor's degrees run three years.

**Canada** programmes generally run **longer** (two-year master's, four-year bachelor's) with **lower annual tuition**, and the longer stay builds the Canadian experience that immigration rewards.

For a quick credential at predictable cost, the UK leads; for a long-term, lower-tuition project, Canada leads.

## Visa Cost and Requirements

The **UK Student visa** needs the **£558 fee** plus the **IHS at £776/year** (paid upfront), a CAS, 28-day funds, and — importantly for Pakistani applicants — a **mandatory TB test**.

The **Canada study permit** fee is modest (**CAD 150 + CAD 85 biometrics**) but demands **proof of funds** (living-cost benchmark around **CAD 20,635/year** plus tuition, often via a GIC) and a **Provincial Attestation Letter (PAL)**.

The UK front-loads cost via the IHS; Canada keeps fees low but sets a firm proof-of-funds bar.

## Work Rights While Studying

- **UK:** generally up to **20 hours per week** in term time.
- **Canada:** recently up to around **24 hours per week** off-campus during term.

Broadly comparable, with Canada currently allowing slightly more term-time hours.

## Post-Study Work and PR Pathway

- The **UK Graduate Route** allows **2 years** of work (3 for PhDs); onward settlement requires moving to a skilled-worker visa with an eligible job.
- **Canada's PGWP** (up to 3 years) feeds **Express Entry** and **PNPs** — clear, published PR routes many Pakistani graduates use to settle.

If permanent residence is the goal, Canada's pathway is more transparent; if a fast qualification and flexible 2-year work window suit you, the UK is excellent.

## Which Is Right for You?

Pick the **UK** for a **fast, prestigious qualification**, a one-year master's, and a predictable document-based visa — accepting the upfront IHS cost.

Pick **Canada** for **lower tuition, a longer runway, and a clear PR pathway** — accepting the firm proof-of-funds requirement.

## How to Decide: A Simple Framework

Ask three questions. **Time horizon:** qualify-and-go (UK) or build a multi-year life (Canada)? **Budget shape:** higher annual cost for fewer years (UK) versus lower tuition over more years plus a big proof-of-funds ask (Canada)? **Is PR the goal?** If yes, Canada's pathways are clearer; if you just want the credential, the UK keeps options open.

## Costs Beyond Tuition

Both are pricey in major cities (London; Toronto/Vancouver). Budget accommodation, the IHS (UK) or provincial health (Canada), the GIC (Canada), flights, the TB test (UK), and setup. Compare your **specific city and programme**, not country averages.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Choosing on prestige alone** rather than time horizon and PR goals.
- **Underestimating the UK IHS** across a multi-year course.
- **Underestimating Canada's proof-of-funds** requirement.
- **Missing the PAL** (Canada) or the **TB test** (UK).
- **Applying late** and missing your intake.

## Head-to-Head Scorecard

Here's how the two stack up dimension by dimension for Pakistani students:

- **Tuition (total):** Canada often lower per year, but the UK's one-year master's can mean a lower *total* — depends on your programme.
- **Course length:** UK wins for speed (one-year master's).
- **Upfront visa cost:** Canada wins (low fee); the UK's multi-year IHS is heavy.
- **Mandatory extras:** the UK requires a TB test for Pakistani applicants; Canada requires a PAL and proof of funds.
- **Work while studying:** Canada slightly ahead on hours.
- **Post-study work window:** Canada (up to 3 years PGWP) beats the UK's 2-year Graduate Route.
- **Clear PR pathway:** Canada wins decisively (Express Entry / PNPs).
- **Speed to qualification:** UK wins.

On balance, the UK is the "fast credential" choice and Canada the "settle abroad" choice.

## Verdict by Scenario

**You want a master's and to be working within ~2 years:** the UK's one-year model is ideal — qualify fast, use the Graduate Route, then decide.

**Your goal is permanent residence:** Canada's PGWP-to-Express-Entry route is the clearest published pathway, and the longer study runway helps.

**You're cost-sensitive year by year:** Canada's lower annual tuition helps, but plan for the big proof-of-funds requirement; the UK costs more per year but for fewer years.

**You're undecided about staying:** the UK keeps options open with a strong qualification and flexible 2-year work rights.

Be honest about which scenario fits you — that should drive the decision more than rankings.

## Cost Snapshot (2026)

Rough first-year figures for a Pakistani student:

- **UK:** visa £558 + IHS £776/year + TB test, tuition typically £14,000–£30,000, living costs £12,000–£16,000+ (higher in London).
- **Canada:** permit CAD 150 + biometrics CAD 85, tuition roughly CAD 15,000–40,000/year, living-cost benchmark ~CAD 20,635/year, plus a GIC.

The UK's one-year master's contains the *total* even though annual costs are high; Canada's lower annual tuition is spread over a longer programme with a firm proof-of-funds requirement. Compare your specific programme and city — the total cost, not the per-year figure, should drive the decision.

## The Bottom Line

There's no universally "better" choice between the UK and Canada for Pakistani students — there's only the better fit for *your* plan. If your aim is a respected qualification in the shortest time, with a predictable, document-based visa, the UK's one-year master's is hard to beat, and the 2-year Graduate Route gives you space to gain experience before deciding your next move. If, instead, you see study abroad as the first step toward building a long-term life overseas, Canada's combination of lower annual tuition, a longer study runway, and a transparent PGWP-to-Express-Entry pathway is the stronger foundation.

Be realistic about money, too. The UK's Immigration Health Surcharge front-loads cost, while Canada's modest fees hide a substantial proof-of-funds requirement you must genuinely meet. Whichever you choose, start early: sort your CAS (UK) or acceptance and PAL (Canada), season your finances, and complete the UK TB test in good time. Both countries reward applicants who present a coherent, well-evidenced story — and both will serve a motivated Pakistani student extremely well. Decide on *speed versus settlement*, prepare thoroughly, and commit with confidence.

## Long-Term Outlook: Careers and Staying On

For many Pakistani students, the real question isn't just where to study but where the degree leads. In the UK, the Graduate Route gives you two years to find skilled work; converting that into settlement means landing a job that meets the Skilled Worker salary and eligibility rules, then accumulating qualifying years. It rewards graduates who move quickly into a sponsoring employer. In Canada, the PGWP feeds directly into Express Entry and Provincial Nominee Programs, where Canadian study and work experience earn points toward permanent residence — a more linear, published route that many Pakistani graduates have followed to citizenship.

Neither is automatic. The UK route depends on the job market and salary thresholds at the time you graduate; the Canadian route depends on your points score and the programs open in your province. Research current rules for both before you commit, because immigration policy shifts. If your priority is a clear, points-based path to staying long-term, Canada has the edge; if you want a strong credential and the flexibility to work, then decide, the UK is excellent. Either way, treat the degree as the first move in a longer game and plan the next steps from day one.
