## Overview

Thailand is a bucket-list destination for Nigerian travellers — Bangkok's temples and street food, the beaches of Phuket and Krabi, the islands of the south, and the cultural north around Chiang Mai. To visit, Nigerian passport holders need a **tourist visa**, which Thailand now offers as an **e-Visa** through its official online portal, with the embassy route as an alternative.

This guide explains the 2026 process for Nigerian citizens — the application, the fee, documents, processing, and approval tips. On VisitPlane, we verify every route against official sources.

> **Key takeaway:** Nigerians need a **Thailand tourist visa** (there's no visa-on-arrival for Nigerian passport holders). Apply through the **official Thai e-Visa portal** (thaievisa.go.th) or the Royal Thai Embassy; processing typically takes about **5–10 working days**, for a stay of up to **60 days**.

## Do Nigerian Citizens Need a Visa for Thailand?

Yes. Nigeria is not on Thailand's visa-exemption or visa-on-arrival list, so Nigerian passport holders must obtain a **tourist visa (TR)** before travelling. The modern route is the **e-Visa**, applied for online and delivered electronically.

## How the e-Visa Works

- Applied for **online** at the official Thai e-Visa portal.
- A **single-entry tourist visa** typically allowing a **60-day stay**, often extendable locally.
- Processed in a few working days and delivered electronically.
- You print it and present it with your passport on arrival.

> **Key takeaway:** Apply only through the **official e-Visa portal** or the embassy; unofficial sites add large mark-ups.

## Documents You Need

- A **passport** valid at least **6 months** from arrival, with blank pages.
- A **passport-style photo** and a scan of the bio-data page.
- A **confirmed return/onward flight** and **hotel bookings**.
- **Proof of funds** (bank statements showing sufficient money for the trip).
- A valid **email** and a card for the fee.

## How to Apply: Step by Step

1. **Create an application** on the official Thai e-Visa portal.
2. **Upload your passport page, photo, flight, and hotel** documents and proof of funds.
3. **Pay the visa fee** online.
4. **Wait for processing** (usually 5–10 working days).
5. **Receive the e-Visa by email** and print it.
6. **Present it** with your passport on arrival in Thailand.

## Processing Time

The e-Visa typically takes **5–10 working days**, sometimes faster. Apply **at least 2–3 weeks ahead** of travel to allow for any document requests. Don't leave it to the last minute.

## Visa Validity and Stay

The tourist visa is usually **single-entry**, allowing a **60-day stay**, which can often be **extended by 30 days** at a Thai immigration office for a fee. You cannot work on it. Track your days and don't overstay, which carries daily fines.

## Why Applications Get Refused (and How to Avoid It)

- **Insufficient proof of funds.**
- **No confirmed return ticket or accommodation.**
- **Weak ties to Nigeria** or unclear travel purpose.
- **Poor-quality scans** or document errors.

## Best Time to Visit and Practical Tips

Thailand's **cool, dry season (November–February)** is the most comfortable for sightseeing and beaches. Practical notes for Nigerian travellers:

- **Currency:** the Thai Baht (THB); cards work in cities, cash for markets and islands.
- **Connectivity:** tourist SIMs are cheap; Grab is the main ride-hailing app.
- **Getting around:** Bangkok has BTS/MRT trains; domestic flights and ferries reach the islands.
- **Food:** incredible street food; halal options are available, especially in the south.
- **Etiquette:** dress modestly at temples (covered shoulders/knees); be respectful of the monarchy.

## What You Can and Can't Do on This Visa

A Thailand tourist visa is for **tourism and short visits**. You **can** sightsee, island-hop, and take tours; you **cannot** work, and you must respect the permitted stay. Overstaying carries daily fines and can affect future entry, so extend properly or leave on time.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Using unofficial visa websites** that overcharge.
- **Insufficient funds** or missing bank statements.
- **No return ticket or hotel proof.**
- **Applying too late** for processing.
- **Overstaying** the 60-day limit without an extension.

## e-Visa vs Embassy vs Visa Exemption

It helps to know exactly which route applies to you, because Thailand's rules differ sharply by nationality. Citizens of many countries enjoy **visa exemption** or **visa-on-arrival**, but **Nigeria is not on those lists**, so a Nigerian passport holder must obtain a **tourist visa in advance**. The modern, recommended route is the **official Thai e-Visa**, applied for online and delivered electronically — convenient and transparent. The traditional alternative is applying in person at the **Royal Thai Embassy**, which may suit those who prefer to submit documents directly or who fall outside e-Visa eligibility.

Whichever route you use, stick to **official channels**. A common and costly mistake is paying a lookalike website that charges a large mark-up for the same government visa, or worse, mishandles your application. The official e-Visa portal is the safest, cheapest option, and it lets you track your application status until the visa lands in your inbox.

## What to See in Thailand

Most trips begin in **Bangkok**, where the Grand Palace, Wat Pho's reclining Buddha, Wat Arun, floating markets, and legendary street food fill the days, with rooftop bars by night. To the north, **Chiang Mai** offers temples, night bazaars, elephant sanctuaries, and cooler mountain air. The south is all about beaches: **Phuket**, **Krabi** (Railay and Ao Nang), and the islands of **Koh Phi Phi**, **Koh Samui**, and **Koh Phangan** deliver turquoise water, limestone cliffs, and diving. With great-value food and accommodation and easy domestic connections, a week comfortably covers Bangkok plus an island or Chiang Mai.

## How to Strengthen Your Thailand Application

Whether your route is straightforward or strict, the same principles separate smooth approvals from frustrating delays — and they are worth understanding before you apply. At VisitPlane, we've mapped these patterns across dozens of visa routes, and the advice below applies directly to Nigerian travellers heading to Thailand.

Start with your **finances**. Officers and the e-Visa system want to see that you can fund your trip with *genuine and stable* funds — bank statements over a few months are far more convincing than a sudden deposit. Present clear evidence that matches the length and style of trip you've described.

Next, line up your **supporting documents** — a confirmed return ticket and hotel bookings that align with your stated dates. Mismatches between your itinerary and your bookings invite questions. Because tourist visas assess intent, it helps to show **ties to Nigeria** — employment, business, or family — so the picture is of a genuine visitor who will return.

Finally, apply through the **official portal**, submit clear scans, and give yourself time before travel. Be honest and complete, and declare any prior refusals truthfully.
