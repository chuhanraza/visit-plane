## Canada: The Visa at a Glance

Canada requires a Temporary Resident Visa (TRV), costing CAD 100 plus CAD 85 biometrics. The process is an online IRCC application with biometrics, no routine interview. In short, it's heavily ties-focused; refusals usually stem from weak ties or funds rather than process.

## Australia: The Visa at a Glance

Australia requires a Subclass 600 visitor visa applied for online, costing around AUD 150–190. The process is an online (ImmiAccount) application, document-led with no routine interview. In short, it's document-led but with firm scrutiny of funds and ties; genuine, well-evidenced applications do well.

## Head-to-Head: The Factors That Decide "Easier"

**Cost.** Canada costs CAD 100 plus CAD 85 biometrics; Australia costs around AUD 150–190. For most travellers the fee difference isn't the deciding factor, but it's worth knowing up front — and remember to add service charges, insurance, photos, and documents to either.

**Process and effort.** Canada uses an online IRCC application with biometrics, while Australia uses an online (ImmiAccount) application. An online, no-interview process is generally less stressful than one requiring an in-person interview — that single difference often shapes which feels "easier".

**Documents and funds.** Both want the same core reassurance: that you can afford the trip and will return home. The bar is set by evidence, not by which country it is — clean, multi-month bank statements, a clear itinerary, and proof of ties carry either application.

**Processing time.** Canada's timeline runs through no routine interview; Australia's through document-led with no routine interview. If you're on a deadline, the faster, more predictable route has real value.

**Approval odds.** This is where the routes differ most. Canada is heavily ties-focused; refusals usually stem from weak ties or funds rather than process. Australia is document-led but with firm scrutiny of funds and ties; genuine, well-evidenced applications do well. Neither "rejects everyone" or "accepts everyone" — strong applications succeed and weak ones fail on both.

## Which Is Easier to Get?

It's worth being clear about what "easier" means. It rarely means one country waves everyone through — it means the **process is simpler, faster, or less scrutinised**, so a well-prepared applicant meets fewer hurdles. For Bangladeshi travellers, that usually favours routes with an online, no-interview process and a track record of predictable decisions.

## It Depends on Your Situation

The honest answer is that the "easier" visa depends on *you*. If you have **limited travel history**, lean toward the route with the lighter scrutiny and simpler process. If you have **strong ties and a clean travel record**, either is very achievable, so choose by destination. If you're on a **tight timeline**, the faster route wins. And if **cost** is tight, factor in the full all-in price, not just the headline fee. Map your own circumstances onto these factors and the right choice usually becomes obvious.

## How to Strengthen Either Application

Whichever you pick, the same preparation wins:

- **Build genuine, well-aged funds** — several months of stable history, with any large deposit explained.
- **Evidence your ties** — employment, business, family, or property that show you'll return to Bangladesh.
- **Make your purpose concrete** — a realistic itinerary, bookings, or invitation.
- **Keep everything consistent** — names, dates, and figures matching across your file.
- **Apply early** — comfortably ahead of your travel date, and don't book non-refundable travel until approved.

## Common Myths About "Easy" and "Hard" Visas

Plenty of misconceptions distort this decision, so it's worth clearing a few up. **Myth one: "some countries reject everyone from my passport."** Not true — every route approves well-prepared, genuine applicants and refuses weak ones; your *profile* matters far more than blanket reputation. **Myth two: "a higher fee means a harder visa."** The cost and the difficulty are unrelated — some of the cheapest visas have the strictest scrutiny, and vice versa. **Myth three: "an agent makes it easier."** An agent can organise paperwork, but can't manufacture genuine funds or ties, which are what decisions actually turn on. **Myth four: "once refused, that country is closed to me."** A refusal is a fixable setback, not a ban — diagnose the reason, strengthen the file, and reapply. Seeing past these myths helps Bangladeshi travellers choose on facts rather than rumour.

## If You're Refused on Either Route

A refusal on one route doesn't mean the other will say no, nor that you're out of options. Find the **specific reason** — usually funds, ties, or an inconsistency — and fix exactly that before reapplying, to the same country or the other. Disclose any previous refusal honestly on future applications; non-disclosure is treated as deception and does lasting harm, while an honestly disclosed refusal is routine. Many approved travellers were refused at least once; the difference is that they diagnosed the problem and came back with a stronger file rather than guessing.
