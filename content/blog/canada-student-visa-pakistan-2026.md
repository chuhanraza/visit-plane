## Overview

Canada is a leading study-abroad choice for Pakistani students — globally respected universities and colleges, work rights during and after study, and clear pathways toward permanent residence. To study a programme longer than six months, Pakistani nationals need a **Canada study permit** (commonly called the student visa).

This guide explains the 2026 process for Pakistani applicants — the acceptance letter and Provincial Attestation Letter, the financial requirement, fees, processing, and work rights. On VisitPlane, we verify every route against official sources.

> **Key takeaway:** Pakistani students need a **study permit**. You need a **Letter of Acceptance** from a Designated Learning Institution, usually a **Provincial Attestation Letter (PAL)**, proof of **funds for tuition + living costs**, and to pay the **CAD 150 permit fee** plus **CAD 85 biometrics**.

## Do Pakistani Students Need a Study Permit?

Yes. To study a programme longer than six months at a Canadian institution, Pakistani nationals must obtain a study permit before travelling. Short courses (under six months) may not require one, but degree and diploma programmes do.

## Eligibility: What You Need First

- A **Letter of Acceptance** from a **Designated Learning Institution (DLI)**.
- A **Provincial Attestation Letter (PAL)** in most cases, under the rules introduced for international students.
- Proof you can **pay tuition and support yourself** (and any dependants).
- Intent to **leave Canada** at the end of your authorised stay.
- A clean background and, if required, a **medical exam**.

## The Financial Requirement

You must show enough money to cover:

- Your **first year's tuition**, and
- **Living costs** — the benchmark for a single student (outside Quebec) rose to about **CAD 20,635 per year** in addition to tuition and travel.

Acceptable proof includes bank statements, a Guaranteed Investment Certificate (GIC), education loan letters, or sponsor documents.

## Documents You Need

- A valid **passport**.
- **Letter of Acceptance** from your DLI.
- **Provincial Attestation Letter (PAL)**, where required.
- **Proof of funds** meeting the requirement.
- **Academic records** and test scores (e.g., IELTS).
- **Statement of purpose** explaining your study plan.
- **Medical exam** results if requested, and **biometrics**.

## How to Apply: Step by Step

1. **Get accepted** at a DLI and obtain your **Letter of Acceptance** and **PAL**.
2. **Arrange your funds** and gather financial proof.
3. **Create an account** and apply online through the IRCC portal.
4. **Pay the study permit fee** (CAD 150) and **biometrics fee** (CAD 85).
5. **Give biometrics** at the visa centre in Pakistan.
6. **Complete a medical exam** if requested.
7. **Wait for the decision**, then receive your permit details / port-of-entry letter.

## Costs and Fees (2026)

- **Study permit application fee:** **CAD 150**.
- **Biometrics fee:** **CAD 85** (total CAD 235 with the permit fee).
- **Medical exam, language test, and GIC** (if used): additional.

These are modest compared with tuition and living costs, but the **proof-of-funds requirement** is the real financial hurdle.

## Processing Time

Study permit processing varies by season and stream. Applications can take **several weeks to a few months**, so apply **as early as possible** after receiving your acceptance and PAL. Check current IRCC processing estimates for Pakistan before you plan flights.

## Work Rights and After Graduation

- **During study:** eligible students can work **off-campus** (recent rules allow up to **24 hours per week** during term) and full-time during scheduled breaks.
- **After graduation:** the **Post-Graduation Work Permit (PGWP)** allows eligible graduates to work in Canada, a key pathway many Pakistani students use toward permanent residence.

## Why Applications Get Refused (and How to Avoid It)

- **Insufficient or unclear proof of funds.**
- **Weak statement of purpose** or doubts about study intent.
- **Missing PAL** where it's required.
- **Ties / intent concerns** — failing to show you'll respect the permit's terms.

The fees are modest, but a refusal costs time — get the funds and PAL right.

## How Much Will Studying Cost Overall?

Rough 2026 estimates for a Pakistani student:

- **Permit + biometrics:** ~CAD 235.
- **Tuition:** roughly **CAD 15,000–CAD 40,000** per year depending on the programme.
- **Living costs:** about **CAD 20,635+** per year (the proof-of-funds benchmark).
- **Flights, GIC, and setup:** budget separately.

Many students use a GIC and education loans — document the source clearly.

## How to Strengthen Your Canada Study Permit Application

Whether your route is straightforward or strict, the same principles separate smooth approvals from frustrating delays. At VisitPlane, we've mapped these patterns across dozens of visa routes, and the advice below applies directly to Pakistani students heading to Canada.

The decisive factor is your **financial evidence**. Show that you can cover the **first year's tuition plus the living-cost benchmark**, with funds that are *genuine and stable*. A GIC is a clean, widely accepted way to demonstrate living costs; if a sponsor is funding you, document the relationship and their capacity clearly rather than leaving an unexplained deposit.

Next, write a strong **statement of purpose**. Explain why this programme and institution, how it fits your goals, and your plans after study. Officers use this to assess whether you're a genuine student — vague or generic statements invite refusal.

Make sure your **PAL and acceptance letter** are valid and match your application, and complete **biometrics** promptly. Be honest and complete throughout; declaring any prior refusals truthfully is always better than omitting them.

## Key Dates and Deadlines

Because Canadian processing can run from several weeks to a few months, timing is critical. Work backwards from your **intake date** (Canada has Fall, Winter, and Summer intakes):

- **8–12 months before:** apply to Designated Learning Institutions and accept an offer.
- **On acceptance:** request your **Provincial Attestation Letter (PAL)** where required.
- **As early as possible:** arrange your **GIC and proof of funds**, then submit the study permit application — earlier intakes and peak seasons see longer queues.
- **Promptly after submitting:** complete **biometrics** and any requested **medical exam**.

Apply as soon as you have your acceptance and PAL rather than waiting, since processing estimates for Pakistan shift with demand. Building in a buffer protects you against a permit arriving after your intended start date — and many institutions allow deferral to the next intake if the timeline slips, so confirm that option with your DLI as a backup.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Underestimating the proof-of-funds** benchmark.
- **Missing the PAL** where required.
- **A weak or generic statement of purpose.**
- **Delaying biometrics** or the medical exam.
- **Booking flights** before approval.

## Funding and Scholarships

Canadian study is funded through family support, education loans, and a growing range of scholarships. Explore **university entrance scholarships** (many automatically consider international applicants), provincial and institutional awards, and graduate funding such as assistantships at the master's and PhD level. The **GIC** you buy for proof of funds doubles as your living-expense account once you arrive, releasing money in instalments — a practical, IRCC-recognised way to both prove and manage funds.

## Bringing Dependants

Your spouse or common-law partner and dependent children can apply to accompany you. Spousal **open work permit** eligibility has narrowed under recent rules and now generally applies to spouses of students in specific graduate and professional programmes — confirm your eligibility before planning, and budget additional proof of funds for any dependants.

## After You Arrive

On arrival you'll receive your **study permit** at the port of entry. Enrol and remain a full-time student at your DLI, stay within the **off-campus work limit** (recently up to 24 hours/week in term), and keep your permit valid. Plan ahead for the **Post-Graduation Work Permit (PGWP)**, which is the gateway many Pakistani graduates use toward Canadian work experience and, eventually, permanent residence pathways like Express Entry.
