## Dummy Ticket — The Visa Applicant's Grey Area

Almost every first-time visa applicant asks the same question: the embassy wants proof of a flight booking, but what if my visa gets rejected? Why would I buy a full ticket before I even know if I'm approved?

This is where "dummy tickets" entered the conversation. But there is significant confusion — and real risk — around what a dummy ticket is, whether it is legal, and how embassies treat it.

This guide gives you an honest, complete answer.

---

## What Is a Dummy Ticket?

A dummy ticket is a flight reservation or itinerary document that looks like a confirmed booking but is not actually paid for or confirmed in the airline's system.

There are two common types:

1. **Fabricated/fake documents:** PDF documents made to look like airline booking confirmations but with reference numbers that either don't exist or cannot be verified. These are sold by some online vendors.

2. **Temporary hold bookings:** Actual reservations made through an airline or booking platform that places a hold on a seat for 24–72 hours without payment. These are technically real reservations for a short window.

The term "dummy ticket" is often used for both — but they are very different in terms of legality and risk.

---

## What Embassies Actually Require

Most Schengen embassies and UKVI ask for evidence of **travel plans**, not a paid ticket. Their official language typically says:

- "Proof of intended travel" or
- "Flight reservation or itinerary"

They are asking you to demonstrate that your travel plans are genuine and thought-through. They are **not** requiring a fully paid, non-refundable ticket. An active reservation — one that holds a real seat in an airline's system for a window of time — satisfies this requirement.

The German Embassy, French Embassy, and most Schengen missions explicitly state that confirmed bookings are not required — a reservation is sufficient.

---

## Are Dummy Tickets Legal?

This is where the answer gets nuanced.

**A real reservation (flight hold) is legal.** If you book a refundable ticket, use a flight-hold service that creates a genuine PNR (Passenger Name Record) in an airline's reservation system, or use a refundable booking that you cancel after the visa is decided — all of these are legitimate.

**A fabricated document is not legal.** Submitting a PDF with invented booking references, fake airline logos, or altered documents constitutes document fraud. This is a serious matter. If caught:

- Your application is automatically rejected
- You are typically banned from reapplying for a set period
- Depending on the country, there may be legal consequences

**Some online "dummy ticket" services sell fabricated documents.** These are the ones to avoid. A vendor offering a "dummy ticket" for PKR 500 with no real booking behind it is selling document fraud.

---

## Schengen Embassy Position on Flight Bookings

The Schengen Visa Code does not require an actual paid ticket at the application stage. Most embassies confirm this. The VFS Global checklist for Germany, France, and the Netherlands all list "flight reservation" rather than "confirmed/paid ticket."

The practical reason: embassies understand that applicants cannot risk buying non-refundable tickets before knowing if the visa is approved. The system is designed to accept reservations.

## Risks of Using a Fake Ticket Service

If you use a service that provides a fake booking reference or fabricated document, you face:

| Risk | Consequence |
|---|---|
| Visa rejection | Instant; noted on your record |
| Application ban | Typically 1–5 years depending on country |
| VIS entry | Fraud flag in Schengen Visa Information System |
| UK/USA impact | Future visa applications to these countries may be affected |
| Criminal liability | Unlikely for first offence but technically applies |

The risk is simply not worth it when legal alternatives exist.

## Legal Alternatives to a Fake Dummy Ticket

### 1. Refundable Flight Booking
Book a fully refundable ticket through the airline directly or through platforms like Expedia or Kayak that allow cancellation. Submit the booking confirmation. Cancel if the visa is rejected. The cancellation fee may be zero or modest.

### 2. Flight Hold Services
Services like Confirm My Flight, Visa Reservation, and several others offer genuine 24–72 hour PNR holds — real reservations in airline systems with actual booking references that can be verified. These typically cost $10–$20. This is the most popular and fully legal approach.

### 3. Book Flexible Fares
Many airlines offer fare classes with no change or cancellation fees. Book one of these, use it for your visa application, and either travel on it if approved or cancel if rejected.

### 4. Letter from Travel Agent
Some embassies accept a letter from a licensed travel agent outlining your proposed itinerary. Check the specific embassy's checklist before relying on this.

---

## How to Verify a Flight Hold Service Is Legitimate

Before purchasing from any flight hold service, verify:

1. The booking confirmation includes a **real PNR code**
2. The PNR can be looked up on the airline's official website
3. The service has verifiable reviews and a traceable business
4. The document shows the correct airline, route, and passenger name

If the vendor cannot tell you how to verify the PNR, do not use them.

---

## What to Do After Your Visa Is Approved

Once your visa arrives, you can:

- Book actual flights using the real-time prices you find then
- Cancel your temporary hold reservation (most expire automatically)
- Use the refundable ticket you booked and change the dates if needed

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Will the embassy verify my flight reservation?
Most embassies spot-check reservations. The German and Dutch embassies in particular are known to verify PNR codes with airlines. Always use a real reservation that can be verified.

### Is a "dummy ticket" from a service like Visa Reservation legal?
Services that provide a genuine PNR-backed hold on a real airline reservation are legal. Services that provide fabricated PDF documents with invented references are not. Always confirm that a real PNR exists and is verifiable.

### Can I submit a screenshot from Google Flights as proof?
No. A search result or price display is not a reservation. You need a booking confirmation with a reference number, passenger name, and fare details.

### What if my visa takes longer than 72 hours and my hold expires?
Simply purchase another hold. Most flight hold services allow you to renew or rebook cheaply. Do not submit a reservation you know has expired.

### Does the UK require a flight booking for the Standard Visitor Visa?
The UK recommends providing a travel itinerary but does not strictly require a confirmed booking at application stage. A planned itinerary letter alongside a flexible booking reference is sufficient for most UK applications.
